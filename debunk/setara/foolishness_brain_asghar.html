Re: Foolishness and intelligence depend on the method of application of brain

In an article, titled above, and published in Shodalap, Setara Heshem wrote: “Sex is biological need, related to psychology, which is science. But pornography is related to perverted sex activities, is being used to make money. These activities are banned in some societies but open in American society and have become an industry. The sex business is part and parcel of capitalist society. Concept of sex related activities vary with formation of society, which depends on time and place. It is also the case with religion.

I read a lot of Setara Hashem’s articles, but never felt the necessity or inclination to rebut or support any of his statements. I, however, could not desist myself from writing this short response, when I read his above unqualified remark.

Setara Hashem is right when he says that ‘pornography’ has become a business, but what proof does he have to support his claim that ‘pornography’ is open in the American society?

Yes, existence of pornography is more visible in America than in any other countries of the world. It is visible in America, because it is an open society. On the other hand, it is not so visible in other countries, perhaps, because most of those countries are not as open on sexual matters as America is.

Pornography is also prevalent in most of the Asian countries as well. To verify my statement, what Setara Hashem needs to do is to go to Korea, Japan, Taiwan and Hong Kong and stay in their hotels. Most hotels in these countries have in-house pornography movies - - their stars being their own nationals - - and these are shown to the guests, against payment, through videos.

In Europe, the situation is not any different. But pornography here is practiced a little more discreetly than it is done in the United States of America.

Pornography, in one form or another, is also prevalent in countries like Bangladesh, India and Pakistan. People in these countries practice it secretly, hence Mr. Hashem’s inability to discern its existence in his midst.

It should suffice for Mr. Hashem’s information that once Dhaka Club was known to many people for being a place where its members swapped their wives among themselves!

Mr. Hashem needs to clarify whether wife swapping is any better than pornography, which, in many cases, people perform for money?

Mr. Hashem should know that throwing dirt at others does not help its thrower in keeping his or her cloth clean. Before casting aspersions on others, intelligent people look into themselves, for spitting against the wind brings it back to the spitter’s face!

Following information may prove beneficial for Mr. Hashem.

Until the late nineteen eighties, prostitution was rampant in Korea, Taiwan and Thailand. Comparing to these countries, Bangladesh enjoyed a better economic atmosphere.

But look at the irony of fate: Countries like Korea and Taiwan in which many people once engaged in prostitution for earning their bread are now giving financial help to Bangladesh; Thailand on the streets of which pimps once surrounded customers is now helping Bangladesh in building one of its flyovers in Dhaka.

My question to Mr. Hashem is: Why a country, like Bangladesh, which prides itself in maintaining a clean and righteous society, should take help from those countries, the people of which, once earned money through the sinful acts of pornography and prostitution?

Regards,

Mohammad Asghar

June 10, 2004
