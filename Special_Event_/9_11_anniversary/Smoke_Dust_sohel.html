The Smoke, Dust and Tears Filled September 11

 

 

 

By Mahbubul Karim (Sohel)
September 11, 2002

 

This is undeniably the time for grief and mourning. The widow's reminiscing the last telephone calls from her husband trapped in one of the suicide plane crashed Twin Towers, the humbling violin sound, a tiny salute from a fatherless child amidst the windy New York city, are all the subtle reminders of the vastness of the calamity from last year's unforgettable terrorist attacks. America is in pain. America is in anguish. The people of America cannot forget the heartbreaking tragedy. America cries with resolved determination.

Yes, there are differences of opinions, views and philosophy among the world citizenry. And yes, there are the feelings of alienation in the international community arose from the recent world political developments. And still there are unified sadness amongst the people with varied religion and cultural customs. There are the synonymous feelings of antipathy against the terrorists of all kinds who have purported or assisted in massacring innocent people in the United States last year. Some of these terrorists are still hiding in the undiscovered caves, and some of them are residing in robust places around the world, snugly surrounded by their powerful peers and charlatans.

Terrorists have succeeded in their true agenda. They have succeeded in imprinting their mark of poisons among the livable consciousness of daily America. Not long after the Twin towers were collapsed, part of Pentagon was fractured and another plane crashed on the field of Pennsylvania; like the Muslim extremists, there were vengeful Christians, Jews and others with remarkably similar "anti-infidel" feelings came to the forefront, in the dark of the night, or in the broad day light, to show the naked power and hatred filled onslaught. A Sikh man was murdered just because he was wearing his turban, a Pakistani man was shot dead because of his religion and look, and there were countless other reported and unreported abuses, many of them are absorbed silently by the scared new immigrant population for the fear of further unjustified persecution.

Many of the brave Americans came forward as well. They have extended their kind hands to some of these terrified immigrants, who were predominantly Muslims. These kind hearted Americans have escorted the scarf wearing Muslim girls or bearded men to their work or schools, they have arranged community meetings where they have tried to disseminate brotherhood and sisterhood feelings in the community. And it is for these non-advertised Americans' relentless efforts and dedication for genuine goodness, many more abuses, tortures and discriminations of the immigrant population have been thwarted, to some degree, but not entirely. The hum of intolerance still lurks underneath the surface, especially when the question of further expanding war around the world are resurfacing like an uncontrolled mushroom cloud.

Visiting mosques and temples by the prominent leadership were the first good step, far away from crusade chattering, in delineating anti-Muslim feelings from America. Many wish that this could be done more often as the leadership goes to weekly Sunday church, one or two cabinet members could visit other religionists' prayers' places more often along with their scheduled news appearances. This could work in positive way in taking away misunderstandings among the Muslim Americans. This could assure many that the war against terrorism is really not against Islam or the vast majority of peace loving Muslims or turban wearing Sheikhs. 

America is built by the immigrants. Among the 3000 or more dead from last year's terrorist attacks, there were many first generation immigrants from around the world. There were Christians, Jews, Muslims, Hindus, Buddhists, Atheists, Agnostics and people with other belief systems of various national backgrounds. The terrorist attack was meant to destabilize America, to rouse one immigrant segments against another, and overall, its clever plan was to polarize our world further along the racial and religious line. Many writers observe that killing numerous innocent civilians in the faraway foreign lands in the name of "war against terrorism" by the vengeful parties were in terrorists' long-standing plan as well. And unfortunately, many detect that the West is dealing the card the way the terrorists wished them to deal the cards in complete synchronicity.

It is not the defiant neuralgic bombs and missiles or pristine military aircrafts filled with "carpet bombing" ferocity will ever defeat the terrorists, it's the international cooperation that is faraway from unilateral arrogance, and the concerted efforts dominated by social justice and equity away from the naked profitability schemes, can truly achieve the desired goals in stifling terrorism out of their unconquerable breeding places.

Let's the painful memory of September 11 and its aftermath remind us once again our unshakable commitment toward human rights, freedom and democracy for all, without holding any discriminatory reservations. This world is for every citizens, from the poor in Mandalay, to the repressed Bedouins of Arabian peninsula, to the railway coolies of Dhaka Kamalapur Station, the Dalits and Brahmans of Maharashtra, Chinese monks, to the blue eyed Irish, and the broad shouldered Americans and the cowboys Australians, the dancing Tongas and Congolese natives, to the Cubans and soccer crazed Brazilians, this is our world. And the only one world we have in our unimaginably short unpredictable life.

My deepest condolences are toward the September 11 victims' families and their friends, and utter shame and disgust to the terrorists.
