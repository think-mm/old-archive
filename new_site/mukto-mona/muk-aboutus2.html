About Us 

Mukto-mona (www.mukto-mona.com) is a loose Internet congregation of secularists, atheists, agnostics, freethinkers, rationalists, skeptics, and humanists of mainly Bengali and South Asian descent converging from all around the world.  Mukto-mona covers wide-ranging issues including religion, religious fundamentalism, politics, philosophy, history, human rights and so on in an effort to promote rationalism, secularism, human rights, religious tolerance, and harmony since its founding on May 26, 2001.  Our mission has always been to promote rationalism, science, and secularism to build an open and free society. To that end, Mukto-mona regularly posts scholarly articles, essays, papers, reviews, and debates on relevant topic in its homepage and forum.  We intend to translate and write more informative articles in Bangla alongside with English to promote rationalism, humanism, and critical thinking among the people of Bangladesh in particular, and other Bangla and Indian regional languages speaking people.  Occidental freethinkers are also encouraged to join our forum.  

Mukto-mona’s unquivering principle is to take a strong stand against all kinds of social injustice all over the world.  We feel obligated to write against their evil design.  Thus, Mukto-mona critiques religion to everything that squishes civil liberty, freedom of press, secularism, social injustice, evils of globalization, neo-colonialism imposed on weaker democracies by the powerful nations, political and religious repression, and a whole list of issues that stymie personal freedom and human development all across the globe.  We take a strong stand against minority repression all over the world including Bangladesh, religious oppressions, and gender-based repression against women and oppression against any other groups of people.  We strongly opposed Iraq war, and oppression against Palestinians, Chechens by Israelis and Russians, respectively. 

Mukto-mona has already been fortunate to have showcased the contributions and blessings of scientists, humanists, rationalists, thinkers, philosophers and literati of international stature, namely late Prof. Humayun Azad (writer, social thinker and educator; Dhaka), Prof. Sirajul Islam Chowdhury (writer, social thinker and educator; Dhaka), Prof. Ajoy Roy (scientist, rationalist, educator, and writer; Dhaka), Dr. Jaffor Ullah (scientist, writer, and columnist; USA), Prabir Gosh (rationalist, writer, and investigator of unusual claims; Calcutta), Dr. Ghulam Murshid (professor, author, researcher, and journalist of BBC; Calcutta), Dr. Ishtiaq Ahmed (writer, social thinker, and educator; Sweden),  James Randi (professional magician, author, lecturer, and renowned investigator of paranormal phenomenon; USA), Prof. Paul Kurtz (secular humanist, philosopher, and writer; USA), Prof. Pervez Hoodbhoy (scientist, educator, columnist; Pakistan), Dr. Mark Perakh, Dr.Victor Stenger (scientist, philosopher, and author; USA),  and many more budding writers and sociopolitical activists round the globe. 

Mukto-Mona has no political leaning whatsoever.  It is a non-profit organization and as a whole, we are not affiliated with any particular political ideology and do not support any particular political establishment anywhere in the world.  However, we strongly endorse Universal Declaration of Human Rights and want to work for implementations of it in Bangladesh and other South Asian countries.  

Mukto-mona strives for implementing social justice across the board.  No human or group of people in our opinion should be the subject of discrimination based on their political philosophy, ethnicity, and religious belief.  As a group, we hold the view that each and every human being is capable of enriching our earth through their deeds.  Thus, we value individuality over any other human qualities.  Establishing a just world where people of many faiths, ethnicities, and diverse opinion could live harmoniously to enrich world’s societies, culture, and polity.  Please help us achieve this lofty goal.
