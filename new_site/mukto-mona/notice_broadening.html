Broadening the horizon at Mukto-mona in 2005 

A rather belated New Year’s greetings to all readers. For many of us, whether New Year should be celebrated with a full vigor or not was a question that was eventually posing a moral dilemma. The salient reason is not difficult to guess given the numbers of problems people have to cope up with lately: terrorism, war, inflation, and Tsunami, the most catastrophic of all, unfortunately, at the end of the year.

Yet, life moves on, life departs, and life arrives. That's where we are obliged not to be just self-centered, but to enjoy the life while making sure - at least "one life has breathed easier because you have lived," to put it in the words of Emerson.

It is a great pleasure to inform our dearest members that we have launched our new and revamped website on the eve of New Year's Day.

From now on, logging into www.mukto-mona.com  would take you to our new web pages. We thank all those who have responded to our call by submitting articles and others for their generous encouragements.

The year 2005 will bring an unprecedented change in our forum, Mukto-mona. Some of these changes will be discernible to our members and some won’t. Many of our members may have already seen a sea change in the direction in Mukto-mona. This was inevitable and to be frank, its making was in progress in the latter part of 2004.

Mukto-mona’s unquivering principle of taking a strong stand against all kinds of social injustices allover the world had prompted us to have a debate on the position of the U.S. presidency. Neutral readers have noticed our strong stand while we brought a plethora of ignominies to Bush for his role in declaring a unilateral and immoral war against Iraq.

The contest for the White House is long gone but we still face the menace of neo-colonialism imposed on weaker democracies by the powerful western nations all in the name of globalization. On top of it, we see a streak of authoritarianism running amuck amongst the policymakers of many nations. We thus feel obligated to write against their evil design. Thus, Mukto-mona will broaden its horizon from critiquing religion to everything that squishes civil liberty, freedom of press, secularism, social injustice, evils of globalization, imperialism, political and religious repression, and a whole list of issues that stymie personal freedom and human development all across the globe.

Therefore, one could see that we are broadening our horizon here at Mukto-mona. We also would like to make this forum a more complete waterhole, so to speak. Our forum, thus far, was focused on religio-political and societal issues. In the process, though, we sometime neglected importance of culture and fine arts. Indeed, no human endeavor could be complete without art and culture. We will encourage our erudite members to take time out and write short articles on world-class writers, painters, cameraperson, vocalists, instrumentalists, etc. We intend to have separate web pages devoted to fine arts to augment our collection of essays, informative articles on society, politics, religion, etc., those that affect our everyday life.

We hope that all these incremental changes in Mukto-mona will make our forum a vibrant meeting ground where we could both learn and disseminate knowledge to make our world a repression free and fulfilling place. With this ardent hope, let us wish our readers a happy and prosperous New Year once again.

Avijit Roy & Jahed Ahmed
