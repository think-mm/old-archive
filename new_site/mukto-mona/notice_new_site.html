New Year greetings with a New look of Our Site 

In some parts of the world, people have finished celebrating the New Year, while in other parts, people are looking forward to its celebrations. For some people, whether New Year should be celebrated is a question that is posing a moral dilemma. Reason is not difficult to guess given the numbers of problems people have to cope up with lately: terrorism, war, inflation, and Tsunami, the most catastrophic of all , unfortunately, at the end of the year. Yet life moves on. Life departs,  Life arrives. That's where we are obliged not to be just self-centered, but to enjoy the life while making sure- at least "one life has breathed easier because you have lived", to put it in the words of Emerson.  

It's a great pleasure to inform our dearest members that we have just launched our new website on the eve of New Year's day. From now on, logging into www.mukto-mona.com would take you to our new web pages. We thank all those who have responded to our call by submitting articles and others for their generous encouragements. Please take some time to review our new site and send your feedbacks as to what needs to be done further to improve it.

Visit: www.mukto-mona.com

The year 2005 will bring an unprecedented change in our forum amd Web-site. Some of these changes will be discernible to our members and some won’t. Many of our members may have seen a sea change in the direction in Mukto-Mona. This was inevitable and to be frank, its making was in progress in the latter part of 2004. Mukto-mona will broaden its horizon from critiquing religion to everything that squishes civil liberty, freedom of press, secularism, social injustice, evils of globalization, political and religious repression, and a whole list of issues that stymie personal freedom and human development all across the globe.

We will encourage our erudite members to take time out to go through our pages and send us feed back. Members are also encouraged to write short articles in future on world-class writers, painters, cameraperson, vocalists, instrumentalists, etc. We intend to have separate web pages devoted to fine arts to augment our collection of essays, informative articles on society, politics, religion, etc., those that affect our everyday life. And if you like our effort, refer it to another like-minded friend. We'll thus feel, it has been worth our effort. 

Last but not the least, we wish all our members a very happy, safe and satisfying 2005! Let us hope- New Year's celebrations would not weaken but strengthen our commitments to the causes of love and reason only further.

Sincerely yours
