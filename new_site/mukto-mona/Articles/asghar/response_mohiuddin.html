Re: Allah is a ferocious Being-An Appeal to Humanity
A response for Mr. mohiuddin

Mohammad Asghar



After reading my article, titled above, Mr. mohiuddin attempted to re-emphasis the Power that Allah has, and by using which He can destroy the whole world at any moment of His choice. Mr. mohiuddin did not have to do it, for this is what I have already inferred to in my article.


 Humanist1969 has already asked Mr. mohiuddin some questions on his theory related to  Allah and His might. I hope he will reply to them candidly, and as soon as possible.

 I do not know how old Mr. mohiuddin is, but I learned from his responses on Vinnomot and Mukto-mona that he has a deep faith in Allah. His deep faith has made him oblivious of some real facts of life.

 Let us assume that Mr. mohiuddin has two sons, whom he helped to take birth, but did not “create” them. One of them is very obedient and gives no chance to their father to complain about anything.

 The other one is arrogant, disrespectful of him (i.e. Mr. mohiuddin), does not carry out his orders, nor does he follow what he wants him to follow.

 

Under the circumstances, Mr. mohiuddin decides to punish him. Now my questions to him are:

 1. Are you going to drown him in a sea, or burn him alive in a ball of fire?


 2. Are you going to treat him at par with your good son, and subject him to the same punishment you want to inflict on the bad one?


 3. To see that both of them are adequately punished, are you going to kill your wife and mother and others along with destroying your own homestead?

 I am sure Mr. mohiuddin's response to all the three questions are going to be in negative, for he cannot or will not do all the horrible things I have mentioned above, as he is a human being and he loves his mother, wife and both the sons, despite one of them being bad.

 If what I have assumed above is true, then should not Mr. mohiuddin ask himself that when he cannot be as brutal as he knows his Allah is, then should not his wife and children and others worship him, instead of Allah? If Allah finds pleasure in destroying both the sinners and the innocents at the same time, what it would take me to convince that Mr. mohiuddin's Allah is worst than Hitler and all the other beast-like humans, who killed, maimed and destroyed their fellow beings in order to satisfy their ego and moribund eccentricities?

 Regards,
 Mohammad Asghar
