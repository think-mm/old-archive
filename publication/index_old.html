Publication: A Bold New Step for Mukto-mona!

Most organizations consider the way they present themselves to the outside world to be a matter of the utmost importance. Mukto-mona  has already emerged as the leading  web-based forum for promoting rationalism, humanism and free-thought. We wish to extend its reach to publication that represents our community and reflects our concerns. By publishing progressive books in Bangla, Mukto-mona will help promote secular humanist outlook and scientific approaches more effectively.  

The time has come to spread our efforts to establish a social system which allows freethinking,  in countries like Bangladesh and others, to cope with the modern world. Today, the progressive world community is marching towards the candid and explicit humanism, rationalism, secularism and free thought. We hope that our move to publication will make Mukto-mona more effective, more useful and appealing to freethinkers and secular humanists, and better able to attract new readers, specially the readers who do not have the privilege to access internet.

Guidelines for Authors

'Mukto-mona Publishers' welcomes contributions on all subjects relating to the progressive, scientific and rational thoughts. All articles become the property of Mukto-mona, a non-profit humanist organization. Authors receive no remuneration for their submissions unless specified. The readership includes scholars, researchers, and lay readers with diverse backgrounds.

Mukto-mona considers only original articles that have not been published elsewhere in a printed form. Authors should submit their articles in mukto-mona forum at mukto-mona@yahoogroups.com for primary selection and consideration. Authors should not submit an article to any other printed media, while Mukto-mona  is considering it. Multiple submissions (articles submitted to several publications simultaneously) will not be considered for publication.

Authors who wish to publish their essay through Mukto-mona Publishers  are expected to prepare their manuscripts according to  "Mukto-mona Submission Guidelines" (this document). 

Articles submitted to Mukto-mona are primarily selected by the Board made up of five members of the association chosen for their expertise in a variety of related fields. Selected article is  forwarded to the Editorial Board for evaluation, a process that may last up to four months. Upon completion of the peer review, the Editor makes a final decision concerning publication.

Editorial Requirements

Author should write clearly and simply; assume an intelligent and progressive general audience. All submissions are judged on the basis of interest, clarity, significance, relevance, authority, and topicality. Categories of submission include Articles, Reviews, Story, Opinion/Editorial, and Departments including but not limited to: Humanism, Rationalism, Secularism, Free thought, Religious violence, Science and Metaphysics, Imperialism, History etc. 

Articles intended for publication in printed media should be precise, focused, formal--and above all critical-- employing a methodological approach. It should address themes of relevance to above mentioned topics in an objective and unbiased manner and providing solid argumentation. Our objective is to attract and influence open-minded readers from all over the world.

Articles which sound closed-minded, contain inflammatory language, and/or display an inappropriately indignant tone will not be accepted for printed media. Keep in mind that a writer's credibility depends to a great extent on the perceived tone of his or her words. This does not imply that we discourage our writers to be passionate and diligent in their defense of a point of view, but rather that common sense and good manners should prevail.

Once a manuscript is tentatively scheduled, proofs are sent to the author. Corrected proofs should be returned within 14 working days. Authors should focus on suggested changes only and try to keep changes/improvement minimal.

Like most publications, Mukto-mona cannot guarantee publication of submitted articles. But we do guarantee careful consideration of each article submitted. Unless otherwise agreed, copyright will be transferred to Mukto-mona upon publication.

Submission Requirements

When a  write-up is accepted for publication, the Guidelines for Final Manuscript Submission will be sent to the author. In addition to two hard copies, authors are strongly encouraged to provide a final, revised version of accepted manuscripts on an electronic medium.

Submissions should include the author's mailing address, phone number, and email address and send to: Mukto-mona Publishers, [Address].  Articles should be submitted as two sets of 8-½ x 11" hardcopy with double-spaced type. In addition, the hardcopy should be accompanied by either a 3-½" computer diskette, a 100-megabyte Zip disk, or a CD-R containing the article text in Microsoft Word format (PC), Rich Text Format (PC .RTF), or plain text/ASCII (PC), .PDF or HTML format. Hardcopy submissions unaccompanied by media will be considered only by advance arrangement with the editors. 

Figures and graphs should be in high quality camera-ready form, or in any common electronic graphics format. 

Hardcopy photos may be glossy or matte; electronic photos are acceptable if in the editors' judgment their resolution is sufficient. Photos may be black and white or color.

Graphics files too large for a floppy disk may be emailed or submitted on CD-R or on 100-megabyte PC or Mac Zip disk.
