Mukto-Mona Prokashani:
A Bold Next Step on behalf of  Mukto-mona!

Desire for publicity is a natural trend for most organizations. Mukto-Mona, a non-profit, non-political* online discussion forum founded by freethinkers, rationalists and humanists of Bangladesh and South Asian origin living abroad back in May 2001, is no exception to this trend. However, unlike many of its commercial counterparts, Mukto-Mona’s desire for a wider publicity is oriented toward the betterment of our existing social-religious-political and educational infrastructure. Contrary to any supernatural beliefs, we, the members at Mukto-Mona strongly, take confidence in the power of human reasoning and science to bring about a positive change in society. Therefore, we are strongly committed to such man-made ideals as democracy, secularism, equal rights of man and woman, nurturing concepts of rationalism, critical thinking, religious tolerance, mutual respect for each other’s cultures.   

As implied above, to members of Mukto-Mona, discussions and debate on rationalistic, humanistic, scientific, religious and political issues amid an amicable and friendly ambience is more than a pastime or amateurish hobby. It’s, on the contrary, an issue of utmost importance given the current rise of religious fanaticism worldwide including our motherland Bangladesh.

Mukto-Mona  has already emerged as the leading web-based forum for promoting rationalism, humanism and free-thought. The time has come, we believe, to spread the news of our efforts and existence among the people back home. We are taking the step to extend our reach to publication in printed media that represents our community and reflect our concerns. We have primarily named it Mukto-Mona Prokashani. By publishing progressive books in Bangla, Mukto-Mona will help promote secular humanist outlook and scientific approaches more effectively. We hope that our move to publication will make Mukto-Mona more effective, more useful and appealing to freethinkers and secular humanists, and better able to attract new readers, specially the readers who do not have the privilege to access internet.

We strongly believe, we are not alone in our missions to see countries like Bangladesh a more liberal, religiously, culturally a more tolerant, and economically, scientifically and technologically a developed country. There are people who may not know about us and the vice versa viz. we are not aware of their existence. We do hope to fill up such gap through our own and independent publications through printed media.

Guidelines for Authors and Contributors

'Mukto-Mona Prokashani welcomes contributions on all subjects relating to the progressive, scientific and rational thoughts. All articles become the property of Mukto-Mona, a non-profit humanist organization. Authors receive no remuneration for their submissions unless specified. The targeted readership includes scholars, students, lay readers with diverse backgrounds and the young readers who are curious to extend their knowledge about the world.

Mukto-Mona considers only original articles that have not been published elsewhere in a printed form. Authors should submit their articles in Mukto-Mona Forum at Mukto-Mona@yahoogroups.com for primary selection and consideration. Authors should not submit an article to any other printed media, while Mukto-Mona  is considering it. Multiple submissions (articles submitted to several publications simultaneously) will not be considered for publication.

Authors who wish to publish their essay through Mukto-Mona Publishers are expected to prepare their manuscripts according to "Mukto-Mona Submission Guidelines" (this document). 

Articles submitted to Mukto-Mona are primarily selected by the Board made up of five members of the association chosen for their expertise in a variety of related fields. Selected article is forwarded to the Editorial Board for evaluation, a process that may last up to three months.  Upon completion of the peer review, the Editor makes a final decision concerning publication.

Editorial Requirements

Author should write clearly and simply; as it goes true with any good writings- purpose of the writer should be conveying the message to the readers effectively than simply creating a grandiloquent write-up. The author should assume an intelligent and progressive general audience. However, depending on the ease and expertise, the respective author should feel free to choose any topics as long as theme is compatible with causes M-M believes in.

 An especial note to authors writing for children: lucidity is must. The write-up, as mentioned above, could be in such diverse forms as story, poetry, essays on science and more. However, some of the topics, Mukto-Mona believes would be especially suitable for kids encouraging critical thinking  are: inspiring stories of great secular scientists/writers/reformers/philosophers, their views about the world, mankind, religion, etc.  in plain language without sounding offensive. Illustrations/supporting pictures is optional but would be considered a plus point, if provided.  It is very important that we uphold science, power of reasoning, freethinking to our next generation children. 

All submissions are judged on the basis of interest, originality, integrity, clarity, significance, relevance, authority, and topicality. Categories of submission (for adult readers) include Articles, Abridged Biographies of famous rationalists/scientists/writers/philosophers, Reviews, Story, Opinion/Editorial, and Departments including but not limited to: Humanism, Rationalism, Secularism, importance of maintaining a scientific attitudes/critical thinking in life, Detrimental effects of Religious Violence, Science, Philosophy,  Anthropology,  and History of civilizations etc. 

Articles intended for publication in printed media should be precise, focused, formal--and above all critical-- employing a methodological approach. It should address themes of relevance to above mentioned topics in an objective and unbiased manner and providing solid argumentation. Our objective is to attract and influence open-minded readers from all over the world. We may also make available selected articles in bilingual forms for like-minded readers beyond Bangladesh, or/and new generations of expatriate Bangladeshis living abroad.

Articles which sound closed-minded contain inflammatory language, and/or display an inappropriately indignant tone will not be accepted for printed media. Keep in mind that a writer's credibility depends to a great extent on the perceived tone of his or her words. This does not imply that we discourage our writers to be passionate and diligent in their defense of a point of view, but rather that common sense and good manners should prevail.

Once a manuscript is tentatively scheduled, proofs are sent to the author. Corrected proofs should be returned within 14 working days. Authors should focus on suggested changes only and try to keep changes/improvement minimal.

Like most publications, Mukto-Mona cannot guarantee publication of submitted articles. But we do guarantee careful consideration of each article submitted. Unless otherwise agreed, copyright will be transferred to Mukto-Mona upon publication.

 

Submission Requirements

When a write-up is accepted for publication, the Guidelines for Final Manuscript Submission will be sent to the author. In addition to two hard copies, authors are strongly encouraged to provide a final, revised version of accepted manuscripts on an electronic medium.

Submissions should include the author's mailing address, phone number, and email address and send to: Mukto-Mona Prokashani, [Address below].  Articles should be submitted as two sets of 8-½ x 11" hardcopy with double-spaced type. In addition, the hardcopy should be accompanied by either a 3-½" computer diskette, a 100-megabyte Zip disk, or a CD-R containing the article text in Microsoft Word format (PC), Rich Text Format (PC .RTF), or plain text/ASCII (PC), .PDF or HTML format. Hardcopy submissions unaccompanied by media will be considered only by advance arrangement with the editors. 

Figures and graphs should be in high quality camera-ready form, or in any common electronic graphics format. 

Hardcopy photos may be glossy or matte; electronic photos are acceptable if in the editors' judgment their resolution is sufficient. Photos may be black and white or color.

Graphics files too large for a floppy disk may be emailed or submitted on CD-R or on 100-megabyte PC or Mac Zip disk.

* Mukto-Mona, as a whole, is an apolitical platform. However, it should be made clear- we strongly believe, political awareness is a must for any/all activists/writers with reformation oriented goals. We are not affiliated with any political party in Bangladesh or beyond (be it right wing or left wing). That doesn’t, and we believe shouldn’t, stop us from criticizing their policies/actions, if we believe, such policies/actions are detrimental, sectarian, or/and violates universal declaration of human rights and freedom.
