Ekushe TV Closes down : Part II

-Ajoy Roy

 

Satellite Transmission also closed down

 

    Even Satellite transmission could not be continued by Ekushe Television as police intervened last night. After the press conference, Ekushe TV assured its viewers that although they have been denied the terrestrial facilities by the BTV, the satellite transmission would be continued. After the supreme court verdict rejecting the revision petition the transmission came to an abrupt end around 5 p.m. when the BTV abruptly snapped the terrestrial facilities. However ETV resumed its satellite transmission at 7 p.m. but it could not continue as police intervened (as reported) and ETV was forced to announce that the ETV is closing down completely from 10-50 p.m. on Thursday, 29. 08. 02.

Hearing by the appellate division on 'review petition'

 

    The full bench of the appellate division of the supreme court constituted of Chief Justice Mainur Reza Choudhury, Justice Md. Rahul Amin, Justice Mohammad Fazlul Karim, Justice K M Hasan, Justice Sayed J R Modassir Hosain, Justice Abu Sayeed Ahmad, and Justice Md. Fazlul Haque heard for last six days from 24-29th August, 2002 the arguments and counter arguments of the learned lawyers from both sides.

 

24. 08. 02

 

Barrister Rafiqul Haque appeared for ETV and took part in the deliberation. Barrister Haque argued that if there were any irregularities in obtaining the license the High court could have sent the matter to the T&T Regulation commission for settlement, instead Barrister Haque contended that the High court acted as an inquisitive journalist and as an enquiry commission which was not the responsibility of the High court. He further said that the same wrong procedure was followed in the verdict of the appellate division. If the verdict of the appellate division were read coolly the learned review petition would be ashamed off. When inquired by the Chief justice which parts of the verdict were of such nature the Barrister Haque remained silent. On the other hand the chief justice informed Mr. Haque that they would feel ashamed if they knew the way the license was acquired.

 

25. 08. 02

 

Barrister Haque resumed his arguments this morning saying that if anything wrong had been done in this matter why the ETV should be punished for the fault or irregularities they did not do. He informed the court that the then Prime minister approved the proposal relating to license. The brief summary of the proposal was prepared by the ministry of information and was placed before the Prime minister. Prime minister.

 

26. 08. 02 -27. 08. 02

    Today (26. 08. 02) Dr. Kamal Hosain appeared before the court on behalf of two foreign investors and continued his arguments on the following day (27. 08. 02). He informed the bench that after one year of establishment of the ETV, the investors invested their capital- now why after three years this could not be continued he failed to understand. Why these companies would have too suffer he raised the question. He further added if there were any irregularities in issuing license the real culprits should be brought to justice. The bench can issue such directives, which would save the life of ETV. The investment board gave their verdict in favour of Ekushe TV and all the ministries honoured the decision. For this reason the foreign investors came forward to invest their capital. Now after three years the question of irregularities is raised. The justice must be done to all and equally. He pointed out that for the sake of justice the review petition should be considered very seriously. It may be recalled that out of the total investment of City Corp International is 40%. The bench however pointed out that in review hearing there is no scope of hearing of the case in details. Once again he drew the attention of the bench that in a democratic country an independent publicity medium is essential. ETV is an independent non-government channel. Since its inception it has been broadcasting neutral opinions, news and our national culture free from government control- we cannot deny its contribution in expressing freedom of opinion, thought and speech. If ETV is closed down the public would be deprived off such news free of government control. For this reason in this review interest of all concerned must be looked upon by the bench. It is also the obligation of the bench to save ETV from its death for the sake of neutrality and freedom of expression that is the basis of our democracy. Advocate Asaduzzaman and Ziaul Haque ably assisted Dr. Kamal Hosain.

 

    Barrister Ishtiaq Ahmed appeared for the chairman of the Ekushe Television limited. Drwaing the attention of the bench he argued that according to the constitution the appellate division can act on its own to invoke its power. There is no limit to this power. Nether the Parliament nor the supreme court has put any control through any law(s). Since the constitution has given the appellate division such power if it wants to employ such power in this case there is no such bar. For the sake of justice the appellate division can apply such power to save the life of Ekushe television. He further pointed out that the verdict given by the appellate division without allowing 'live' is against the natural justice.

 

    Barrister Ahmed argued that the irregularities pointed out by the High court in giving its verdict goes against the government. The writ petitioners have not cited any single instance of irregularities. Who were responsible for those irregularities- have not identified in the verdict of the high court. It was obligatory on the part of the petitioners to prove those allegations of irregularities, but this was not done. The learned judges of the high court gave their verdict on incomplete documents of proof and guided by their own suspicion. Example of such verdict could not be found anywhere.

 

    Barrister Ahmed in his arguments appealed to the bench that the Ekushe Television wants justice from the bench- not any sympathy. He said that the Mr. A S Mahmud got the license as Owner of the ETV on behalf of his establishment. What was wrong in it- the learned lawyer inquired. Mr. Mahmud is a law abiding citizen, he pays regular income taxes. What is his fault? He said that he is very happy to be associated in this case for public interest. He drew the attention of the bench to the fact that verdict of the high court is not flawless. It said that the license was issued through some irregular procedures- but was not mentioned who were responsible for it, who did these irregularities - the minister. state minister, secretary, DG of the BTV, or the prime minister ? So in his opinion the verdict is incomplete.

 

28. 08. 02 -29. 08. 02

 

    Barrister Ahmed resuming his hearing argued that that the supreme court has its own power which the court can invoke in allowing the review petition of the Ekushe Television. He pointed out to the bench that public in general wants the continued life of ETV.

 

Arguments for writ Petitioners

 

    Lawyer Mr. Abdur Razzak appeared for his clients i.e. three writ petitioners. He did not argue much except saying that there is no scope for reviewing the case as the live to appeal had been turned down by the supreme court appellate division on 2nd July.

 

    The attorney general A F Hasan told the bench that the government is not a party in this hearing. It is under pressure from two sides the petitioners and the ETV. One side is blaming the government for issuing license of broadcasting to ETV with terrestrial facilities and the other side blaming the government for giving license in irregular ways.
