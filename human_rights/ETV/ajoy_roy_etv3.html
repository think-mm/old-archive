Ekushe TV Closes down : Part III

-Ajoy Roy

 

 

Epilogue : What followed next ? 

 

ETV started its Terrestrial transmission from 14th April (Bangla Navabarsa), 2000 as a non-government TV channel. It soon became popular for its neat, neutral and free from government control objective presentation. But it did not show anti government attitude either. It was critical of government actions where it was necessary- as a free and independent media must do. It also praised government actions where it should be. Latest example is issue of banning polythine shopping bag- the channel praised the zealous actions of the environment minister. The transmission was a great relief for those viewers, specially of rural areas and low income group people who got tired of hearing the monotony of tales of achievements of the government and its ministers including the Begum Saheba propagated by the channel known as BTV. Its low standard of cultural presentation including drama and magazines, one sided views of discussions conducted and presented by a set of so called intellectuals who sold their intellect to their master i.e. Begum Saheba irritated general viewers and most of them switched over to ETV as they cannot afford to expensive dish channels.

 

Our judiciary could not rather did not feel nor appreciate this finer aspect of the issue. We only lamented and could not prevent the course of action. We lost our battle on the cultural and intellectual front; we lost on the legal front also.

 

Because of the rejection of the review petition by the supreme court appellate division on 29th August, 2002 its transmission came to an end after 2 years 4 months and 15 days. The end was unceremonious and abrupt. The death of ETV was never expected nor desired by the common mass, intellectuals and general intelligent viewers except some uncultured slaves like pro-government elements about whose intellectual level I have serious doubt.

 

Who filed the writ petition and why ?

 

Let us begin from the beginning. After completing the agreement between the government and the Ekushe Television on 9th March, the license was allowed to hand over to Ekushe Television Limited. Ekushe Television came to air on 1st Baisakh, 1407 BS (14th April 2000) coinciding with our new years day. After the caretaker government came to power (15th July, 2001) three persons in the public interest filed a writ petition (in September 2001) against ETV on the charge that gross irregularities was committed in giving the ETV broadcasting license with terrestrial facilities from BTV free of cost. The petition pointed out that as a result of extending terrestrial facilities free of cost the government has been incurring a loss of Tk 60 lakh per year.

 

Who are those great guys came forward to safe guard the public interest after more than year of transmission of ETV ? Were they sleeping during AL government when the agreement was signed and license was issued ?

 

It is quite obvious that the move was political and politically motivated. The main issue before them was not the public interest - the issue before them was to defame ETV and end its activity, which according to their assessment were not conducive to their election victory. 

 

Antecedents and past activities of these petitioners amply prove it. They are Prof. Choudhury Mahmud Hasan of department of pharmacy, Dhaka University, Prof. Md. Abdur Rob of department of geography, Dhaka University and journalist Gias Kamal Choudhury. Needless to say all these three persons are well known political supporters of BNP-Jamat axis- all of them are direct and indirect beneficiaries of BNP government in the past. Mr. Rob and Mr. Hasan are active workers of Jamat i Islami and top leaders of the pro BNP-Jamat group known as Sada Dal in Dhaka university teaching community. Mr. Mahmud Hasan was appointed chairman of Bangladesh medicine and drugs control board by the government during the tenure of Begum Khaleda her first term. Mr. Abdur Rob got promotion during the same time. Role of great journalist Gias Kamal serving the cause of BNP is too well known to elaborate. He is an out and out BNP man. I do not know the English equivalent of the Bengali word 'chamcha'? That is the appropriate word one should put on the shoulder of Gias Kamal as a decorative badge. Who does not know the benefits he derived from Khaleda government in nineties and still deriving? Who does not know his appointment as press counselor in UK ?

 

On 26th September the high court issued a rule to ETV that till the case is disposed off terrestrial broadcasting must be kept suspended by the ETV. The high court however gave permission to continue transmission on the condition that ETV should submit an appeal. Ii is strange that before making any preliminary hearing the HC issued such an order !

 

Has the HC verdict really protected the Public Interest or destroyed it ?

 

On 27th March the learned high court gave its epoch making verdict that killed not only a fine institution but killed feelings of millions of weeping Bengalis who got accustomed with ETV broadcasting for the last two years. To these weeping millions ETV provided an window to see a bigger world that lies outside the geographical limit of Bangladesh and deeper insight of our own beloved Bangladesh- its people, its land and enriched culture. ETV freed them from the clutches, torture and chain of government controlled BTV channel.

The high court did not take note of the feelings of these people although this is case dealing with public interest. The high court only took notice of interest of only three persons and the government interest as it was incurring so called loss of 60 lakh taka per year; but in looking after the interest of those persons, self proclaimed guardian of the public interest and perhaps the government interest it never occurred to the learned members of the bench that their verdict might kill interest of millions of Bangladeshi, the values and conception of a neutral, control-free television channel. Hundred dollar question is has their verdict really safe guarded any sort of public interest, or their devoid of human touch action destroyed the very root of public interest. Would the learned justices constituting the high court bench ever think over it

of their far-reaching verdict? Would they think at their leisure time: has it done good to the society or strengthen the hand of tyranny.

 

Reaction

 

 

Immediately after the rejection of the review petition by the appellate division of the supreme court the attorney general remarked that Ekushe Television, after the pronouncement of the judgement, has no valid or legal basis for transmitting television programs through their channel. They have no valid license- either terrestrial or satellite. The attorney general advised the Ekushe TV to show respect to law and should stop their transmission immediately. The lawyer appearing for the three infamous writ petitioners as expected reiterated what the attorney journal said. This shows that the claim made by attorney general that the government is not a party in the issue is not true. Government is very much in collusion with the writ petitioners. This is the reason why attorney general did not defend the decision of the government though he should have been as continuity of governance demands. This is an indication that government is not clean. It wished the death of the ETV as its later work proved beyond doubt.

 

ETV's lawyer Barrister Rafiqul Haque however differed with the observations of the attorney general. As ETV has already applied for a fresh license with the Bangladesh Telecommunication Regulatory commission, till the body takes its decision the ETV can continue their transmission without violating any legal aspect. He further commented that high court had declared license of ETV invalid but it did not declare action of ETV in handing over the license to Ekushe ETV limited invalid or illegal. According to the BTRC (Bangladesh Telecommunication Regulatory Commission) act the Ekushe Television Limited got the power of broadcasting TV programme till 31st January, 2003. Barrister Ishtiak Ahmed and Dr Kamal Hosain more or less agreed with the contention of Barrister Rafiqul Haque.

 

Today (30. 08. 02) barrister Kamal Hosain elaborated his points regarding continuing transmission of ETV programme. He pointed out that no where in the verdict of appellate division of the supreme court it has been mentioned that broadcasting of ETV would be stopped. The verdict of the bench did not direct the government to close down ETV forcibly. If any one utters such thing it is totally wrong. The ETV has already applied for license according to Bangladesh Telecommunication Act 2001. The regulatory commission would decide the issue independently, the verdict of the appellate division in no way should have impact or influence on their decision or disqualify the ETV obtaining the license. Dr Hosain further commented that for the interest of democracy, we need a government control free independent TV channel. In developing the process of democracy the contribution of ETV has been recognized. Before the establishment of ETV the electronic media was and still is functioning with government controlled directives. The learned lawyer drew our attention that the high court has declared ETV license ineffective and illegal but the license of Ekushe Television Limited has not been declared nether invalid or illegal. The supreme court appellate division has clearly said "The counsel for the Ekushe TV Limited has submitted that it has filed an application with regard to TV license with the Telecommunication Regulating Commission, established under the Bangladesh Telecommunication Act, 2001. Our judgement will have no bearing in considering the application by Ekushe for license by the said commission which is free to decide in accordance with law."

The information minister in his reaction reflected the opinion of the government, which is quite antipathetic to ETV cause. In an interview with BBC the 'red-eyed' (physically appeared so) minister commented that the terrestrial as well satellite license both are declared invalid by the court verdict. He further said that once ETV has become illegal no license could be issued in the same name. After the court verdict, the government has no intention to continuing programme of Ekushe TV channel, the minister continued. In answer to a question he said that if any one apply for license in future 'we would consider the application on legal basis'.

 

These contradictory comments by the minister showed total lack of basic understanding of laws. On the one hand he is uttering that he would consider the application for license according to law, at the same moment he gave the verdict ' no license could be issued in the same name'. This attitude of a senior minister reflects the opinion of the government of Khaleda Begum indicating the intention of total annihilation of Ekushe TV channel.

 

The law minister Maudud Ahmed in an interview with a BTV correspondent said that Ekushe TV channel has no legal basis to continue after the supreme court verdict. If they continue to broadcast even on satellite it will be a risky step. The message is clear that government is not going to tolerate it. To the eye of the government such action on the part of the Ekushe would be treated as illegal. So government made it quite clear what action it would take if ETV continues its transmission even via satellite communication.

 

The erstwhile state-minister of information during AL government expressed his reaction by saying that ministry of information gave approval to ETV its broadcasting facilities- but for some strange reason this ministry was not made a party. He alleged that the government suppressed papers on which validity and legality of issuing license to ETV could have been proved beyond doubt. If the government produced all the papers the situation would have been different.

 

Is the License issued to ETV illegal and invalid ?

The verdict of HC says so. This has been upheld by the supreme court. But the lawyer of ETV Barrister Rafiqul Haque argued otherwise. He pointed out that there was no dearth of transparency in the procedure that was followed to issue license to ETV. The technical evaluation committee headed by the then BTV chief engineer Mr. Anisur Rahman gave a satisfactory report. But during high court hearing of the writ petition Mr. Rahman absented himself on the health ground although he later on submitted an affidavit. However the license was not issued only on the basis of the technical report. All the rules and formalities were observed. A summary was prepared and submitted to the then prime minister to which the PM gave approval. The approval of the investing board was taken. The license was obtained according to the Bangladesh telecommunication Act. So there were no lack of transparency on the part of the Ekushe TV.

 

Barrister Kamal Hosain also opined in the same way. In a press statement he stated that a fresh challenge on the part of the attorney general on the matter of issuing license to ETV has created a new issue. The writ petition by three persons in the name of public interest was filed in September 2001 during the caretaker government of Justice Latifur Rahman. The office of the attorney general, on behalf of the caretaker government, informed the high court bench all matters relating to ETV license is legal and valid. In it, it was stated that all the formalities regarding opening of a non-government TV channel had been completed in a legal procedure. All the relevant and related matters were discussed several times in relevant ministries including finance, interior, defense, post and telecommunication and board of revenue. The entire matter covering all these aspects in a summarized form was presented before the then PM for approval. Thus all the formalities necessary for running a non-government TV channel was observed. A report dealing with all these matter was submitted to the high court division bench. During the hearing in the high court the government of Khaleda Begum did not submit any affidavit contradicting the report submitted during the caretaker government. The attorney general now has no scope of challenging the issue of giving license to ETV.

 

The last moment of ETV broadcasting 

As the verdict of the appellate division of the supreme court is pronounced on 29th August at 11-40 am, the government immediately moved on with unimaginable speed to stop transmission of ETV immediately. To the eye of the government ETV is an illegal TV channel - so any transmission terrestrial or satellite must be treated as illegal. Hence government is out to stop illegal activities of ETV. The Begum Saheba immediately summoned the information minister Tariqul Islam and attorney general A F Hasan. After being briefed She asked them to take immediate steps to stop transmission of ETV channel forthwith.

Following the directives of Begum Saheba the information ministry started moving quickly without wasting any more time. The BTV director general rang ETV's chief programmer Mr. Nowajesh Ali asking him to stop ETV's terrestrial broadcasting immediately which Mr. Ali declined. Immediately afterwards BTV switched of the electronic system of terrestrial network. Thus the programme of ETV's terrestrial came to an abrupt end in the afternoon. Following the decision of the ETV management led by Simon Dring resumed satellite transmission from 7 p.m. evening. This enraged the government. Immediately two offices from CID arrived at the Jahangir tower, head office of the ETV and asked to close down transmission otherwise they would forcibly do it. The two officers again visited the tower at 9 p.m.

 

With unprecedented speed the government sealed six terrestrial centres of transmission of ETV at Rampura, Chattagram, Sylhet, Khulna, Nator and Rangpur after delinking terrestrial microwave linking of ETV. The ETV has been asked to dismantle their towers and VHF equipment. This has put ETV authority in a very difficult situation as it is time consuming; they need place and space for preservation and more over these equipment for which the authority has spent millions of taka are of no use except in terrestrial transmission.

 

Following this threat and fearing the brutal police action that might lead to destruction of valuable equipment and life as well, the ETV authority decided, after consulting with their attorneys, to close down transmission. During this melee the officials from the Telecommunication Regulatory Commission brought in some papers showing government -decision canceling all license of ETV. This is the final and severe blow to the ETV from Begum Saheba's government. This is the way the so called autonomous and independent bodies and commissions in our state work and enjoy their autonomy under Begum Saheba's governance.

 

The government hurriedly removed the last legal basis of ETV. So there is no other alternative but to stop transmission on the part of the Ekushe Television. Hurriedly the programme organizer prepared a final announcement telling its viewers that ETV is closing down but with hope that soon they will come afresh. The announcement call itself 'Ekti Bishesh Ghosana' (a special announcement). At that time, nearing 11 p.m. a drama serial based on novelist Showkat Ali's 'Abashese Prapat' was being transmitted.

 

The Last Announcement of ETV 

The final announcement reads as follows: ( Thursday, 29. 08. 02, time 11 p.m. )

 

" Ekushe Television informs its countless viewers and well-wishers that the appellate division of the supreme court has rejected the review petition of Ekushe Television thus upholding the verdict of the high court. But the supreme court cited that Ekushe Television Limited has submitted application for license according to Bangladesh Telecommunication Act 2001. In the verdict the said court has said that there is no bar in considering their application of obtaining license. It was further mentioned in the verdict that Bangladesh Telecommunication Commission would take independent decision in this matter according to law.

 

Under this backdrop, in consultation with its technical personnel and keeping in view the observation made by the supreme court, the ETV authority decided to continue normal transmission till the issue is settled by the Bangladesh Telecommunication Regulatory Commission. But today at 5 p.m. the government took all steps with a view to stopping all activities of Ekushe Television - permission to use BTV tower and its space was cancelled and the microwave linking facilities and all frequencies needed for transmission given to ETV were withdrawn.

 

Under this circumstances we, with deep regret and sorrow, are forced to close down our entire regular broadcasting programmes. We hope that through legal procedure we will return to you soon. Ekushe Television expresses its deep sense of gratitude and offers thanks to our millions of viewers, listeners and well-wishers scattered inside the country and through out the world including United Kingdom, Middle East and Australia. Ekushe Television helped opening a world-window for common people of Bangladesh. It was a window not only looking for our mother earth but a window for looking at a bigger, a more developed world lying beyond us. Ekushe Television is your television. "

 

Popular newscaster Mr. Shamsuddin Haider read the announcement. As he finished reading it, he broke down into tears. And that tears spread out throughout the millions of viewers of ETV and that needed no tower transmission, microwave linking, nor any space for frequency band of BTV or any support from government of Khaleda Zia or the support of Bangladesh supreme court, the ultimate guardian of our fundamental, constitutional and legal right.

The Ekushe Television at a glance

1998 : The government invited tender for establishment of non government Television Channel and obtaining license to broadcast TV programmes from interested companies. Seventeen companies including Ekushe Television took part. The ministry of information sent those proposals for evaluation to the technical evaluation committee.

On 9th July the technical evaluation committee returned the proposals with a report of evaluation to Mr. Akmal Hosain, Secretary of the ministry of information. Citing the example of neighbouring India the report mentioned that in general the print media, NGOs and religious institutions are discouraged in establishing Television Network.

 

1999 : The agreement of license was signed on 9th March between the first Party Government of Bangladesh and the second party Ekushe Television. On behalf of the Ekushe Television Abu Sayeed Mahmud signed the agreement. Mr. A S Mahmud in a letter of 18th March to information secretary Mr. Akmal Hosain sought permission to transfer the license to Ekushe Television Limited. The ministry of information granted the permission of transfer of the license to Ekushe Television Limited on 5th April.

 

2000 : The ETV started its transmission on 14th April, the day of Bengali New years ( 1 Baisakh, 1407 BS)

 

2001 : Messers Choudhury Mahmud Hasan, Professor of Pharmacy (DU), M A Rob, Professor of Geography (DU) and Gias Kamal Choudhury, Journalist submitted a writ petition in the public interest against ETV in the High Court in the month of September, 2001. The petition alleged that license granted to ETV for broadcasting TV programme using Terrestrial facilities of BTV is illegal. As a result of the transmission using free facilities of terrestrial facilities of 2nd channel of BTV government has been incurring a loss of Tk 60 lakh per year.

 

The High court issued a rule on 26th September directing the ETV to stopping its TV transmission till the petition case is disposed off. The high court however issued stay order of the rule provided ETV filed an appeal against the rule.

 

2002 : On 27th March the high court gave its verdict on the writ petition case stating that the procedure through which ETV obtained the license was fraudulent. The license is therefore invalid and ineffective. The high court kept this verdict abeyance for 5 weeks if ETV intends to appeal against this verdict to the supreme court.

 

On 2nd July the supreme court appellate division of 7-members rejected the live to petition of the Ekushe television thus upholding the verdict of the high court. The court however allowed for continuance of transmission for another 5 weeks during which the ETV might submit a review petition to the appellate bench of the supreme court.

 

On 24th August the appellate division of the supreme court sat for hearing the review petition case. The bench consisted of 7 members led by the chief justice.

 

On 29th August the appellate division of the supreme court rejected the review petition and thus once again uphold the decision of the high court.

 

The ETV lost its final legal battle it has been fighting since September 2001.

 

Personal Note

 

The Ekushe episode has brought some questions to surface that are agitating my mind.

     

  • In which country are we living and what kind of government we are governed by?

     

 

The Court Verdicts

 

I have profound respect to our legal system including the justices and lawyers. And I sincerely hope my comments would not be treated as tantamount to contempt of court.

     

  • The high court has given its verdict in the public interest. Which section of this public the three petitioners represent ? Yes the HC verdict has protected the interest of those three elite, but in giving the verdict have the learned justices took into account of the interest of millions of common mass for whom ETV was a window to outside world, a developed world- vast and varigated?

     

     

  • The question is why the learned bench of HC did not take serious note of the affidavit submitted by the office of the attorney general on behalf of the caretaker government which stated categorically that the license issued to ETV was given through legal procedure following all lawful formalities.

     

     

  • Why the ministry of information, which piloted the entire mechanism, was not made party to the writ case? Why the then secretary of information was not asked to the hearing?

     

Where had the file dealing with relevant matters of license in the information ministry gone? Why attempts were not made to recover the file? Why chief engineer of BTV whose evaluation report was one of the key elements in giving license to ETV was not asked to appear before the HC bench?

     

  • Why the HC acted as an investigating burro? The HC verdict stated that the license given to ETV was obtained through fraudulent procedure. But why the verdict did not point out or identify who were involved in it and how was it accomplished? Can the learned bench issue such a far reaching verdict that would affect not only an institution that has invested 750 millions of taka and provided jobs to over hundreds of young and talented persons and earned reputation as an independent neutral and objective television media that was not know in this part of the world but also affect interest of millions of viewers, on incomplete documents and on the basis of assumed forgery.

     

     

  • Why the highest courts of the country including the appellate division of the supreme court acted so mechanically. Why they applied the laws devoid of human feelings and human touch. Why they would not consider the far reaching consequence of a verdict that they are going to pronounce would impact the society and its members for the welfare of whom all laws are made? Are the laws above human feelings and humanism?

     

     

  • Let us assume an illegal child under a fake parent hood became a renowned man with lot of talents and contributed a lot to the society - when it is discovered that his birth procedure is illegal would the law of the country kill him? Assuming that the license to ETV has been acquired through illegal method for this can the highest courts kill ETV an illegal child in the view of the guardians of our legal system?

     

     

  • My final question is directed to the guardians of our legal and judiciary system. The government is an institution so also is the judiciary and both are continuum- with change of individuals like PMs or Chief justices are their continuity lost ? Or does the previous decisions or verdicts become invalid ? If the decision is a good one and has delivered good to the society, but arrived at in a, say not too transparent way, detected later on would the law(s) kill the decision that has proved beneficial or try to redress or rectify the flaw of the procedure ?

     

Let me give another example. Say one BNP MP in the interest of public filed a case pointing out that the design, place of construction, estimated cost, and construction of the Bangabandhu Jamuna Setu involved malpractice and whole procedure was completed on the basis of fraud and forgery- and our courts found true of his allegation. Will the highest court instruct to demolish the bridge ? And as soon as the court pronounced its verdict would our government send its ministers and cadres to rush to the Jamuna setu and start demolishing it as the bridge had been erected with illegal method by a previous government?
