Ekushe TV Closes down : Part I

-Ajoy Roy

 

Dear Mukto-Monas,

 

From Today 5 p.m Ekushe TV has closed down its transmission following the verdict of the appellate division of the supreme court of Bangladesh. Here is my reaction.

 

Ajoy Roy

=============================================================

 

    At last The ETV Ekushe Television has to be closed down. It has stopped its transmission from 5 p.m.  following the judgement of 'review appellate division' of the supreme court. The judgement upholds the high court decision of 27th March. This means the appellate division agreed with the findings of the high court that ETV got its license through procedures that show some discrepancies, irregularities and lack of transparency. The high court declared in its judgement that the license is illegal and ineffective.

 

    The appellate division bench of the supreme court constituted of Chief Justice Mayor Reza Choudhury, Justice Md. Rahul Amin, Justice Mohammad Fazlul Karim, Justice K M Hasan, Justice Sayed J R Modassir Hosain, Justice Abu Sayeed Ahmad, and Justice Md. Fazlul Haque gave its verdict today (29. 08. 02) at 11-40 a.m. The full division bench has been hearing both sides for last six days from 24-29th August, 2002. The learned lawyers like Barrister Dr. Kamal Hosain in favour of two USA based companies who invested in the ETV, Barrister Ishtiak Ahmed for Chariman of the ETV and Barrister Rafiqul Haque for ETV appeared. On the other hand attorney general A F Hasan and Barrister Abdur Razzak appeared for the Government and the writ petitioners respectively. Even today the full bench of the appellate division continued its hearing till 11-20 a.m. The arguments, eloquence and human approach of the learned lawyers proved futile. Tragedy of the episode is that even the tight lip judges of the  bench appreciated clear, neat and decent performance of ETV, its presentation and neutrality.

 

    It may be recalled that the High Court declared in its verdict on last March 27 stating that the license obtained by ETV for transmission and broadcasting is irregular and thus in effective. The ETV submitted a 'live to appeal' to the Supreme Court against the High Court verdict, which the supreme turned down the appeal by saying 'dismissed' on 2 July. However the Supreme Court allowed continuing ETV's transmission till 24th August within which the ETV authority may submit review petition to the appellate division of the Supreme Court.

 

    In deed this is a sad day for us. We do not want to criticize the learned Supreme Court's judgement - but we want to say that a nice decent TV channel like ETV has to be guillotined under state laws. The episode of ETV clearly points out-  how our state laws operate mechanically devoid of any human touch, sense of beauty and finer points of life. Ekushe had to succumb to the mechanical demand and so called system of our judiciary. What a pity!

 

The history of ETV and its case hearing would be the subject of my next episode on Ekushe TV.

 

Epilogue

The verdict of appellate division that heard the review petition means that the ETV does not possess a valid and legal license any more and hence, according to the attorney general ETV must stop all its activity so far transmission is concerned forthwith. The Ekushe TV authority in a press conference however told that after consulting their legal experts and aids and the technical cum engineering staff they have decided to continue their broadcasting even after the Supreme court verdict. This means even though the terrestrial transmission may not be available the satellite transmission would continue till the verdict of the regulation commission gives its verdict regarding their fresh appeal for new license.
