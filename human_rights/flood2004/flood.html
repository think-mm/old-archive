MM Flood Fund update: Dr. Roy received first fund transfer in Bangladesh

Dr. Roy's confirmation email to Dr. Alamgir:

Dear Dr. Alamgir,

This is to let you know that a sum of (BD) Tk. 148,679.70 (Taka one lakh forty eight thousand six hundred seventy nine & Paisa seventy) only   credited to my account that is equivalent to Singapore dollar 4,355/- (four thousand three hundred fifty five) only. The rate of exchange was 1 S$ = Tk 34.14. The amount has been credited on 21st August. Because of turmoil prevailing in the city, I could not go to the Bank to inquire about. Hope this message will remove your anxiety.

As regards programme, our probing team had visited the area from 15-18th August and saw a few schools that were severely damaged. Yesterday, I had a talk with a leader of the locality and a social worker who commands respect, who called on me at Dhaka. We have two schools in mind one at a Char Khedaimari (in between Roumari and Chilmari) at the mouth of Bramahputra and another at Nageswari about 25 Km from Kurigram. I have asked him to make estimate(s) for both schools with the help of the local people (teachers and respected persons) and the school committee(s). After the estimation we would make a final decision where we would make our contribution. When the estimation is made I myself would visit the places and schools. But remember, the place is still inaccessible and perhaps it would take a few months. In the meanwhile local people will work out on their need- nature & amount.

Kindly pass on this message to concerned persons, mm_publication@yahoogroups.com as well as to moderator MM for posting. With regards,

Ajoy Roy

Dhaka : 26. 08. 04

A humanitarian appeal from MM
 Please Help the Flood stricken Bangladesh People

 

 

Pic: Satellite images have revealed the devastating impact of recent flooding in Bangladesh, with two thirds of country submerged in water.: Flooding is shown as dark blue and bright white to highlight two ways in which the satellite Aqua can spot ground water (Image: NASA GSFC)

Mukto Mona has taken a small but valuable initiative to collect donation for flood affected people in Bangladesh. We would like to extend our gratitude to everybody who have made donations for this humanitarian cause through MM or any other reliable organizations during this critical times of our country. Thanks to NFB, Vinnomot, Banglar Nari, Muktochinta and other forums for posting Mukto Mona’s appeal for donation for flood affected people in Bangladesh .

 

How the money will be spent:  

MM has decided to go with Dr. Ajoy Roy’s suggestion to rebuild one or two schools in flood affected areas after the water recedes.

Professor Ajoy K. Roy is part of a team which is  going to most flood devastated area in northern Kurigram, namely Roumari, a police station under Kurigram district- a char island where the mighty Brahamputra enters Bangladesh . They might visit some other places adjacent to these localities - namely Chilmari, Nageswari and Bhurungamari. Both Roumari and Bhurungamari have sentimental attachments to lot of Bangladeshis’ minds . These two localities remained free during 9 -months of our war during 1971. The Bangladesh flag all the time flew in those two localities. Freedom Fighters set up their local administration in those areas through the directives of Free Independent Bangladesh government at Mujibnagar, when almost rest of Bangladesh was under Pakistani occupation.

This team will collect data regarding damage of school houses and relevant matters in educational area. The team may proceed to Kurigram in the next week as soon as the communication is restored. After getting back the data we may decide which school(s) to take up, and a detail plan would be chalked out then.  

Dr Ajoy Roy is also getting in touch with some remote areas in the district of Bhola to estimate the extent of damage to school houses- the area where he did some erection of low cost houses for some riot stricken poor families following October 1(2001) general election.  

Mukto-Mona advisory board will send a detail report to its members and readers on how and where the money will be spent as soon as a final plan is made after all the data is gathered from the above mentioned areas.
