"Operation Clean heart"

The Arrogant Cowards
Shafiq Ahmad 

Those who are very happy at the army operation cannot see through their myopia the long term implication of the army call up, which results in eleven heart-attack deaths in roughly as many days of operation to turn the heart sacred (or, is it meant to convey that they are the ones with sacred hearts??!!! That would be funny indeed). If these bull-headed sepoys can beat up and take 85-year old women of their own country to their camps blindfolded, only a mad man will nurture the delusion that they will "deliver" us from the scourage of large scale politically-patronized crime.

Those who are cheering the army raid should press the government to first arrest and blindfold those godfathers who form part of that very government, before the sepoys blindfold innocent housewives and their octagenarian mothers-in-law.

I know there will be people who will come back with lame but vociferous arguments against what I have just said, but I could not care less. We are a coward nation and I know that even the officially-organized insult and torture of our elderly will not be reason enough to put aside our myopia and our silly partisanship.

Army 'beats up' 85-yr-old lady, SI's wife in city

Staff Corespondent

Army personnel yesterday allegedly beat up the wife and mother-in-law of a police sub-inspector (SI) after taking them to an army camp at Mugda in the city's Sabujbagh area.

Shahatunnesa, 85, mother-in-law of Kabirul Islam Molla, SI of the Dhanmondi Police Station, was admitted to the Dhaka Medical College Hospital (DMCH) in a critical condition while his wife Minara Begum was released after first aid.

Witnesses said Shahatunnesa and her daughter- were supervising construction work of their building at 87/A, West Madertek in Sabujbagh at about 9:00am when an army team arrived there.

The troops rounded up two youths in front of the building and then sought Minara's permission to enter the building to conduct a search.

Minar let them in, but then got angry when the troops went to the roof.

"Why are you creating unnecessary hassle for us?" she was quoted to have said.

At this, the troops became furious and some of them were locked in a scuffle with Minara Begum. They also grabbed Shahatunnesa when she came to rescue her daughter-in-law, family members alleged. The 85-year-old lady was allegedly kicked and punched.

The army then blindfolded them, took them to a camp at Mugda where they were beaten up and confined. They were however released after more than three hours.

SI Kabirul Islam expressed his grievance over the incident.

"Why should they bat up my old mother and wife? What right do they have? I am a policeman. So, why should criminals stay in my house?" said Kabirul.

"They (army) were dragging away my daughter and I tried to stop them," said Shahatunnesa, while being treated at the DMCH. "But they kicked me in the waist and clubbed me. Later, they took us to the army camp."

No case was filed till filing of this report last night.
