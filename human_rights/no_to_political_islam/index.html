'No' to Political Islam 

Taj Hashmi    

1

This is an appeal to all peace-loving people –– Muslim and non-Muslim –– who would extend their helping hand to any effort to uplift humanity, liberty, equality, fraternity, and above all, justice anywhere in the world. What happened on that dark day of human history, September 11, 2001, which shattered world peace and disgraced Islam and Muslims throughout the world, is a turning point in world history and the history of Islam. It is high time that sensible and civilised Muslims and non-Muslims put their heads together to understand the causes of this catastrophic event with a view to defusing the unnecessary tension, conflict, mutual hatred and lack of trust and understanding between Islam and the West. This is a global and collective responsibility of both the Muslims and non-Muslims of the world to take a positive step towards preventing more violence, injustice and uncivilised behaviour in any name and form, especially in the name of Islam.
   I am sending this message to each and every honest and sincere, brave and righteous person to join our movement, “No-to-Political Islam”, to strengthen our cause –– bringing peace and justice within and outside the amorphous Muslim community. Our prime object being the exposure and the eventual eradication of all obscurantist views and beliefs, programmes and objectives bearing the prefix of “Islamic”, we believe, should get wholehearted support of every civilised human being irrespective of her/his faith and nationality. We would most appreciate your lending support to the movement to crush the evil of “political Islam” from Indonesia to Morocco, and Australia to America and beyond, once for all.

   We want Islamic scholars (not the so-called Ulama, whose knowledge does not go beyond the age-old Fiqh or Muslim jurisprudence, and the controversial Hadis literature), both Muslim and non-Muslim, and organisations and individuals who promote human rights to support our movement against “political Islam” and all the variants of the not-so-divine Shariah law, anachronistic to civilisation, common sense and antipodal to the teachings of the Holy Prophet. 

 Now, what do we mean by “political Islam”? Since all our activities in power perspective, within a community, society or a club, or within a state and beyond at the inter-state level is “political”, whatever Muslims do in power perspective within a clan, village or community by citing Islam as the source and sanction behind such activities is “political Islam”. The persecution of village women in the Salish courts in Bangladesh in the name of Shariah or death threats and execution of women in the name of Islam in countries like Afghanistan, Iran, Saudi Arabia, Pakistan and lately, Nigeria, come within the broad definition of the expression. And we want the disempowerment of the self-styled custodians of Islam, both Ulama and the non-Ulama sections of Muslim leaders, to establish the rule of law in accordance with the principles of human rights and common sense. We do not want the preponderance of the Ulama (Sunni Islam does not recognise any established and formal clergy) and the obsolete Shariah in deciding the fate of the Muslim community. We want the preponderance of “spiritual Islam” at the cost of the “political” one. 

 We know some people justify “political Islam” arguing that Islam is a very unique religion. The religion of Islam, as it began in Mecca in 610, was transformed into a state with the Hijrat or migration of the Holy Prophet from Mecca to Medina, they argue. Some even cite Iqbal and Gandhi as both of them believed in the inseparability of religion from politics. They, however, ignore the fact that Iqbal’s and Gandhi’s association of religion with politics was in the spiritual and ethical sense, rather than in the political sense of the expression. 

 Nothing can be farthest from the truth that the Prophet of Islam ran a theocracy, mingling religion with politics. Although his was a unique situation, the Prophet being the preacher of a new religion and the head of the Medina-based state for about a decade, yet he relied on both the Quran and local customs, traditions and consensus of his followers in the day-to-day running of the administration. Many Shariah codes are but replication of the pre-Islamic rules and traditions. The early caliphs (632-661) also relied on local traditions and common sense in running the civil and military administration of the state. So, there is hardly anything rigid and “Islamic” or divine about the Shariah law as secular, mundane needs and traditions moulded the bulk of the Islamic code. 

We should not ignore the fact that the Holy Prophet was not only the first Arab nationalist –– unifying tribes into a nation –– but also a great and successful synthesiser of mundane, secular (pre-Islamic) Arab traditions along with the spirituality of Islam. We often forget that the Ummah or polity of Islam, as the Prophet conceived of and promoted in Medina, at least in the first five years of his rule, had more Jewish and non-Muslim members than the Muslim minority. It is wrong to assume that the Prophet promoted and ran a theocracy. So, we believe that the mullahs and others who promote an “Islamic State” are talking about something, which never existed before. 

 For centuries “political Islam” is being sold to unsuspecting Muslims as an integral part of the Islamic faith system. But a close look at Khomeini- or Taliban-type of states shows that “political Islam” does not bring any good to anybody other than promoting the interests of the ruling clergy. A monolithic and theocratic Islamic State cannot achieve progress with such a dogmatic and unyielding attitude discriminating against non-Muslims and others. It is divisive, belligerent and dangerous to humanity. Such an effort is bound to backfire endangering the existence of both Islam and its peaceful adherents throughout the world. 

 Since we aim at bridging the gap between Muslims and non-Muslims by placing an alternative to militant and obscurantist “political Islam” for the sake of peace and justice, we would like to enlighten and educate both liberal and militant Muslims and friendly and not-so-friendly non-Muslims through a concerted effort of our team. We believe that ignorance breeds prejudice and prejudice is the nursery of hate-crimes and terrorism. In accordance with Sir Sayyid Ahmad Khan (1817-1898), the founder of the Aligarh Muslim University in north India, and some of his followers, Chiragh Ali, Sayyid Ameer Ali and others who tried to revive the spirit of rationalism, secularism and peaceful co-existence with non-Muslims among Indian Muslims in the 19th century, we are also going to bridge the gap between ignorance and knowledge eventually to bring the East and West closer to each other for the sake of global peace and progress. 

We firmly believe that the so-called tide of “Islamic fundamentalism” is a passing phase and a part of the growing pain and birth pang of progress. But we should not underestimate its baneful effects on the Muslims and the world at large. The bulk of the backward Muslim community throughout the world has been experiencing an unfair deal at the hands of their rulers and their overseas patrons. The colonial legacy of exploitation and misrule is still felt almost everywhere in the Muslim world. Even people in the prosperous but abysmally backward Arab world are not free from such exploitation in the name of Islam and patriotism/nationalism. A similar passing phase was witnessed in the 12th and 13th centuries in parts of modern Syria, Lebanon and Iraq. The “Old Man of the Mountain”, Hassan bin Saba, ran a large gang of hashish-smoking killers (hence the term “assassin”) belonging to the Ismaili sect of Shiites who indiscriminately killed Sunni Muslims and others with impunity until their suppression by Halagu Khan in 1258. This would be too trite an assumption that Ismailis are synonymous with terrorists, as we know how peaceful and civil are members of this relatively prosperous Shiite sect, wherever they live as traders and professionals.
   Consequently through our movement, we are trying to establish that Islam per se is not the problem or threat to world peace and progress. Some misguided Muslims under equally or more misguided leaders, motivated by “political Islam” for various socio-economic and political reasons are the problem. As one can no longer portray the Ismilis as “Assassins”, so eventually one would not associate Islam with terrorism provided we liberate the religion and its followers from the clutches of “political Islam”. We want Western support and cooperation in this regard. As the Western media and intellectuals distinguish Hitler and Nazism from Germany and Christianity for the sake of objectivity, our movement aims at bringing about similar objectivity between Islam and terrorism. Like Buddha, we believe that: “Not by hate is hate destroyed, but by love alone is hate destroyed.”

 Our movement is going to educate Muslims and others about how Muslim vainglory and neglect for philosophy, mathematics, rationalism, science and technology since the 11th century has been catastrophic to the community. We must know that the builders of the Taj Mahal and the Red Fort and the Ottoman conquerors of Europe did not establish a single university (the Ottomans established one as late as 1879). Consequently most Muslims have remained pre-modern, pre-capitalist nourishing medieval, feudal and tribal traditions and culture. Since Islam has not gone through its Reformation and Muslims have not staged their Renaissance to revive the lost glory of the early Abbasid era (before the ascendancy of Mutawakkil the Bigot as the Caliph), half-educated mullahs reign supreme everywhere from Indonesia to Morocco in the Muslim world and elsewhere among the Muslim Diaspora in the West. Our movement is a step towards Muslim Renaissance and Reformation of Islam.
   However, the Ummah needs a thorough home cleaning and soul searching. And this cannot be achieved without the support of Muslim and non-Muslim human rights groups, intellectuals, legal experts, historians, anthropologists, philosophers, scientists and others who want liberal, secular laws and institutions for everyone, everywhere. 

Please feel free to contact me giving your feed back, telling us what you can do for the promotion of our cause:”No-to-Political Islam”. Quite a few enlightened Muslim and non-Muslim scholars and human rights organisations have already lent support to our movement. We want organisational support and team work with human rights activists, development agencies, feminist organisations, NGOs, media and academia throughout the world to fight “political Islam. We need your support and cooperation, now.

 

No-to-Political Islam: further elaborations

  I have been overwhelmed by very positive response from so many people from Australia, Singapore, Bangladesh, Britain, India, Pakistan, US, Canada and elsewhere that it is not possible to acknowledge them all personally. However, I thank them all on behalf of my colleagues and partners in the movement against “poltical Islam.” I am also appreciative of those friends and well-wishers who have mild to serious disagreement with our programme.
   Before shedding more light on our programme and modus operandi, I would like to make a few observations and comments on some of the comments and querries of the readers.
   I am in full agreement with those who think that “political Islam” is anachronistic to Islam and that it is a new phenomenon, not older than the beginning of Western imperialism. Muslim empires and kingdoms remained secular and the not-so-organised Ulama had hardly any role in the running of the state machinery other than giving counsel to government officials and rulers in the form of Fatwas or legal opinions, if and when solicited by the latter. Muslim kingdoms were much more secular than their European counterparts who had to listen to the organised Christian clergy, especially the Pope in the Catholic world. Most importantly, the concept of an “Islamic State” is alien to Islam and early Muslims. The two most frequently used expressions to denote the Muslim community,”Millat” and “Umma”, do not correspond to “Nation” or “Polity” rather they transcend these concepts to mean universal brotherhood and global Muslim community.


   I fully agree with the view that there is no “Islamic system” of running the government, there are only Islamic values and ethics. And while the Quran is sacrosanct and the Muslims are supposed to believe in its divine origin as part of their faith or Iman, there is no such obligation on part of them to accept the Hadis literature and Fiqh (Sayings/traditions of the Prophet and Muslim jurisprudence developed in the 8th-10th centuries). One does NOT cease to be a Muslim by renouncing those Hadises which contradict the Quran, science, common sense … Fiqh cannot be anything permanent for all ages. No Imam Hanifa, Hanbal, Shafi or Malik has any right to take away the right of Ijtihad or reasoning to formulate new laws and principles according to the exigencies of time. So, no Shariah law is that divine or sacred that Muslims cannot raise questions about it, eventually to abrogate the law, if it goes against the Islamic principles of peace and justice. These concepts are universal.

   We, by no stretch of the imagination, want to revive the Abbasid era. We only admire the spirit of rationalism and tolerance which developed under the early Abbasids. And figuratively, we want the revival of the Early Abbasid era, as we also want the revival of the spirit of enquiry and rationalism as developed by the Mutazilites, Qaramatians, Sufis and even the Kharijites. In short, we want to replace the dogmatism and intolerance of Maududi-Hasan Banna-Syed Qutb along with the intolerance and fanaticism of the Taliban and Iranian brand of Islam with liberalism and secular laws and institutions throughout the Muslim world. We want the preponderance of secular, liberal tradition both in the public and private domains. We are opposed to all forms of violence, including stoning to death for adultery and female genital mutilation, exclusion of women from the public life and forcing them to wear Hijab (let alone the barbaric Burqa), as the Quran does not sanction these barbaric pre-Islamic customs and institutions.


   Some commentators have rightly observed that Islam is not the only backward religion and promoter of violence. And that the West, especially America, played an important role in promoting “Islamic terrorist” groups, including the Muslim Brotherhood, Jamaat-i-Islami, Taliban, Hamas and many other clandestine groups during the Cold War. We are aware of this fact. But we need to clean our house first, especially in the wake of the Nine/Eleven. No sensible Muslim can condone what a tiny faction among Muslim militants have been doing by killing unarmed and innocent civilians as such acts are in contravention of the Quran and sayings of the Prophet. We most definitely are not strengthening Western imperialists at the cost of the Muslim world. On the contrary, we want to save both Muslim and non-Muslim victims of “politicval Islam”. In fact, most victims of Islamic fanaticism have been Muslims, in Muslim majority countries.


   Now, to elaborate our programme and modus operandi, I would like to clarify first that we are NOT launching a political movement against “political Islam” in the traditional sense of the expression. Ours is a cultural movement to motivate Muslim intellectuals, lawmakers, politicians and members of the civil society to speak up against those Mullahs who justify violence in the name of Islam and justify subjugation of women, minorities and other weaker sections of society in the name of Islam. We will eventually reach Muslim lawmakers, lawyers and civil society through our publications, seminars, conferences and the media.
   We are going to approach like-minded NGOs and members of the civil society in the Muslim world. We will eventually publish our literature in various other languages, including Arabic, Bengali, Hindi, Urdu, Persian and Indonesian. We are also contacting the media and various political and cultural organisations in various countries to circulate our message.
   We are also going to educate the average Western intellectual, politician, students and the common people who want to work for global peace and justice. We will eventually bridge the gap between the West and Islam. We do not believe that either of the two can live in peace by giving fillip to the concept of the “clash of civilisations”.


   We welcome constructive suggestions from both Muslims (both practicing and non-practicing) and non-Muslims. But ours is not a forum for useless polemical discourse. We do not want to be confrontational, but we will remain steadfast and firm in our determination to establish the truth for the sake of justice and global peace. People working against all forms of religious extremism (Hindu, Christian, Jewish and others), gender activists, development practitioners, lawyers, teachers, journalists, students, human rights activists and social workers are most welcome to join our movement.


   ‘No-to-Political Islam: an appeal’ was first published in various web pages on August 8, and ‘No-to-Political Islam: further elaborations’ on August 17. The ‘further elaborations’ has been slighthy abridged. Dr. Taj Hashmi is the Joint-Convener of the Movement, “No-to-Political Islam”. He has done graduate studies in Islamic, South and Southeast Asian history, politics and anthropology. He has taught at universities in Bangladesh, Australia, Singapore and Canada. His publications include four books on the history, culture and politics of South Asia, Islam and gender. BUILDING THE PARTNER-TEAM - NO TO POLITICAL ISLAM.

In last one year substantial amount of progress has been made towards launching a signature-campaign “No To Political Islam” by a team of Partner-Organizations of Muslim and non-Muslims from the platform of one of the highly reputed humanist organizations of the world, as the “Host-Partner” of the campaign. At the moment, some notable Islami Scholars and Institutions are working for it as well.

For centuries Political Islam has been sold to unsuspecting Muslims as an integral part of the Islamic faith system. But a close look into it shows that Political Islam does not reflect the humanity, justice and equality that Islam preaches. It strives to establish a monolithic theocratic Islamic State. No nation, community or state can achieve progress with such a dogmatic and unyielding attitude that seeks to impose its will on all of humanity, creating division and enmity among the peoples of the world. Such an effort is bound to encourage aggressive attempts from the proponents of other religions. The propagation of “Hindutva” in India and the Ten Commandments engraved in the granite-statue erected in front of Alabama judicial building already reflect such an attempt. Similar possibility remains with “Halakha”, the codified Jewish Law in Israel.

While non-political Islam has a tremendous wealth to present humankind, Muslims must do much better than Political Islam to establish their rights, to prosper, to conduct liberation-wars (as Bangladeshis, with some 75% Muslims of the population, fought and won liberation war in 1971 – the word “Islamic Jihad” was never used) and to resist some Western-government’s arrogances for its geo-political interest. The effort of creating Islami-State cripples the development of Muslims and holds them restless, suspicious and aggressive. Muslims cannot afford to carry this liability anymore. This effort in the past was a human endeavor in their own circumstances. But now we know the importance of living together without conflict and we must progressively develop our present legal system and its application through secular democracy complying with the fairness, justice and equality that all religions including Islam preach.

This is no idle dream. Recently Muslims made much greater progress under secular democracies of North America, Europe and Australia than they did in absence of such system elsewhere. In fact many Muslim scholars are of the opinion that, while no system is perfect, in its true sense, God’s Law has largely been more or less established in these countries, if by that term one means justice and humanity. Muslims have been allowed to migrate in huge numbers without any overpowering racism, their institutions have exploded in number, they are able to assert their legal rights and they have been able to observe Islamic way of life without any hindrance. After living and prospering under secular democracy, many Muslims now realize that this planet is not theirs alone, that they have to share it with others in honor and equity, and not impose their will on others.

As the Prophet (SA) established and led the nascent Muslim community in Medina, it is possible to get misled and mislead people by the notion that his mission was to establish both Islam and Islamic State. But the Qura’an proves it otherwise in such clear words for all Prophets including Muhammad (SA) that there is no scope of human-argument, (For Muhammad (SA) see Sura Al Qahf 29 and 56, Sura Yunus 108, Sura Al Gashiyah 21 and 22, Al Anam 48 and 107, Al Ahzab 45 and 48, Sura Al Mayedah 92 and 99 and specially in Sura Muhammad 9. For other Prophets: -Al Araf-62, 63, 67 & 68, 79, and 93.) Development of Medinian nascent administration was entirely and absolutely circumstantial. Had there been no resistance from his tribe in preaching there would have been no such development. According to many Muslim scholars, in many cases political Islam directly contradicts the Qura’an and the Sunnah. So it is highly embarrassing primarily to Muslims because of its denial to basic human rights including denial of women’s and non-Muslims eyewitness and legitimate rights in certain cases, and denial of non-Muslim’s natural right to lead or defend his/her own motherland.

This campaign is not about reforming Islam or Muslims. As Political Islam effects everybody, it is a peaceful joint effort by non-political people of all faith to liberate Islam from its political stronghold. As in today’s dynamic pluralistic world people of every faith are moving to and living all over the world, we firmly believe that abandoning Political Islam is the first step we can take to strengthen communal harmony and to ensure peaceful co-existence of all faiths on our planet.

Different Partner-organizations of this campaign do have different reasons/opinions to act for the common goal of rejecting Political Islam. Any effort to reconcile all the different reasons/opinions is difficult if not impossible. So their own different reasons/opinions will be separately linked to the dedicated website, which is almost complete. Translating the campaign material in different languages is going on, and will be linked to the Front page.

We are no politicians, police, military or State-machine. All we can do is to create public-awareness and a pressure-group. Once a rich signature-tank is developed, we will proceed to influence all the Governments, International Organizations and medias of the world including the UN and European Parliament, where our Host-Partner currently has its permanent member-representations.

We are at the end part of the long preparation. Please consider your organization joining the team of Partner-organizations and spread this call.

Thanks.
