Indian River connection project


On  last 14th August Indian President APJ Abdul Kalam declared the Indian governments decision to interconnect 30 main rivers including Ganges and Brahmaputra on the occasion of 57th Independence day of India. This project is being initiated to alter the main river water flows with the goal of relieving the water scarcity of the Southern / Western states of India. The tentative budget for the project is over 100 billion dollars. Bangladesh is worried that this project violates international regulations and once implemented would cause severe water scarcity during the dry seasons and push the nation towards ecological disaster apart from negatively affecting agriculture and economy. Mukto-mona is inviting readers and writers to write about this important issue.
