"Activists arrested after cinema blasts"

Mymensingh Episode- its Follow Up : Shariar and Professor Muntasir Mamun taken to Custody.


-Our Dhaka Contact

The official death toll now stands at 18 and over hundreds are injured some with severe injuries. The injured persons are being treated at Mymensingh Medical college hospital, Dhaka combined military hospital and in some clinics of the town. According to the Mymensing hospital source, as reported by channel I, 15 severely injured persons are fighting for their life, many of them lost their limbs during blasts. The bomb blasts took place in 4 cinema halls of the town from 5-30 p.m. to 8-20 p.m. 

So far 21 persons have been arrested by the police in and around the city for suspicious movement. Police source informed that one employee, ticket checker of Ajanta cinema hall first discovered a packet wrapped with black paper under a chair inside the hall. He brought the packet in a room adjacent to a ticket selling counter. As he tried to open the packet, visibly out of curiosity, it exploded. Hall cinema halls are now sealed completely. 

Prime minister Begum Khaleda Zia today visited the Mymensingh hospital and the cinema halls. She deplored the incident and assured that culprits, whoever they are would be brought to justice and severely dealt with. She however hinted this heinous crime against common men perhaps been committed by those people who are out to defame the country and the government. She talked to the injured persons and assured them of best treatment. 

The opposition leaders belonging to AL including Abdus Samad Azad, Jillur Rahman, Matia Choudhury today visited Mymensingh and solaced the victims and relatives of the deceased. They sternly warned the government for failure of protecting lives of innocent people even during Eid holidays. The leaders of the 11 party alliance including Dr. Kamal Hosain, Comrade Khalequzzaman, Hasan Ul Haque Inu, Rashed Khan Menon and other political leaders including Husain Muhammad Ershad of Jatiya Party and freedom fighter Kader Siddiqui visited Mymensingh and the victims receiving treatment at various medical hospitals at Mymensingh. They not only condemned those directly involved in the incidents but failure of the government to combat terrorism which exceeded all previous records. They have not ruled out the possibility of conspiracy committing such crime for perpetual continuation of army operation. Many political leaders like Advocate A. Halim of AL, Hader Akbar Khan, Mujahidul Islam Selim and AL chief Shaikh Hasina condemned the incident at Mymensingh  with sorrow and shock, and called it as preplanned. They demanded impartial inquiry and punishment.     

 In the meanwhile our minister of home affairs opened his mouth after a long time in connection of the arrest of suspicious persons in Mymensingh. Echoing the voice of the Prime minister he slanted the opposition forces for current terrorist activities. He further alleged that the forces (within and outside) that are engaged to destroy country’s sovereignty are encouraging these forces of terrorism for their self-interest. But the honourable home minister, that even under operation clean heart the arrested miscreants and godfathers belonging to BNP far outnumbered those of other political parties including AL. Only party that emerged clean is the Jamat I Islami. Thanks to Khaleda Zia. 

 

In the meanwhile ministry of home affairs instituted a one man ‘Inquiry Commission’, headed by a retired Supreme Court justice Sultan Hosain Khan, to probe into the series of bomb blasts in Mymensingh. The commission will try to identify the people directly involved in it and those are behind, and recommend necessary way-outs for prevention of such events in future.  

Not satisfied with arrests in Mymensingh, under the advice of home ministry, the police have, as reported, so far arrested over fifty persons throughout Bangladesh including Dhaka. 

Today (8. 12. 02) as usual a pre dawn move by the police and intelligent department arrested Professor Muntasir Mammon, Mr. Saber Choudhury, a prominent AL leader, recently bailed out from a similar arrest by the order of HC, Shariar Kabir, noted Journalist & human right activist and Mr. Shafi Ahmed, an ex student leader belonging to Jasad faction of Chattra League. All of them were taken to police custody from their respective residents at different localities. They were produced before magisterial court of Mr. Masud Ali Kabir. The court granted three-day remand to the police custody, although the police prayed for 7 days. Professor Muntasir Mamun, a professor of history department, DU, is a well known and popular columnist. His sharp writings off late invoked anger in the government circles. His activities for the release of Shariar Kabir and other human right activities equally annoyed the PM and other top BNP leaders. They are now out to teach him a good lesson, come what may. It is a calculative move as has been pointed out by our home minister- who reiterated that these persons are actively engaged conspiring against the country and destroying the image of the country. What a pity! A powerless professor with his small pen could be so dangerous that a weaponless professor has to be taken under police custody. Case of Shariar Kabir is quite well known to members of our forum and needs no introduction.

 

Epilogue

In another town Gaibandha, north Bengal a powerful bomb planted in one cinema Hall (choudhury) has been successfully defused by the police after receiving a message from a phone call.
