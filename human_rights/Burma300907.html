Mukto-Mona Condemns the Anti Democratic Violence Unleashed by the Government of Burma

Published on September 30, 2007

 
 

Three days ago we carried a statement from Reporters Without Borders on the brutal violence unleashed by the military dictatorship ruling Burma against students, Buddhist monks and the general Burmese public. Today we are sadly forced to issue a second condemnation, this time our own, as the violence in that unfortunate country spirals out of control and the government shows not even the slightest consideration for the horrendous suffering of its people, something for which it is squarely responsible.

This time, as compared to the regular repression of the Burmese people for over two decades now, something particularly brutal is happening inside Burma and that has provoked even the most die hard supporters of the thuggish Burmese regime to call for restraint. Singapore, whose Senior Minister Lee Kuan Yew once famously asked Burmese Opposition leader, the Nobel winning Daw Auung San Suu Kyi to “Shut up” must be wincing because his son and current Prime Minister of Singapore, Lee Hsien Loong called for calm and restraint from the Burmese generals. Also, China, Burma's largest trading partner and the nation that has sold Burma's generals most of their arms, has called for calm in an unusual, public reprimand to the Burmese generals.

What we hear is that the Chinese waited for UN envoy Ibrahim Gambari to meet the leaders of the Opposition as well as the top leadership and then issued the rare for them public statement when Mr Gambari was denied meetings with both Gen Than Shwe and his Deputy Maung Aye. The Japanese government has sent the Deputy Foreign Minister to enquire into the brutal shooting of a Japanese journalist and to express their condemnation of the violence. What effect either measure would have is not clear. The Burmese regime has worked to isolate that country so extensively from the rest of the world, that it could get away, literally, with continued mass murder.

We hope that the Chinese and Indian governments which hold the key to tightening the screws on the Burmese government would do this in a visible and definite way which hurts the Burmese generals. As the hapless nation's two major partners, these nations hold the keys to bringing the Burmese generals to heel. Failure to do this would make them as responsible as the Burmese generals for the blood that is now being shed inside Burma.
