Bijon B. Sarma

 

In response to my writing concerning media-blindness of the common people in the west Mr. Alamgir expressed opinion and I wrote something to clarify the matter for those who have interest to know. The same however, seemed to Mr. Alamgir as “Mohabharat ..... loaded with gibberish and ... personal opinions” etc. Probably he thought I would engage my entire effort to satisfy his query. Sorry that I did not give it that much importance for valid reasons. I do not believe, the statistics like number of medias, their roles in election, preaching for or against the rightists/leftists, humanists/Islamist etc. can reveal whether or how much the medias influence the common people’s choice of ‘president’.

 

The people of the world as a whole look ahead to see the US president to play positive role for the world community. The same however, may not be the expectations of the US-citizens. But definitely they look for their own welfare. Now the obvious questions are :    

 

(i)  In view of the WTC incident, US-President’s relation with and handling of the Saudi’s and specially, Bin Laden, the government’s failure to catch Laden etc., was their President’s role in the previous term conducive to such expectations ?

 

(ii)  Did the US’s attack on Afghanistan and Iraq resulting in killing of innocent people and deaths of the US soldiers please the US citizens ?

 

(iii) Was the constant failures of their government’s programs throughout the world endorsable by the US citizens ?

 

The other candidate, Mr. Kerry did not have any such spot. Still the US citizens did not elect him. Some additional questions may be :   

 

(i)    Was there any mass rigging in the election ?  Reply : No.

(ii)   Was there any case of gratification ? Or, do the US voters bow to gratification ? Reply : No.

 

QUESTION NO. 01 : No statistics, no propaganda, our common sense tells us, what the US voters should have done in the natural process. After seeing the result of the election we question : what made the US voters to take the ‘reverse’ decision ?

 

QUESTION NO. 02 : What made a prudent US citizen like Mr. Alamgir (who was born in Bangladesh, got US citizenship and now calls his motherland Bdesh) to look for various statistics of medias in websites to prove his ‘notion’ ?

 

The reply to both the questions are : Over-emphasizing on, or being carried over by the medias. I used the term ‘blinding’ to mean the same.  

 

In this age of multiplicity, manipulation and fraud it is nearly impossible to quantify the influence of the medias on people, because what matters in this case is not their number or size, but also their aggressiveness of propaganda, techniques used, cooperation of the political parties, persons employed in preaching, time and extent of broadcast etc. Quite often we have seen  prominent medias to present items like, “Bush has got xx% rise in popularity in x days, Kerry had yy% decrease in y days” etc. We did not believe those because we know how the medias fabricate or manipulate those by using numerous techniques including using paid personnel. We have experiences how the US government arranged ‘manipulated election’ in Indonesia, and what they are now doing in some developing countries .

 

If I take that ‘100.00% medias of Bangladesh and India are anti-Bush’ (as alleged by Mr. Alamgir)  and that ‘99.9% of B(angla)deshi people hate Bush’ (alleged by him), I can assure him, they do it not due to the influence of any media, but due to the activities of the US-president. Fortunately the people of Bangladesh and India have personalities and they are not so ‘orphan’ as to depend on the propaganda of the medias.

 

In spite of affluences and influences, the people outside USA, in general, have little respect for the US because of numerous reasons like, poking nose in other country’s internal affairs including election, too much profiteering tendency by utilizing the ‘dishonesty of the politicians’ and other weak points of the developing countries etc. They also could not accept the activities of one of their ex-presidents for telling lies after taking oath and the failure of their system to impeach the person. The case of ‘99.9% (this figure is wrong) of B(angla)deshi people (to) hate Bush’ (observed by Mr. Alamgir) happened due to such reasons.

 

Even though the WTC incident and attack on Pentagon (why the US-medias highlight the commercial WTC building only ? And if there is any truth in Mr. Alamgir’s statement that ‘100.00% (this figure also is wrong) medias of Bangladesh and India are anti-Bush’, it happened so because the medias had to respect people’s feelings.

 

If Mr. Alamgir thinks Bijon Sarma’s comment like ‘US medias brain-wash citizens’ is to be considered as ‘insulting’ (as alleged by him), then the comment of Mr. Alamgir, an honorable US citizen that “99.9% of Bdeshi people hate Bush is just a reflection of 100% anti-Bush media brain-washing prevalent there” is also ‘insulting’ for the people of his motherland.
