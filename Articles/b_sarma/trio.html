THE TRIO

  Bijon B. Sarma

  

 

(01)                               WHO CRIES ON ROAD IS A “MOTHER”

 

(ON THE STREET OF MANHATTAN WALKS A LADY)

 

Amidst the clean environment of the city

Unclean is her gown,

Among the mob of the descent ladies   

She looks like a clown. 

 

(But she does not care for that).

 

The only thing she cared for in her life 

Was her only son,

The young man went to fight in Iraq 

And was finally gone.

 

(Mother got the information last week)

 

Since then she asks the question

Whomever she finds on her way, 

“Did you see my David, dear ?”

As if the onlooker knows, but won’t say.

 

(IF THIS LADY COULD GO TO FALLUZA, SHE COULD SEE) 

 

In the shattered, scorched and ruined city

A lady removes the brick,

As if her son is hidden under the ruins    

To play his favorite ‘hide and seek’. 

 

(People say, ‘Abdul’ was killed by the US soldiers).

 

The mother cannot think of a life  

Without her beloved son,

Tears roll down her cheeks   

And are dried up by the sun.

 

(BRING A LARGE BALANCE)

 

Bring a large balance, place David on one pan

Place Iraq’s entire oil on the other,

And see, young man David to go down  

And the oil-wealth to weigh like a feather.

 

Now bring another, place Abdul on one side

Dump the other with Saddam’s money

Abdul the little boy is heavier

Where affluence of the king seems so funny.

 

 

You cannot bring back David or Abdul

At the cost of money or oil,

Leaders, why then cause them to die

And their mothers’ hearts to boil ?

 

(WHEN THE LEADERS DISREGARD OUR REQUEST THE MOTHERS CRY)

 

“Who cries ?”  : American or Iraqi, we don’t bother.

We know :  Who cries on road is a “Mother”.

 

 

(02)                                                       WHO IS “DAVID” ?

 

 

(I  KNEW DAVID, BUT DID NOT KNOW WHO HE WAS)

I knew a young man, David by name

Do not know whether he was a man or a son,

If he was a son, he must have parents

If a ‘man’, he might have none.

 

(MAN IS A HUMAN BEING)

 

Man is a kind of human being, born out of two

He may be remembered for the ‘moments pleasure’,

The leader needs men to fulfill his ‘mission’

To fight and acquire others’ treasure.

 

Son is different, he bears the blood

He is made of father’s love and mother’s laugh

He cannot forget them till their death,

Nor can they forget him, the tie is so tough.

 

(WHAT THE PAIR AND PARENTS CAN DO)

 

 A pair can sacrifice a young man

For the leader, to fulfill his wish,

 Parents won’t leave their son

Even for the golden dish.

 

(ALL PAIRS ARE NOT PARENTS)

 

Death of a young man may make a pair happy

The ‘compensation’ may create ‘cheers’,

Celebrations and parties may increase their status

They only need to shed ‘crocodile tears’.

 

Parents however, cannot stay stable

They even forget the life’s code,

Some may turn mad, and some other, 

 Roam and roam on the Manhattan road.

 

(LEADER NEEDS PAIRS, NOT PARENTS)

 

If there was no pair in USA, only the parents

The leaders’ aggressions won’t be so fine 

They might have problems to initiate wars in Iraq,

Afghanistan, Vietnam and Palestine.

 

 

(03)                                                       WHO IS “ABDUL” ?

 

(I  KNEW ABDUL, BUT DID NOT KNOW WHO HE WAS)

I knew a little boy, ‘Abdul’ by name

Do not know what he was for his mom,

Whether dear son or a ladder to the heaven

For, they use them as suicide-bomb.

 

(MOTHER PROTESTED)

 

“Wrong, wrong, you are wrong gentleman

 You know a woman has no place there,

Son’s smiling face is my heaven on earth 

‘Heaven over seven skies ?’, I don’t care.

 

(“BUT MADAM, SUICIDE-BOMB IS A REALITY”)

 

 “Yes I know. This is done by the men,

Who lurk for that heaven’s pleasure

They can have sons by many wives

By sacrificing some, they increase the ladder.

 

(IF THERE WAS NO LADDER, NO HEAVEN)

 

If there was no ladder and heaven for the father

His love for the son could have been fine, 

Still innocent sons could have been killed in Iraq,

But no Abdul, sacrificed in Palestine.
