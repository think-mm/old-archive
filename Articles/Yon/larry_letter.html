My letter submitted to CNN's Larry King Live:

Yonatan Fishman 

Larry,

Regarding your panel discussion of "God in the Tsunami" (presented on CNN’s Larry King Live), it is rather deplorable that no atheistic/naturalistic viewpoint was presented, as if the existence of God were an incontrovertible truth. Are the panelists so blinded by faith that they refuse to consider the (likely) possibility that a benevolent God does not exist and that the tsunami was an entirely a-moral natural phenomenon? Benjamin Franklin once remarked that "to see by faith is to shut the eye of reason". The most reasonable explanation for why bad things happen to good people is that a benevolent and all-powerful deity who cares about humanity simply does not exist. The pathetic acrobatic attempts of the panelists and many other religionists to fit a square God into a tsunami-shaped hole in order to derive a cosmic meaning to such natural disasters, reflect a most primitive form of superstition motivated by a conceited and anthropocentric view of the universe. To such horrific events we must respond with compassion driven by a sense of common humanity, not with comforting delusions that get a non-existent God off the hook for crimes against humanity. Prayer and blind faith in fairy tales will, as always, be ineffectual. It is only through reason, science, and international cooperation that such painful disasters might in the future be prevented.

Yon Fishman, Ph.D.

7 January, 2005
