SHAFIQ AHMAD - THE CANDLE OF 1971. 

Ideology, philosophy, values, patriotism do not float in the sky. They are contained in individual’s head and heart. While most people only talk and discuss, there are some that translate these talks in real actions. That is only what contributes to advancement of mankind.

Such a person is Shafiq Ahmad, a distinguished member of Mukto-mona - the writer of “EKATTORER GHATOK DALAL-RA KE KOTHAY” (Killers and Collaborators of 1971: An Account of Their Whereabouts).

Killers of 1971 are killers and rapists of not only humankind. They are some of mankind’s most cruel and heinous enemies of values. It needs imagination to conceive the tremendous mental pressure, unfathomable patriotism and boundless love for fellow countrymen that drove Shafiq Ahmad to take up this huge effort of tracing the leading killers of 1971. Indeed this is a thankless job to serve a forgetful nation of his own. The book is a record of our devils as well as our failure to bring them to justice, not to mention awarding them wealth and power.

This book should have been the Bible of all Bangladeshis. In our recent dark days, this book can show what mistakes and blunders we did before as commoners and leaders, and what we should do now. Does not matter how much we are lost in darkness, does not matter how furious is the stormy ocean, this book can always serve as our lighthouse. Once we translate its message to action, we can always come back to our lost track of values.

As a nation we are used to the faulty culture of recognizing our heroes/heroines only when they are gone. It is time we recognize when they are still with us. Hence Mukto Mona is honoring itself by declaring it’s deepest respect and recognition to this exceptional patriot of ours, Shafiq Ahmad. Indeed he is one of our nation’s brightest heroes.

We know he is currently engaged in further research deep into 1971. We wish him a healthy and active life so that he can serve our beloved nation further in the unique way as he did before. He is more than an individual, he is an institution himself; an institution of 1971, the ever greatest glory our unfortunate motherland ever had.

Promising its every service to his future endeavors, Mukto-Mona’s pride is boundless to have this bright indestructible candle of 1971 as its honorable member.

Moderator, Mukto Mona.

16.12.2002
