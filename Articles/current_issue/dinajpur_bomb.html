Severe bomb blast at Dinajpur: again on the day following Eid ul Azhah

- From our Dhaka Contact

Many historians think that history repeats itself. But we didn't think that our terrorists or their godfathers read the writings of those historians who believed in such social dynamics. The terrorists made history at least in the domain of history terrorism in Bangladesh to repeat itself. These people have not forgotten the events of Mymensing event where precisely on the day following Eid-ul-Fitr five cinema halls were the scenes of severe bomb blats in which at least 18 peoples died and hundreds of people severely injured. The notorious anti-social elements backed by fundamentalists of different political shades once again reminded us that whether "Operation Clean Heart" is on or off, they can do any menace whenever & wherever they want to do.

This is time they have chosen the northernmost district town Dinajpur. According to late night news as reported by Channel I, a severe bomb explosion took place at the heart of the town in a house located at a mahalla known as Chhoto Gurgola at about 6-30 morning today (13th February), the day following Eid-ul-Azhah. According to the report as many as seven bombs were exploded in a house that has been almost completely destroyed. Several persons have been injured. The police have cordoned off the house and journalists were barred from takings pictures of the place of occurrence, the damaged house or the injured persons. According to the report, many eyewitnesses said that just immediately after explosion a microbus removed at least two injured persons to an unknown destination. The police are keeping silence about the matter and they have not been able to trace the fleeing microbus with its passengers. However police could arrest three persons from within the house all in a severe injured condition. They are Shagu, Shafiqul and Badal. The eyewitnesses opined that the bombs had exploded perhaps while some bombs were being made. It is learnt that after two minutes of interval two bombs and then within 15 minutes in total seven were exploded.

The police has confiscated a mobile telephone, few publications of an organization named Jamiyatul Mujahedin and some monograms describing how to make bombs, nails, sands and other materials including explosives thought to be useful for bomb making.

The event is a reminder to all of us that whether Al Quaida or no Al Quaida or whether ISI agents are active or not in Bangladesh, armed terrorists groups contributing to fundamentalism and conservatism are very much active in Bangladesh and they are fast learning the know how of making and using modern weaponry
