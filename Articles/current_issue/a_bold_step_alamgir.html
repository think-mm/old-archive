Iraq War: A bold step towards a peaceful world?

Dr. Alamgir Hussain

I had earlier replied to Mr Bishnu Dey on the article "My thought on the Iraq War" expressing an opposing view when he tried to make a morally justified case (on behalf of America) about the ongoing war in Iraq. However, I have myself have been a staunch supporter of the War throughout run-up and continue to be. But I see this not as a morally justified case on behalf of either party involved but as a necessity on behalf of the Iraqi people. More precisely "I see it as a necessary evil". Indeed "this war should been completed 12 years ago". And possibly a "lack of foresight" or will by Mr. Bush senior has landed us in this situation. I believe the world failed to judge the kind of resolve this animal Saddam possesses. An affluent nation has been turned to ashes thanks to his invasion of Kuwait over a decade ago. I have always been critical of imposing sanctions on nations because of the misbehavior of its tyrant leaders. I had earlier heavy-handedly criticized the US for putting sanctions on nations like Iraq and North Korea which has caused immense sufferings to the helpless people without doing a thing to the despotic leaders. The leaders have never starved for a day for the sanctions but the common people have been starving day in and day out.

I was skimming through the pages of MM and read plethora of articles bemoaning the deaths of a handful of innocent/civilian Iraqis in the war. But can I ask these anti-war humanists how much thought have they spared for "some 1.5 million" children who have died for lack of medicine and malnutrition over the last 12 years. These kids are not innocents? The truth of the matter is, these huge number of children are dying a lot more difficult death than the few dying by the stray missiles, bombs and in crossfire. Why should one mourn the death of those hundreds and thousands of children: they aren't innocent!

Indeed, many a humanists have put the blame squarely on the shoulders on US/UN-authored sanctions for the death of these children. I couldn't disagree but the question I ask: could the US/UN have done better? Saddam's name is associated with death of people, which may well count to a million or more. How a civilized world could embrace a man whose hands are bathed with blood of a million? Still quite shamelessly the world was ready to shake hands with this beast if not for his utter refusal to comply with the UN and give up weapons of mass destruction over the last 12 years. I would say, a chance for peace ended when Saddam expelled the weapon inspectors in 1998. Indeed a decisive war due right after. But the cruel world waited another 4/5 years only to allow death of hundreds of thousands of children across Iraq. Has anybody bothered about those hapless souls?

Humanist/pacifist's hypocrisy

I could say with confidence that those millions of so-called peace-lovers now-a-days swarming the streets of world capitals and metropolis against this war in the name of innocents being killed have hardly ever spared a thought for the agonizing death of over million children across Iraq over last 12 years. But today, their hearts get filled with endless pain seeing a handful of people dying (a rather simple death) because of the war. If this is not outright hypocrisy, I beg someone to teach me what hypocrisy is. And I have said this job should have been finished 12 years ago and all supporters of this war (not the peace-loving opponents) would agree to this than would anybody else. But the truth of the matter is, if the UN/US troops wouldn't have stopped when Saddam offered peace, these same peace-lovers would have swarmed the streets around the world the same way they are doing today because continued campaign to topple Saddam would have cost more civilian lives. We would have seen the same pictures - I can assure you of that.

China/Russia/France : humanist peace-lovers best friends

It's surprising that the anti-war humanists have found their closest allies in China, Russia and France including many others like Pakistan, India and so forth. With the exception of France, (Algeria oppression comes to mind though)China and Russia have been hotspots of flagrant violation of human values and rights in modern times. The world hasn't forgotten what the Russian communist leaders did at the Tienanmen Square some 12 years ago. We just watched how Vladimir Putin's rampaging soldiers went on to a orgy of indiscriminate massacre in Chesnia leading killing of an estimated two hundred thousands civilians and freedom seekers. Pakistan massacred no less than a million civilians and freedom fighters in just 9 month in 1971 in Bangladesh. One prominent case being made for this war is Saddam's use of Chemical Weapons against the freedom-seeking Kurds. I didn't see any difference amongst these atrocities, which ally Saddam, China's communists and Russia Putin in one camp and no wonder why they shouldn't take an anti-war stand. However I have been intrigued by the French stand though. At least part of Iraq's chemical and biological weapons came from France and it's probably guilty feeling. And it's also intriguing about France that even in 1991 gulf war, 1994/95 Bosnia war and the most recent Kosovo war against Slovodan, they been a reluctant part all throughout.

American Hypocrisy

A lot of fuss has been whispered (including myself some time ago) about America itself being the creator of Saddam. I think it's more appropriate to say, America (other Western allies including Britain, France) was a bedfellow of Saddam for some time definitely not a creator. Saddam is a man of his own making who rose to the top as a hit-man, exactly the same way Hitler did. Hobnobbing of western nations with Saddam in the backdrop of the atrocities of Islamic revolution in Iran is definitely a mistake and I thought it would be stupid to stop US from realizing its mistakes. It's ironic that Iraqi people today are suffering from a quest of ridding Iraq weapons of mass distraction including chemical/biological weapons, which were loving gifts from US, UK and France for the beloved friend. But I would say it's better to do what needs to be done.

American Interest

In the run-up to this war, numerous politicians (including American/British), celebrities (including Dustin Hoffman) and the man-on-the-street have reasoned loudly that nemesis of Iraqi people is the American oil being reserved under Iraq's soil. Just the other day, I was talking to a China friend and his outright reasoning was the poor US economy and hence America needs to occupy Iraq for its oil. However, I have time and gain failed to grasp the logic this notion is based on. To my limited knowledge of world history of past one century, I failed to find a single occasion when America has invaded a land for grabbing its resources. Of course, America has virtually ruled countries like Japan and Germany (and to some extent South Korea with heavy military presence there) since the Second World War. But I haven't seen any sign of America siphoning out the wealth of these countries. Had it been so, these countries would have been bankrupt with half-a-century's Blood-sucking by America. I have not seen any sign of that. Remind you, Japan is the second largest world economy, Germany is a wealthy nation and South Korea is one of Asia strong economy. Of course, Saudi Arabia is a case in contentious case in question but I find it hard to agree to the notion that America is out to grab Iraqi oil at the cost of hundreds of lives which it never has done.

Democracy not for imposing

One of the themes of the war in Iraq is to bring freedom and democracy to Iraqi people. And nobody here would disagree that Iraqi people deserve better freedom and a lot better life. In this regard, I have heard a plethora of renowned pundits around the world (including many of my eastern and western friends) arguing democracy is not for imposing, it is to be flourished naturally. I wonder if those great pundits have opened their eyes for a minute to see around. Who is Asia's finest democracy? One need not even open eyes to see it is Japan. A country ruled by one of the most inhuman, murderous and despotic regime before arrival of the US has now transformed into Asia's best democracy. And without dispute, the next best democracy of Asia is South Korea, which also prospered over the last 50 years under the supervision of the US. And what about Germany? Should one still dispute, democracy is not for imposing.

No business of America

While disputing over this issue of War, I have bothered more for the good of the Iraqi people than the Weapons of mass destruction. Many a friends have often come with the notion than America has no business to do here. It's the Iraqis who have to achieve it by toppling Saddam. There's no instance of achieving freedom and democracy otherwise. This is one thing that always irritates me. Something is not in the history and hence it shouldn't be attempted. It's like people used to send messages before via postal mails/telegrams and hence emails shouldn't be used to do the same today. Isn't it utter nonsense?

About Iraq, everyone in this forum would agree that there will come a day when people will rise and go after Saddam. But at what cost on part of the Iraqi people? One simple calculation will do it. If people are to rise and achieve Iraqi freedom, it may cost a million lives instead of a few thousands (I don't think civilian casualties going to cross some 5 thousands in this war) in the US-led ongoing war. And how much time it could take 10, 20 or 50 years or even longer. The way Saddam's sons were shaping themselves up, another generation of Saddam offsprings were sure to rule this country. That simply means extreme poverty for Iraqi people for another 10-50 years or longer. And how about those hundreds and thousands of Iraqis who were to face inhuman tortures/death in the hands of the beastly regime. And of course, millions of children who would have been in line for definite agonizing death. I don't just understand what kind of peace-lovers these anti-war heroes are who are ready to allow death of millions of Iraqis along with unlimited suffering instead of buying them peace at a very cheap price.

One last thing, I believe everyone on this forum will agree with America's immediate withdrawal from Saudi Arabia which indeed Mr Osama bin Laden is fighting for. But the truth of the matter is if US/British troops leave Saudi today, tomorrow Saddam will go and kill hundreds of thousands of Kurds who are enjoying a relative freedom under the Saudi-based US-British military protection over the last decade. It should be cited that when US/British backed off after pushing Kudrs/Shia's for a freedom offensive against Saddam in 1991, estimated 50 thousand Shias and 100-200 thousand Kurds were slaughtered by Saddam. Not only that Saddam has drained the otherwise fertile lands of Kurdish northern region into barren deserts by diverting the water flow of Tigris/Euphrates.

It needs to be done

Many would agree, it's the US and allies who bear substantial responsibility for the suffering of the Iraqi people. It's also true that US/allies played villain once again when they incited anti-Saddam revolts amongst Shias and Kurds following 1991 war then by backing off which costed hundreds of thousands of lives and lead to inhuman torture on many more. That way US and allies lose a great deal of credibility when they now blabber about moral responsibility. However, this is a necessity to mitigate sufferings of the ailing Iraqi people. And of course everybody would argue there are more pressing and long-due issues like Palestine. But I don't see much logic in why we shouldn't allow US/allies to solve the problem in Iraq just because they are not willing to solve the problem in Palestine. Every problem being solved is good for the world and I will put myself squarely behind such move any time.

Other problems that plague the world today include North Korea, Robert Mugabe (Zimbabwe), the regimes in Iran, Syria and Saudi Arabia, India/Pakistan (Weapons of mass destruction; nuclear bombs) plus the paralyzing conflicts across Africa. However, US seems to have targeted Iran and Syria next without paying heed to more pressing problems. But I would like to see a United World take a sincere stand to solve all these issues one by one either by pressure or military means whatever necessary. That way, only the world could become a truly peaceful place. Probably that's how it's not gonna happen as reflected in US's choosing of next target. Of course, the most pressing problem in Afghanistan has been taken cared of. And now Iraq. Whoever be the next, it's only good for the peaceful world. Who knows? Maybe the success in Afghanistan and Iraq may lead to new line of thinking along the continual efforts to make this world a better place. And I'm always for it.
