Dinajpur Bomb Blast: Home Minister Speaks in Parliament

 

Today (16th February) Air Vice Marshal Altaf Hosain Choudhury made a statement at the national assembly on post Eid ul Azha terrorist activities with particular reference to Dinajpur incident and formation of an Islamic belligerent political party Shadat-I-Al-Hikma .

 

On Dinajpur incident the minister said in his statement that those who are involved in terrorist heinous activities like this would be arrested at any cost. Those who are trying to halt the development programmed of the government by destabilizing the elected government and carrying on terrorist cum destructive activities with a view to tarnishing the image of the country in the international community would be exposed to the public. The minister was speaking under section 300 of the parliamentary procedure. The minister informed the house that the police had confiscated some leaflets, hand-notes with instruction of an organization Jama?atul Mujahedin, large number of bombs and huge amount of materials of making bombs. He assured the lawmakers that government would make no stone unturned to roundup theses anti state elements and terrorist activists. Continuing his statement, the minister informed the house that on 13th February a severe bomb explosion took place inside a semi-pucca tin shed house in a locality called Gurgola in Dinajpur town. The police arrested a young man of 22 years named Badal in injured condition from the place of occurrence. According to a statement of Badal there were other two injured persons who had escaped by a rickshaw. A locally made revolver, 13 rounds of bullets, 13 bombs, 3 pellets, 3 active bombs, 3 locally made shotgun, 170 plastic containers and huge quantity of materials of making bombs, mobile phones, bow & arrows were etc. were recovered fro within the demolished house. The minister quoting police source stated that about 5-6 months ago six or seven youths rented the house from its owner Shohrab Ali, who is now performing Hajj in Mecca. The police arrested one Farukh Hosen alias Khaled, an official of adjacent Ayesha Siddika Salafia Islamia Girls? Madrasa and another person of the same madrasa Hafez Habibur Rahman. Police also found, after searching the madrasa, many maps of various countries, documents and papers of Islamist organization. The inhabitants of the locality said that miscreants used to visit the madrasa quite frequently. In this connection two cases had been filed in the past and attempts are being made to arrest involved persons.

 

The home minister further informed the house that the activities of newly formed political party Shadat-I-Al-Hikma has been banned & attempts are being made to arrest leaders of the party. The honourable minister sought the cooperation of the members of the parliament in curbing terrorist activities and heinous actions like bomb explosions. Regarding Shadat-I-Al-Hikma the minister informed the house surveillance of activities of the party revealed that its terrorist & destructive activities are posing a threat to law & order and safety of the country. One leader of the organization, Kawsar Hosain, now on bail under high court order, had been arrested for such activities on previous occasion. On Sunday, 16th February, police arrested a leader of the party named Shamim with two pipe guns and party-documents.

AL proposal : Parliamentary joint team to investigate rejected

 

After the speech of the home minister, AL leader Mr. Suranjit Sengupta took the floor on point of order. He thanked the home minister for acknowledging existence of armed fundamentalist groups conducting anti state and destructive activities inside Bangladesh. He suggested need of the time is to discover the source of the supply of arms and ammunition and unearth the root of terrorism. He proposed a formation of a parliamentary committee with members from the treasury and opposition bench in accordance with section 189 of the parliamentary procedure under the leadership of the speaker. The committee is to investigate all events of bomb explosion including those took place at Dinajpur, Mymensingh and Tangail. The treasury bench members including the law minister rejected the proposal. The speaker rejected the proposal on the ground that the proposal did not fall within the scope of ?point of order?. At this stage the AL members walked out of the assembly in protest.

 

Hikma Activist Shamimuddin arrested

 

It is learnt that an activist of recently formed fighting organization Shadat-I-Al-Hikma, Shamimuddin (25), a founder member of the party was arrested day before yesterday (Saturday, 15th February) from Rajshahi with arms & ammunition. Police arrested him from a hostel building located at Haragram of Mollapara. The owner of the hostel building is Alauddin. They have recovered from under his bed a pipe gun, two rounds of bullets used in shotguns and a pistol. A case has been filed under Ps Rajpara against him with two others namely the socalle convenor of the party Sayed Kawsar Husain Siddiqui and one Mujibur Rahman, roommate of Shamim. The CMM court sent him to jail prison. It may be recalled that a belligerent political party Shadat-I-Al-Hikma was formed with Sayed Kawsar Husain Siddiqui as convenor.

 

11 Party Expressed Concern

 

The 11-party central committee in its meeting on 15th February expressed its deep concern and anxiety over the incident of Dinajpur bomb explosion. The meeting, chaired by Manjurul Ahsan of CPB termed that the incident once again showed total failure of Khaleda government in dealing with terrorism. The leaders of the 11-party urged upon the people to unmask the persons behind such notorious groups and asked the government to arrest real culprits.

 

In a joint statement leaders of the United Social Movement- Ajoy Roy, Pankaj Bhattacharya, Ziauddin Tarek Ali, Rawshan Jahan Sathi and Hayat Mahmud expressed their concern over post Eid ul Azha violence incidents at different areas of the country including Dinjpur bomb blasts. They also expressed concern over rapidly increasing anti-state activities of fundamentalists in the country. Recent example of such activities is open formation of belligerent parties like Shadat-I-Al-Hikma and Islamist organizations like Jama?atul Mujahedin.
