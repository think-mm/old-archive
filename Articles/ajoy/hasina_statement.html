Statement of AL chief at the Dhaka Metropolitan Court

Ajoy Roy

After being picked up from Sudha Sadan by the joint force in a dramatic manner she was taken to Dhaka Metropolitan court at about 7-30. In the drama not less than thousand member strong force took part. I clearly remember even when Bangabandhu was killed by a section of military personnel only a handful of armed men took part. What a fun! An unarmed women who happened to be once PM of the country and chief of a most popular political party of democratic tradition has to be arrested with strong force of thousands armed members. When she was being taken to the local court at 7-30 pm her prison van was followed by a convoy 14 vehicles. In this sense the government gave her real VVIP treatment.

At about 8 am she was produced before the court of Dhaka Metropolitan Magistrate Quamrunnahar. The public prosecutor moved the case showing the documents of the case filed by one businessman Ajam J Choudhury at the PS Gulshan on 13th June. The prosecutor told the court that former PM Hasina has been arrested in connection of this case. The PP appealed to the court that in the interest of the prosecution she should be sent to jail. The lawyers of Hasina led by ex-law minister advocate Abdul Matin Khasru argued for bail without success. They argued that the case is a fabricated one and conspiratory in nature. In the case nowhere the word ‘Extortion’ (chandabaji) was used, argued Advocate Sahara Khatun. The court after hearing the two sides ordered a detention of one month in the jail. She was then moved to a house in the campus of Jatiya Sangsa hurriedly converted to a sub jail.   

In the court erstwhile PM and AL chief Sheikh Hasina addressed the court for 35 minutes pleading her ignorance in the case brought against her on June 13, 2007. Following is the brief report on her statement at the metropolitan court. 

Statement of Hasina 

Ex PM began her statement alleging that the present government brought this false case against her, so that “I can not participate in the coming general election.” She alleged, “At about 5 am the members of joint force informed me that they had come to arrest me. When I asked them did they have any arrest warrant, avoided the question saying that they were there at the ‘order of the top’. I then accompanied them silently in black glass covered prison jeep.”

Hasina continued at the court, “I just now come to know that I have been charged with case of ‘Extortion’ for which the government has arrested me. Me lord, since three generations our family is more or less economically solvent. Our family property is still undivided (ajmali) and we never felt splitting among the sharers. The poor and common men enjoy most of our property. The property at Dhaka has been transferred to the Trust. I have no property of my own. My husband is a Nuclear Physicist who had built the Sudha Sadan out of his income earned while he worked abroad for several years and he lives in this house. I have no greed for wealth for that reason I did not acquired any property or wealth. I had a struggling life of 60 years. So question of extorting money from others does not arise. My father, most of his life, had spent in jails of Pakistan. I myself was interned and gave birth to my first son Joy while I was interned by the Pakistanis. I had never affectionately insisted my parents for anything nor I, any time, lovingly requested my husband even for a Shari. So demanding money from others I cannot dream of. Not a single person in this country would believe this allegation.”

“From the student life I was associated with politics while I was a school student at Azimpur Gils’ School and I was a VP in Badrunnesa College”, continued the AL Chief. “In the college I was General secretary and vice president of Students’ League. Never in my life I have demanded anything wrongly. I have been associated with many democratic movements and struggles in this country. In 1971 along with my father our family suffered. Pakistanis once decided to eliminate our entire family while we were interned at their hands. But ultimately Pakistani minded army personnel of BD army killed my father, mother, younger brothers and many relatives in 1975. Thank God we (my self, my husband and Rehana) were at that time away from the country in Germany. In 2005 on 21 August I was again threatened with my life when grenades were thrown targeting me and other top AL leaders. I was threatened of life at least 19 times. But I am not afraid of these. I only fear almighty Allah and none else.” 

Sheikh Hasina further said, “If I had thrust for wealth I could have earned for my children who could then lead a lavish life without doing anything. My son and daughter earn their livelihood by doing jobs- they are not dependent on me. My son Joy has two degrees in computer technology and he is managing his life through academic pursuit. I was involved in wrong practices they would not have struggled so much. My father did politics for the common men and made supreme sacrifice for his country. Following the footsteps of my father I dedicated my life for the welfare of my countrymen. I have no other ambition except to serve my people in a humble way. Only Allah above and my people in this world have the right to try me, none else.” 

“After the liberation, only twice civil governments came to power one under the Bangabandhu and the second one under my premiership. And during those periods country witnessed visible development. During my tenure as Prime Minister, Ganges sharing pact, Chittagong hill tracts peace talks were signed. Before 2001, then 1600 MW electricity was produced. After we took over, our government increased the production to the tune of 4000 MW. When we took over the food stock was 1 crore 90 lakh tons and when we left the government the stock was 2 crore 70 lakh tons. During AL government the rice was sold at Tk 10 per Kg and Soyabin oil was sold at Tk 28 per liter.” 

Addressing the court she continued, “ We bought Mig 29 jet fighters at a price of only 119 crore billions US dollars instead of real price of 289 crore billions US dollars.” Regarding extortion of money for the party she said, “ The usual practice for maintaining party expenses always had been that the party supporters and well wishers spontaneously give subscription and donations to the party fund. We must distinguish between ‘extortion’ (chnadabaji) and ‘subscription (chanda). This is the time honoured practice how a political party conducts its affairs in financial matters.” 

“We launched movement for transparent ballot box and right voters list- none of the demands has been materialized. The road map for election recently declared by the EC is nothing but a staged drama. The road map election result has already been made too.” Addressing the judge, she remarked, “Though you are the symbol justice, you have no power to deliver justice. You have been given ideas what to do with me. We took several steps to make judiciary an independent system. Our government appointed first lady justice, recruited women in police and army. I want economic emancipation of the people; without economic emancipation democracy does not take roots and without democracy, peoples’ development is an impossibility.” 

Turning to the police, she asked them, “You did go to the bazzar yesterday; was the goods so costly in my time ? “ 

Now the entire effort is to break the political parties. There could not be democracy if you destroy politics and political parties. Without democracy state becomes a failed one. If there is no democracy there won’t be any independence of judicial system and there would be no rule of law.  

In order to snatch away my rights this false charge has been brought in against me. I am completely innocent. I want justice, yes real justice. For the sake of my people whom I love so much I came back to my country against all odds and forgetting the love and affection of my son and daughter. My countrymen love Awami League and me. This false case has been brought against me so that I cannot contest in the election and AL could not win the election. This is the objective of the government..”           

Prof. Ajoy K. Roy--a reputed scientist and human rights activist from Bangladesh--is the member of Mukto-Mona advisory board. He writes from Dhaka.
