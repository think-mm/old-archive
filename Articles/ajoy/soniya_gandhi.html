Dramatic Developments in Indian Politics

-Ajoy Roy



In a dramatic development in Delhi, Mrs. Sonia Gandhi declined to accept the office of the Prime Minister. Until yesterday, it was sure that Sonia Gandhi would the country of over one hundred crores people as its Prime Minister. She was invited yesterday (17th May) by the President Abdul Kalam to see him in connection of forming central government under her leadership. But Sonia refrained from seeing the President hurriedly and planned to see him today (18th May). Since then i.e. yesterday night rumors started spreading that Sonia might change her mind, possibly in view of the BJP’s latest stand (i) refusing to accept her as PM because she was born in Italy, a foreigner in the eye of BJP leaders, (ii) declaring the day of oath of as black day in the history of India, if Sonia takes the oath as PM, (iii) making a decision in BJP and NDA parliamentary meeting to boycott the oath taking ceremony, (iv) taking a vow by the BJP workers and RSS fanatics to take the issue to the streets, thereby creating a law & order and confusing situation, (v) issuing threats of making nuisance and confusion in the country by two top BNP leaders Shusama Swaraj, a member of the Rajya Shabha and Uma Bharati, chief minister of Madhya Pradesh, if Sonia becomes PM.

Sonia saw President Kamal

Today Mrs. Sonia Gandhi, leader of the United Progressive Alliance (UPA) accompanied by top leaders of UPA including Dr. Manmohan Singh and Mr. Pranab Mukherjee. The meeting took place at 12 noon and lasted for an hour. After the meeting she told the awaiting journalists that she would see the president again tomorrow (19th May). This created some confusion and rumor started spreading very fast that Soniajee, as she is beloved called by her followers, has declined to accept the premiership. Later on it was known that Sonia did not submit any list of MPs belonging congress and UPA or the probable list of ministers to the President. Instead, she talked to him in general terms regarding formation of stable new government of UPA, an alliance led by congress commanding a majority in the Parliament.

A Great decision of a great lady

As thousands of congress supporters started assembling in front of the congress central office in New Delhi, it is almost certain that she has declined to accept the nomination of PM. Throughout the country, including West Bengal are found demanding for reverse of her decision. In order to clear the confusion and communicate her farm decision to her colleagues she at 7 p.m. addressed the congress parliamentarians that she has made a farm decision not to accept Premiership of India. Elaborating factors influencing her such heart braking decision she said that a great confusion is being created nationwide on a trivial issue of her foreign origin. In an emotionally choked voice she said that again and again the personal issue of her foreign birth is being brought in the national level. She further added that she had never aspired for that post, what she worked for was a return of democracy based on secularism. She said that her prime concern is to provide a strong and stable government in India. She said that since she stepped in politics she has been working for making Indian democracy and secularism strong. She then added, “I pledged myself to work with the country.’ Before announcing her decision she said, “The position I am in today is because a inner voice always pushed me to do so. This inner voice today tells me not accept the office of the PM for the greater interest of the country.’

According to my assessment the factors that influences her decision are:

(i) she became tired of this issue of foreign origin being brought out again even after decisive election victory, a personal embarrassment for her and the family.


(ii) the opposition, more particularly BJP leadership threatened boycotting the oath ceremony and calling it black day in the history of India.


(iii) BJP workers supported by RSS promised to take the issue to streets and organizing a nation wide movement. She did not want this as this critical juncture of starting the process of setting secular democracy to jeopardize on a personal issue.


(iv).A case has been filed in a lower court seeking Sonia’s eligibility of becoming PM.


(iv) India faces bigger problem today than her being a citizen of foreign origin.

Whether her decision would go down as historic one and would bring good to the Indian people remains to be seen. Jyoti Bose, the CPIM leader was of the opinion that her children Rahul and Priyanka, in view of RSS’s threat, not to expose their mother to any more danger, must have influenced the decision.

Uma Bharati tendered resignation

In the meanwhile two ladies are trying to snatch away the limelight focusing on Sonia’s face to their ‘bright faces’ made of 100% Indian skin. They are trying to stage a drama of their own. One is Shushama Swaraj who has threatened to resign from the Rajya Shabha with her husband. She further said that she would shaven her head if Sonia becomes PM. Thanks to Sonia that Indian people would have not to see the ugly head of such a beautiful lady. The other lady is Uma Bharati, chief minister of Madhya Pradesh, who saw the President yesterday to put forward her case against Sonia’s becoming PM. Perhaps, the President turned a deaf year. Undaunted, the great lady send her letter of resignation to the BJP president Mr. Naidu. Another farcical drama as she knew at heart that this so called resignation letter would receive no attention.

Good news is that whether Sonia or no Sonia components of UPA are holding together. The decision of Sonia has thrown ice-cold water to the face of BJP led NDA. Now it is almost 2 a.m. morning of 19th May, and thousands of congress supporters still are shouting and pressing for reverse of Sonia’s decision. She solaced her supporters that ‘she would sleep on their request’ . I think she has taken the correct decision for good of India’s secular democracy by taking away the initiative from the fundamentalists of BJP led NDA, still a strong anti secular force to be reckoned with. It is not easy to give up the temptation of Premiership of world’s largest democracy. She was, and still is at the doorstep of Indian Premiership. It is a hard, but a very thoughtful decision she has made. Let us see.
