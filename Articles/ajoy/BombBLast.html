Bomb Blasts at AL meeting: I. V. Rahman’s condition critical 

 Dhaka - a city of mass protests

-Ajoy Roy

 
The city witnessed a city of protests and violent rallies of general public. Following the gayebi-jamaja (prayer meeting for the departed souls in absence of their dead bodies) a mourning procession was brought. AL leaders including its Secretary general Abdul Jalil, Workers Party President Rashed Khan Menon, Ganoforum leader Dr. K, led the processionamal Hosain, Dilip Barua, Hasanul Haq Inu etc. The procession soon joined by large numbers of common people turned into a protest procession. It moved through zero-point at Matijheel parading through Bangabandhu Avenue ultimately ended at the central Shahid Minar. The mass demonstration continued in the city till 8 p.m. In many areas the enraged mass destroyed many motor vehicles. The police at many points tried to disperse the mass and in doing so resorted to lathi charge and firing of rubber bullets and tear gas shells. These resulted injuries to common people. The entire Motijheel, Purana Paltan and Bangabandhu Avenue areas turned into a battlefield between the mass & the law-enforcing agency. Police and BDR have been employed in all-important points of the city.         

 Police filed two cases

 The Motijheel police filed two cases on their own (1) case of setting fire and assault against police, and (2) Bomb assault in public place. The Motijhee PS police authority did not entertain the case prepared by AL secretary general Abdul Jalil and Sheikh Hasina’s political secretary Saber Hosain Choudhury. It however recorded the case as FIR. The case filed has two points, (1) Bomb assault to AL meeting with an aim to kill its leader Sheikh Hasina, (2) Conspiracy against democracy. Commenting on the refusal by the police to take the case, AL secretary general said, ‘We do not expect anything better from the Government & its police administration.’

 I.V. Rahman’s condition

 Ms. I. V. Rahman’s condition continued to be critical. She has been transferred to CMH fro DMCH yesterday night. She has already been given 22 bags of bloods. It is learnt that both legs of her have been imputed. PM today went to CMH to see I. V. Rahman and Major general (retd.) Tareq Ahmed Siddiqui, an AL leader. He gave a brief description of the yesterday’s incident on being requested by the PM. The PM requested the attending doctors to provide best treatment to Mrs. Rahman and others receiving treatment at CMH.

 It is now learnt positively that AL leader Sheikh Hasina received a terrible shock during series of bomb and grenade blasts in her very presence. It appeared certain now that she was the prime target of the violent assault. As a result of this assault she received minor injury at her left leg, but she had suffered an internal hemorrhage inside her ears. This has caused heard of hearing. Attending doctors advised her to take a week’s rest and to keep herself away from noisy and loud sound source.

 American Ambassador & India High Commissioner met Sheikh Hasina          

 US ambassador Harry K. Thomas today saw AL chief at her Sudhabhaban residence, Dhanmondi and expressed his sympathy to her. He stayed with her for an hour. Speaking to attending pressmen at Sudhabhaban, the ambassador that he was terribly shocked at the event. He was shocked to learn so many people died and injured. He condemned the incident telling that in a peaceful protest rally such violent bomb blast is simply barbaric and against the spirit of democracy. In a democratic system everyone has the right to protest, he opined. Indian High Commissioner Ms. Bina Sikri also went to express her sympathy to AL chief at her residence. She thanked God that AL chief escaped the assault unhurt. She condemned the incident as cowardice and against the democratic norm.

 Sheikh Hasina, AL chief & leader of the opposition today accused the government of its involvement in the bomb last incident in a AL mass rally at Bangabandhu Avenue. She appealed to the countrymen to come forward to throw out this government from the state power. She appealed for an international inquiry of the incident. He held the government and the communal force responsible for series of bomb blasts, violent killings and terrorism through out the country.   

 She pointed out that following the incident the Begum Zia has no right to stay in power- she should resign immediately. But would she ? Has she that morale ? 

 An Inquiry committee firmed     

 The PM, after holding a cabinet meeting, announced today formation of a one-man inquiry commission headed by Chief Justice of the Supreme Court, Justice Joynul Abedin. The committee was given three week’s time to submit its report.

 The state minister of interior affairs told the press today that he has received more than once directives from PM to look after the law and order situation of the country. The state minister has informed that a red alert has been announced throughout the country. The law and order and peace keeping forces have been advised to take appropriate measure to preserve law & order situation. The minister, a mouthpiece of his master Begum Shaheba vowed that at all costs the culprits would be brought to justice.  

 But we have heard many a times such empty vows, Mr. State minister, not only from your predecessor, air vice marshal Mr. Altaf Choudhury, who once said ‘the hand of law is too long for any culprit to get away’, but from your Master PM Khaleda Begum of empty and sugar coated assurances. But how many culprits have been arrested, not to speak of brought to justice, may I ask Mr. Babar ? And about Madam PM, the less we say better. Yes, I mean it.

 Today two grenades have been discovered, one inside the Dhaka Central jail (!), and another was found lying Golap Shah Mazar, near chawk bazaar.

 Entire Country Protests 

Through out the country protests against bomb blasts were held & in many cities total hartal was observed. On many occasions, the police clashed with the protesters. At Bhairabbazar railway station, a Subarna Express, Chittagong bound train was attacked by a violent crowd. 12 bogies were set fire and completely destroyed, though however no causality was reported.  

The incident raises some salient points :         

  • The question of our so called security system which terribly failed to give minimum security not to common mass, even to the important personalities including the AL chief and leader of the opposition. Was the security system at all serious even in view of the series of violent actions being conducted against important AL leaders including Mr. Suranjit Sengupta, recent bomb blast at Sylhet town in which an AL leader was killed, a popular AL leader of Gazipur killed recently. Or were they deployed there to clash and lathi charge the AL leaders and supporters and beat the attending mass as they were instructed to carry on in the past.

  • Do our intelligence system at all exist ? How come our so well trained intelligence departments including one of Army intelligence totally failed to detect such a violent bomb cum grenade blast publicly in a mass rally of the opposition party ? Or should we assume that allowed the things to happen. Our army, police and other law enforcing agencies cannot escape the responsibility. They must answer to the public why they failed to protect such a large number of innocent public being killed and severely injured. It is no use then keeping such institutions with public money.

  • Why government failed to provide full proof security arrangement of a prescheduled important meeting of Awami League. Specially in the backdrop of recent violent activities (including bomb blasts) of some terrorist groups throughout the country, why our law and order enforcing agencies did not advice the government taking appropriate preventive measure in the meeting of AL. Or should we believe that our security authorities remained callous because of the government’s attitude. And government’s attitude is perhaps as their record shows ‘let AL suffer & disappear’.

  • How come grenades usually used by army could be obtained by terrorists or the transplanted agencies of the interested circles and successfully carried on their mission !

The appropriate authorities including the cabinet headed by Begum Khaleda must answer these and some similar questions. She cannot escape her responsibility. She knew the attitude of the communal & Islamic fundamentalist forces now surround and support her to wards the AL and its chief Sheikh Hasina. These mullah’s jehadi supporters can do anything, - is our democratically elected PM Begum Shaheba unaware of their terrorist activities & motives. If she pleaded innocence, I am not prepared to accept it.                

Dhaka 22 August, 2004
