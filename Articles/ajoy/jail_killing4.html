Jail Killing Tragedy-Part 4: Deposition of the then Jailor

-Ajoy Roy

 

The then jailor of the Dhaka central jail Mr. Aminur Rahman is another very important eye witness of the jail killing tragedy, who not only identified the ill fated 4 national leaders to the killers bur saw how brutally they were gun downed by so-called Capt. Moslem (in fact he was Risaldar Moslemuddin Khan –wrongly introduced himself as Capt. Moslem at the jail gate) & his killer colleagues. Before the special court located at Nazimuddin Road on 10th May 2001 deposition of Janab Aminur Rahman, the then jailor of Dhaka Central Jail was heard by the court on 10th May 1971 at 9 a.m. followed by cross-examination by defence lawyers. The deposition of Janab Rahman is reproduced below.

 
DEPOSITION OF THE THEN JAILOR ON JAIL KILLING TRAGEDY
 

“ After Bangabandhu was killed in August 1975, Major Dalim, Major Shariar, and Major Faruqh on different occasions telephoned to the central jail to know the whereabouts and welfare of the arrested political prisoners on behalf of the then president Janab Khandakar Moshtaq Ahmed. On phone they inquired about the arrested AL leaders like where they were kept etc. On 3rd November at about 3-15 a.m. I came to the jail gate after being instructed by the IG prison by phone. At the jail gate IG prison told me that he had been informed by Bangabhban that some miscreants might come to take away a few prisoners forcibly outside the jail. As per instruction of the IG, I ranged alarm bell and cautioned the officers and employees under me. On hearing the alarm deputy jailor Abdul Jahid, Md. Toeb Ali, Md. Abdur Rauf, Md. Amanullah, Md Iqbal Hosain, and three assistant sergeants of the jail arrived there.

 

A little later a telephone call came for IG prison. After talking over the phone he said, ‘one captain Moslem by name and a few soldiers would come at the jail gate. You go to the gate and bring them to me.‘ At about 4 a.m. morning 5 armed persons in uniform arrived at the gate. They were dressed in back and khaki uniform but bearing no badge. After making entries of their names at the gate register book, they were taken inside the jail. At that time I came to know the identity of Capt. Moslem. As per previous instruction of the IG prisoner I took them to the office of the DIG prison. At that instance I found DIG was talking on a telephone. I came back to my office. A telephone call came at that time from Bangabhaban at my office and Major Rashid wanted to talk to IG prison. On being informed the IG came to my office and talked to some one very respectfully uttering ‘sir, sir. .. ‘

 

In the afternoon, fully armed Major Dalim came to the jail gate. He threatened me to get him inside the jail. On being informed that for entering inside the jail permission of the jail authority is required he got furious saying ‘no permission from any corner is needed. Take me inside.’ I knew the power of Major Dalim. Seeing his ferocious mood I took him inside the jail. He wanted to know where and how AL leaders were housed, and then he also talked to erstwhile SP of Dhaka Mahbubuddin, then interned in Dhaka jail. Then he left the jail.

 

Anyway, at this time Capt. Moslem said, almost in an ordering tone, ‘let’s go inside.’ The IG with Capt. Moslem and all jail officials present then entered inside the jail. When inside, Captain Moslem ordered to take them where AL leaders were interned. At their order we went near to the new jail. Sayed Nazrul Islam and Tazuddin Ahmed were at the very first room of the eastern side of cell no. 15. Capt. Mansur Ali and Quamruzzaman were kept inside two rooms on the western side of the same cell. As per instruction of Capt. Moslem Subedar Abdul Wahed Mridha and I put all 4 leaders inside the room 1 of cell no. 15 and remove all other captives elsewhere. I then informed the IG that 4 leaders have been put in room no. 1. At that time the jail officials and armed persons were together. On hearing this, the men in uniform entered the room no. 1 and killed those 4 leaders after opening fire all around on their bodies. After completing the operation they left the jail almost running.

 

After some time a jail security guard informed us that another 4-army persons were at the gate. When I rushed to the gate the waiting persons asked me to take them inside. I did so after entering their names in the register. Then they said, ‘Shala (brother in law) take us inside. We have to confirm if the leaders really died.’ They threatened me with arms. The soldiers ran towards the new jail and bayonet charged the already dead bodies of the leaders after entering inside room no. 1. Subedar Abdul Wahed Mridha and myself saw this terrible scene as we followed them. Then they left the jail.

 

On the very day (3. 11. 1) in the morning after the incident I recorded the entire incident in `jail book’ in the form of a report. The dead bodies of the national leaders were left in scattered condition inside room no.1 of the cell no.15 till 11 a.m. of 3rd November. The chief prison guard and other prisoners put the dead bodies together with their heads pointing north. I collected the watches and rings of Sayed Nazrul Islam and Tazuddin Ahmed putting them in a handkerchief-packet, and then returned to my office. On 4th November, DIG prison Kazi Abdul Awal made an ejahar (FIR) of the killing incident with the Lalbagh Police station. On the same day a surathal-report (a report describing physical conditions of killed bodies) of the dead bodies was made and postmortem was carried on inside the jail. On 5th November dead bodies were handed over to their relatives. “ (Translated from Bangla)

 

Cross examinations of the witness                                        

 

After the deposition, the witness was cross-examined by lawyers defending Faruq ur Rahman, Major Dalim, Major Rashid etc. The main object of the cross-examinations by the defending lawyers was to show that the witness failing to do his duty and take appropriate measure on the fateful day was now giving a false witness taught by the government. The lawyers did also misbehave with the witness threatening him not to say anything beyond except what he was asked to answer. (11. 05. 01)  

 

Dhaka, 25. 10. 04
