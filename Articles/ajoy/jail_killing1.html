Jail Killing Tragedy: Farcical Judgment
Part 1

-Ajoy Roy

 

Almost 29 years ago 4 prominent national leaders who led our war of liberation to success during those critical days following the horrible night of 25th March 1971 were simply killed in cold blood by a gang of murderers in uniform. These leaders were Sayed Nazrul Islam, Vice President of war time cabinet of Mujibnagar government, Tajuddin Ahmed, Prime Minister, Capt. Mansur Ali, Finance Minister and AHM Quamruzzama, Minister of Interior affairs, relief and rehabilitation. The incident took place between 3-4 a.m. November 3, 1975. As the evidence revealed in those critical days between 3-7th November, Mustaq Ahmed, the then so called puppet president installed by the killers of Bangabandhu, personally directed his 3-4 men led to kill those national leaders and gallant freedom fighters in the central jail. The order was executed with hundred percent precision of success by a team of army personnel led by Capt. Moslem. The jail authority in spite of its best intention failed to protect the nation leaders against. At that time, at the jail office present were Jailor Mr. Aminur Rahman, DIG prison Mr. Khandkar Abdul Awal  and IG prison Md. Nuruzzaman etc. They were simply bewildered, panicked and at a loss what to do at the gunpoint of the intruders in uniform.  At gunpoint they were taken to the ward where 4 leaders were housed. As we know now, the leaders were forcibly isolated from others and taken inside a separated small room, and killers immediately opened brush fire again and again all around till total silence prevailed. The killers then left the place. The death occurred between 4 and 4-30 a.m. Some time after, around 5-30 a.m. morning another killer team led by nayek A. Ali came inside the jail to make sure that operation was successfully carried out. They straight went to the ward and bayonet charged dead bodies to confirm the death. The team left thereafter.

 

The dead bodies lied scattered inside the room till dead bodies of Shahid Tajuddin Ahmed and Syed Nazrul Islam were handed over to their relatives at the jail gate at11 p.m. night of November 4, 1975 and bodies of Qamruzzaman and Capt. Mansur Ali were handed over to their relatives on the early morning of the following day (November 5, 1975).

 

The Trial and the judgment

 

Here are the chronological developments of the jail killing trial procedure:

 

4th November 1975: The then DIG prison Mr. Abdul Awal filed a jail killing case with the Lalbagh police station. The CID was entrusted with the investigation, but within a few days it had been suspended as per directives coming from the top.

 

17th August 1996: After the AL came to power the case was revived. And after a long postponement of 21 years the resumption of investigation started by the CID afresh from 18th August. Mr. Abdul Kahar was given the charge of investigation.

 

15th October 1996: The investigation officer after 2 months submitted the charge sheet against 21 accused. As Khondakar Mustaq Ahmed, the chief architect of jail killing plan, Mr. His chief aid Mahbub Ali Chashi were dead, they were exempted from the list of accused.

The list of accused:

  1. KM Obaidur Rahman, BNP politician (an Ex Awami leaguer) (on bail)
  2. Shah Moazzem Hosain, Ex Prime Minister during Zias regime ( )
  3. Nurul Islam Manjur (on bail)
  4. Taheruddin Takhur, once a zealous follower of Sheikh Mujib turned to a Mustaq accomplice and close aid. (on bail) 
  5. Lt. Col. Faruqur Rahman, the chief planner of killing of Bangabandhu and chief accomplice of Mustaq in jail killing plan (in jail)
  6. Lt. Col. Sultan Shariar Rashid Khan (in jail)
  7. Lt. Col. Shariful Haq alias Dalim, a disgruntled army officer who took active part in killing of Bangabandhu. (absconding)
  8. Lt. Col. Rashed Chudhury (absconding)
  9. Lt. Col. Nur Choudhury, claimed to have killed Bangabandhu by shooting him directly on the terrible night of 15th August. (absconding)

Out of this list Lt. Col Faruqur Rahman (5), Major Bazlul Huda (11), and Lt. Col. Sultan Shariar Rashid Khan (6) are in jail as condemned prisoner being found guilty of murdering Bangabandhu. Accused nos. 1-4 and no 13 were on bail during the trail. Rest 13 accused are at large although it is believed that government knows their whereabouts.                  

 

A special court was constituted for this historic trail at a house very near to Dhaka Central jail. The judge of the case was Mr. Matiur Rahman, a metropolitan judge.

 

12th October 2000: Trial of the case formally began and continued till 20th October 2004 on which the judgment was pronounced. In total it took 277 working days to complete. As many as 64 witnesses were produced out of total 75. The date of judgment was shifted twice from 7th September on account of (1) judge Matiur Rahmans illness and (2) controversy arising out of introduction of new witness and demand of production of certain papers in connection of the place of conspiracy. Anyway finally the court proclaimed the judgment.

 

 

Judgment of the Court

 

Out of 21 accused three were given capital punishment : Risaldar Moselmuddin Khan (who identified himself as Capt. Moslemuddin when he entered the jail with his 3 accomplices), Dafadar Sarafal Ali and Dafadar Abdul Hashem. All are absconding now.

 

Lifetime imprisonment was given to:

 

Lt. Col. Faruqur Rahman, (in jail)

Lt. Col. Sultan Shariar Rashid Khan (in jail)

Lt. Col. Shariful Haq alias Dalim (absconding)

Lt. Col. Rashed Chudhury (absconding)

Lt. Col. Nur Choudhury (absconding)

Major Mahiuddin Ahmmad (absconding)

Lt. Col. Abdur Rashid Hosain, (absconding)

Major Bazlul Huda (in jail)

Major Ahmed Shariful Hosain (absconding)

Lt. Col. Abdul Majed (absconding)

Capt. Kismat Hashem (absconding)

Capt. Nazmul Hosain (absconding)

 

Five accused were acquitted honourably- all were in bail. More over the accused Major Khairuzzaman was reappointed in Foreign office, un precedent example that an accused person of serious case like killing of as many as four national leaders who led a successful victory of our liberation war. This was a clear indication of Governments lenient view government has shown towards this gentleman, and courts soft attitude towards 4 anti AL and pro BNP politicians when the court admitted bails to them. Even a child could assume that what would be the fate of the trial. The fate of the trial was a foregone conclusion, and the court rightly read the mind of the government what it wants. And the learned judge obligingly did it. This is the condition of our judiciary system at the lower level where government control is visibly apparent.

 

Judgment Rejected by sons and daughters of the victims     

 

The four families of the victims very rightly rejected the judgment coming from a weak indecisive judge like Matiur Rahman. As for example Mr. Tajnin Ahmed, MP, son of late Tajuddin Ahmed reacted very sharply accusing the government that it has played a childish game with case in order to make sure of the release of their men and save the lives of killers now absconding. Even the condemned killers of Bangabandhu killing case were spared. An accused during trial was promoted- is this is not a farce, questioned. All of them said they would appeal to the higher court.    

 

In the 374-page judgment the learned judge made some comments saying that this killing is a national disgrace. He lamented that it is an irony of fate that these 4 leaders who put their lives at stake for our independence were killed by a group of people. The judge accused the investigating officer for poorly making the case, which has damaged the merit of the case. His comments in favour of some released persons are very curious which reflects his sympathy for those persons. He blamed the investigation officer for listing their names without valid ground. May we ask the learned judge whatever may be the fault of methodology the fact is the real lanners, the brain of the project successfully evaded the long hand of law. This is the failure of our judicial system. Mr. Rahman, you were very harsh and critical of the procedure of investigation and at one stage you mentioned that it was not at all an investigation, and was politically motivated. If you felt so strongly why did you proceed further of the trial for 227 working days; you could have stopped the trial in the interest of fair trail and ordered for reinvestigation. But you didnt instead you took the advantage of the flaw (?) of investigation to release of the men of your likings. Is this a fair trail, learned judge?
