Jail Killing Tragedy: Part 2

-Ajoy Roy

 

In our previous posting we narrated under what circumstances the 4 top national leaders who led us to victory in our liberation war. Some army personnel killed them in presence of the then IG Prison Mr. Nuruzzaman, DIG prison Khandakar Abdul Awal and other top jail officials. Zaman left the prison around 5-45 a.m. He then accompanied by DIG prison on the same day (3rd November 1975) to the office of the home secretary and reported the jail-killing incident. He sought advice from the secretary what to do next, especially in regard to disposal of the dead bodies. As per written instruction received on the following day (4th November, 1975) of the secretary the dead bodies were handed over to the families of the victims. Here is the statement of the then IG prison which he sent to the then secretary home affairs, government of Bangladesh along with an attached report of the DIG prison Khandakar Abdul Awal on 5th November 1975.

 

Statement of the jail-killing incident 

On 3rd November 1975 early morning of 3 a.m. I received a telephone call of Major Rashid from Bangabhaban (President’s House). He enquired from me if there were any trouble inside the central jail. I informed him that at this very moment the exact situation was not known to me. He informed me that a group of persons in uniform might go to the central jail to take away some prisoners out of the jail forcibly; the major instructed me to alert the jail security guards, and immediately proceed to jail gate. I almost immediately contacted the central Warder in Charge of the jail gate to take the message to the jailer so that proper security arrangement could be strengthened. After 3-4 minutes I received another phone call from Bnagabhaban from yet another army officer who wanted to know if I had already cautioned the jail guards. When I answered in the affirmative, he instructed me to go to the jail gate to see for myself the security arrangement taken. I then ranged to the DIG prison informing him the latest development and asked him to come to the central jail immediately. I also proceeded to the central jail gate without further delay where I found the jailor. I asked him to alert the security system of the jail gate. In the mean while the DIG prison also arrived there. I once again informed about the message I received from Bnagabhaban. Just at that moment we received another phone call from Major Rashid who informed me that within a short while one Capt. Moslem would arrive at the jail gate. He told me that the captain would tell me something. He further instructed me to take the captain inside the jail office and then Messrs 1. Tajuddin Ahmed, 2. Mansur Ali, 3. Syed Nazrul Islam, 4. Quamruzzaman be produced before him. At this stage I wanted to talk to the President directly and the phone was handed over to him (the president). Before I opened my mouth the President (Kh. Mustaq Ahmed) wanted to know if I had clearly understood the instruction of Major Rashid. When I said ‘yes’, he ordered me to carry out the instruction. Within a few minutes a team of 4 soldiers led by Capt. Moslem dressed in black robes arrived at the jail gate. After entering the office of the DIG prison he ordered us to take him and his companions to those persons mentioned above. I told him that as per instruction of Bangabhaban he (the captain) to tell me something. In answer to it he said that he would shoot them (the 4 leaders). We became simply bewildered at his words. Myself and DIG prison tried to get in touch with the president over phone but without any success. At this moment another phone call came to the jailor’s number. As I received the phone, Major Rashid enquired if Capt. Moslem arrived there. I said ‘yes’ and told him that I was at a loss to understand what was happening here. Major Rashid then asked me to talk to the president. I told the president the intention of the captain and his team to kill the 4 prisoners. The president replied that what the captain wanted to do let it be done. At this we got very much excited. The captain at the gunpoint ordered me, DIG prison, jailor and other jail officers present there to take him to the place where the above-mentioned captives were kept. The captain and his accomplices were then behaving very madly, and we had no other way to disobey their order. They ordered us to separate those prisoners from others and put them inside another room. The order was carried out. There the jailor identified them. The captain and his accompanying soldiers opened fire at the prisoners and killed them. After some time another team of soldiers led by Nayek A Ali came to the central jail to confirm whether victims were dead. They straight went to the ward and bayonet charged the bodies of the victims lying there.

 

Signed 

N. Zaman

IG Prisoner (maha kara paridarshak)

05-11-75                              

 

Do I need to make any comments after this gruesome report furnished by one who had witnessed the cold blooded murder of our great national leaders at the instruction of the Killer planner Kh. Mustaq Ahmed and his faithful aid Major Rashid. This is simply sickening. Yet that major is still at large, and the learned judge saved his life by giving him life term imprisonment. Bravo our judicial system !
