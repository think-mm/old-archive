Dear MM-Moderator,

 

As per your request, here is my comments (IN RED BOLD) on the debate between Dr. Muqtedar Khan Director of International Studies at Adrian College in Michigan.  And  Dr. Daniel Pipes Director of the Middle East Forum. Some parts are deleted to shorten, this is to be read with the full text of the debate. Comment on Daniel Pipes is not deemed necessary.  

These are all personal opinions without factual or refferal back-up. How much personal opinions can serve? 

Thanks. 

fatemolla.

 

  

    

Dr. Daniel Pipes's Response: Islam can be interpreted many ways, and there is nothing about it that immutably contradicts democracy. That said, deep and extensive changes will have to precede such changes. 

[THERE ARE SERIOUS ELEMENTS IN POLITICAL ISLAM WHICH ARE IN HEAD-ON COLLISION WITH DEMOCRACY AS WE SEE IT TODAY. ALSO, IF ISLAM CAN BE INTERPRETED IN MANY WAYS, WHICH ONE HAS TO GO THROUGH deep and extensive changes THAT will have to precede such changes? ]

 

Dr. Muqtedar Khan's Rebuttal: I agree there will have to be deep and extensive changes within the Muslim world and in U.S. relations with the Muslim world. 

[WHAT TYPE OF CHANGES?  LEADERS OF POLITICAL ISLAM ARE VERY STRONG AND WILL PORTRAY ANY CHANGE AS AN ASSAULT ON ISLAM ITSELF. ALSO, WHY NOT CHANGE IN MUSLIM PSYCHE?]

 

 What we can do to hasten this process is to ensure that our government stops supporting, financing, and legitimizing dictatorships and monarchies in the Muslim world. 

[THAT CAN NOT BE DONE IN NEAR FUTURE. DICTATORS WILL BE THERE IN THE MIDDDLE EAST].

 Democracratization will probably mean that Muslim governments will be more interested in advancing the wishes of their own people (as in the case of Turkey and its reluctance to support the U.S. in the war against Iraq), but we should be ready to accept this as a necessary consequence of democracy. As we know, the alternative is extremely undesirable to all. 

[ONLY DEMOCRACY IS NOT ENOUGH, IT HAS TO BE IN COMPLIANCE WITH UHRD.] 

  

Dr. Muqtedar Khan's Response: Most non-Muslim critics and often ignorant Muslim advocates of the Sharia (the Islamic Way) equate the Sharia to Hudud laws, the stringent punishments for fornication (flogging), theft (amputation), and adultery (stoning). The maqasid (objectives) of the Sharia is to establish social justice, equality, tolerance, and freedom of religion in societies. 

[PRESENT SHARIA LAWS ARE NOT EVEN CLOSE TO SERVE THE SAID PURPOSE.]  

The Hudud laws are a tiny part of the Sharia. Some of these laws are not even Qur'anic; they are taken from the Old Testament, such as stoning the adulterer (Deuteronomy 22:24). 

[THE WORD HUDUD CAME FROM “HADD”, LIMIT. IT IS IMPLIED THAT THOSE LAWS ARE ALLAH’S, OUT OF HUMAN LIMIT TO TOUCH.] 

Yes, I believe that when the Sharia is interpreted and implemented by educated, enlightened, and compassionate people it will establish social justice and coexist harmoniously with a democratic polity. 

[WITH ALL THOSE UNJUST LAWS? IMPOSSIBLE.  PLEASE CHECK UPCOMING EXAMPLES IN BANGLA.]

  But if uneducated, angry, and bigoted people take the law in their hands and presume to speak on behalf of God, then tyranny is the most likely outcome. 

[SHARIA AS IT IS NOW IS IMPOSSIBLE TO BE INTERPETED AND IMPLIMENTED IN ANY BETTER WAY.]

 

Dr. Muqtedar Khan's Rebuttal: Dr. Pipes seems to contradict himself. First he says that there is nothing in Islam that contradicts democracy and then insists that Sharia is antidemocratic. Sharia is the essence of Islam. 

[NOT  AT  ALL.  The Sharia is decidedly democratic. ABSOLUTELY NOT.]  

The reason for Islam's great record of tolerance and pluralism in the past is the correct understanding and application of the Sharia. 

[NOT AT ALL.  THAT WAS ONLY THE  CULTURE SET BY SOME OF THE KHALIFAS.] 

Unfortunately, the recent examples set by the Kingdom of Saudi Arabia, the Taliban, and others are against the letter and the spirit of the Sharia and have given it a bad name.  

[NOT EVEN CLOSE. EXTRENSIVE PROOFS ARE ALREADY POSTED TO PROVE THAT SHARIA IS ENOUGH ITSELF, IT  DOESN’T NEED ANYTHING ELSE TO BRING A BAD NAME.  IT IS POSSIBLE TO SUPPORT EACH OF TALIBANS ACTIONS, INCLUDING DESTRUCTION OF STATUE OF BUDHA, FROM THE MOST AUTHENTIC SCRIPTURES.  SAUDI HAS HER OWN LAW, OVERLAPING WITH SHARIA.]

 

 The Sharia is elicited from the Qur'an and the Traditions of the Prophet Muhammad (pbuh).

 [NO.  THERE ARE IMPORTANT SHARIA LAWS CONTRADICTING THE QURA’AN.] 

Unfortunately, the underdevelopment of the Muslim world also includes a widespread ignorance of Islam -- even among those who claim to speak for it -- and this severing of Muslims from Islam is partially a result of colonialism. The key really is who, the ignorant or the knowledgeable, defines and interprets the Sharia. 

[SHARIA’S INJUSTICE  IS CRYSTAL CLEAR IN ITS LAWS AND VICTIMS OF FORMAL SHARIA COURTS AND INFORMAL FATWA-COURTS.] 

 

Dr. Muqtedar Khan's Rebuttal: I am not sure whether the U.S. is more secular than, say, France and Canada or even Iraq under Saddam; after all, we have a president who believes in "faith-based initiatives" and has Bible sessions in the White House. We have also had Christian mullahs running for president (Jesse Jackson and Pat Robertson). 

[THEY ARE NOT RUNNING FROM THE PLATFORM OF A RELIGIOUS PARTY WITH AN AGENDA TO ESTABLISH CHRISTIAN GOVERNMENT  BASED ON CODIFIED CHRISTIAN LAW. ]

The focus of the Sharia is on social justice, and Muslim thinkers need to advance contemporary understanding of social justice that includes individual rights and guarantees equality, including gender parity. 

[EASY TO SAY BUT UNREALISTIC.  SHARIA IS IN FRONT OF US. IT IS A MIILION MILES AWAY FROM SOCIAL JUSTICE. AND IT IS UNCHANGEABLE. EACH OF MUSLIM THINKERS WHO TRIED TO ADVANCE IT ENDED UP IN EXILE, TORTURE AND DEATH.]

 

Dr. Muqtedar Khan's Rebuttal: I agree. It is problematic to treat Islam as a nondynamic concept and also to treat its civilizational manifestations as monolithic. Islamic law itself is very diverse and Islamic practices are kaleidoscopic. Nothing, including the understanding of what constitutes the Sharia, is frozen or static. 

[INDEED POLITICAL ISLAM AND HUDUD PART OF SHARIA ITSELF CLAIM HUDUD AS STATIC AND FROZEN FOREVER. THE MEANING, HADD ( limit) MEANS IT IS OUT OF HUMAN HAND. ANYBODY TOUCHES IT IS SEVERLY CONDEMNED AND EVEN KILLED. IT MAY NOT BE EASY TO UPDATE THE TAZIR PART TOO.]

 When Muslim societies will feel safer and will be assured that the West is not seeking to recolonize them or destroy their faith, I am confident they too will become more democratic and protective of individual rights. 

[DR. MUQTEDAR IS MISSING ONE IMPORTANT POINT. FEELING INSECURE IS INGRAINED IN MUSLIMS.  IT IS CENTURIES OLDER THAN 9-11. MUSLIMS LOVE TO PLAY  “VICTIM SYNDROME”. POLITICAL ISLAM LIVES ON MUSLIM’S FEAR OF NON-EXISTING GHOST OF IMAGINERY WESTERN CRUSADE.  LONG AFTER THE CRUSADE WEST IN GENERAL ABANDONED ENMITY TO MUSLIMS (except economic and geo-political interest in the Middle East) AND EXTENDED HELPING HAND FOR MANY CENTURIES. BUT POLITICAL ISLAM ALWAYS MADE MUSLIMS BELIEVE THAT WORLD’S EACH AND EVERY JEWS/CHRISTIANS DO NOT GO TO WORK, DO NOT ENJOY FAMILY LIFE, DO NOT GO TO MOVIE OR VACATION, DON’T DO ANYTHING ELSE THAN SITTING IN CLOSED DOOR ROOMS HISSING CONSPIRACY AGAINST MUSLIMS.]

Recent Pew studies confirm that Muslims deeply fear the U.S., and this fear is heightened by the Bush doctrine of preemptive strike. When more secure, Muslim understanding of their faith becomes more liberal, as in Islamic Spain, and when insecure, Muslim interpretation of their faith becomes more conservative, as in Afghanistan under the Taliban. 

[THE TALIBANI CONCEPT HAS NOTHING TO DO WITH FEAR.  IT WAS ALWAYS THERE EVEN WHEN MUSLIMS WERE STRONG. CONCEPT OF SHARIA-BASED ISLAMI STATE IS  CENTURIES OLD, FROM 12TH CENTURY IBN TAYMIYAH TO 10TH CENTURY HASAN BANNA TO SYED KUTB TO MAWDUDI.] 

 

Dr. Muqtedar Khan's Response: There are internal as well as external barriers to democracy in some parts of the Muslim world. Much of the Muslim world was under colonial occupation. It has yet to recover from the debilitating impact of exploitative foreign occupation. However, a large part of the Muslim world is democratizing now except for most of the Middle East. 

[HALF A CENTURY IS GOOD ENOUGH A TIME AT LEAST TO OPEN EYES, IF NOT TO MOVE. VAST PART OF MUSLIM WORLD BECAME DEMOCRATIC IN THE BEGINNING OF INDEPENDENCE.  AND THEN DUE TO CONTINUOUS FAILURES OF CORRUPT AND UNABLE GOVERNMENTS, PEOPLE BECAME DESPERATE FOR JUST ALTERNATIVE. POLITICAL ISLAM CAME US TO FILL THE GAP AND IS PROGRESSING FAST.]

This region has become politically authoritarian and will need systematic reforms to trigger democratization. Muslim democrats must work towards reform and elimination of internal barriers. Until now the U.S. was a major external barrier to democracy in the Middle East. For example, in 1953 a CIA coup transformed a democratic Iran into an oppressive monarchy that resulted in the revolution of 1979. The U.S. has also supported monarchs and dictators, including Saddam Hussein, in the name of stability and freedom of access to oil. Yes, the U.S. has obstructed the flowering of freedom in the Muslim world. 

[I DON’T THINK SO. PEOPLE THERE ARE SATISFIED WITH SHEIKHA. ALL THE US DID IS  DID NOTHING TO OUST MIDDLE EASTERN SHEIKHS AS SHE DID WITH ALENDE, LUMUMBA, DR. MUSADDEQUE ETC.]

 

 Muqtedar Khan's Response: This is a dubious claim. Do these scholars include Orthodox Jewish practices in Israel and the role of religion in Israeli laws such as citizenship when they make this claim? Do these scholars also show how the beliefs of the coming Armageddon and creationism are compatible with modernity? Modernity had repressed Christianity and to some extent Judaism through reformation, but Christianity is experiencing resurgence in the Americas and Africa. Modernity helped Europe colonize the Muslim world, but it did not defeat or repress Islam, ..

...  and therein lies the difference. I believe that Islam is incompatible with modernity, inasmuch as modernity is opposed to religion. 

[MODERNITY IS NOT OPPOSED TO RELIGION.  MODERNITY IS CONTEMPORARY THINKING, ACTING AND LIVING.]

 However, Islam has a built-in tradition of Ijtihad (independent thinking), which facilitates reform and reinterpretation. If encouraged, Ijtihad can help modernize and revitalize Islamic societies. 

[WITHOUT ANY EXTERNAL INFLUENCE MUSLIMS LIVED WITH THEIR SELF-DESTRUCTIVE TOOL OF IJTIHAD FOR CENTURIES. THE RESULT IS THEIR FRAGMENTATION, REFRAGMENTATION, AND EXTREME INTOLERANCE TO FELLOW MUSLIMS.]

Let us not assume that everything about modernity is good; the Holocaust, the two world wars, nuclear weapons, and environmental degradation are some of the consequences of modernity. Terrorism too is a modern phenomenon.

Dr. Daniel Pipes's Response:- Slavery still exists in a host of majority-Muslim countries (especially Sudan and Mauritania, also Saudi Arabia and Pakistan) and it is a taboo subject. 

[THAT IS NOT TRUE.  SLAVERY AS A WHOLE IS REJECTED BY MUSLIMS FOR GOOD. EXCEPTIONS ARE NOT EXAMPLES.]

 To enable pious Muslims to avoid interest, an Islamic financial industry worth an estimated $150 billion has developed.

 

The challenge ahead is clear: Muslims must emulate their fellow monotheists by modernizing their religion with regard to slavery, interest and much else. No more fighting jihad to impose Muslim rule. No more endorsement of suicide terrorism. No more second-class citizenship for non-Muslims. No more death penalty for adultery or 'honor' killings of women.

No more death sentences for blasphemy or apostasy."

First of all, slavery, as was practiced in America as an industry and as an economic strategy, was a modern phenomenon. Sweatshops and sex slavery, both are modern forms of slavery, and still benefit western consumers. 

[HERE IS A MIX-UP BETWEEF SLAVERY AS REFLECTED IN THE SLAVE-MARKETS, AND ECONOMIC SLAVERY. THEY ARE NOT EQUAL.]

The use of terrorism by some is abhorrent, but struggling for freedom in Chechnya, Kashmir, Palestine, and fighting against genocide in Bosnia is, I think, a good thing.

 [AGREE.]

 Dr. Pipes seems to suggest that Muslims should give up the struggle for justice and the Wilsonian ideals of self-determination.

 

Dr. Muqtedar Khan's Response: I agree with Fareed Zakaria. Religious texts are not blueprints for any society; they are essentially answers to existential questions and articulate general universal principles of ethics. Unlike some contemporary Islamists who insist that the Qur'an is the constitution of the Islamic state, the Prophet Muhammad himself governed Medina by a social contract called the Compact of Medina.

 Can we have an Islamic society where barbaric punishments are not enforced? Most certainly. 

[SOUNDS NICE, BUT UNREAL. JUST TRY AND MOST PROBABLY YOU WILL BE DECLARED AN APOSATE.]

Stable and secure Muslim societies will not feel the need for identity politics -- demands for Hudud implementation is an exercise in identity manifestation 

[–TOTALLY AGREE.]

  and will work towards public good, and for that we need democracy in the Muslim world as soon as possible.

Islam brought equality and dignity to all, including women, and that is undisputed. The problems are the current postcolonial hodgepodge of Muslim practices guided by widespread ignorance of Islamic principles in an environment of insecurity.
