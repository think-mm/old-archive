'Shiksa Andolan Mancha’ Launches Movement Against Government’s Proposed one stream Education at SSC level

Mukto-Mona Dhaka Contact

 

Published on February 13, 2007

Date line: Dhaka, November 28, 2005 

The Shiksa Andolan Mancha (Platform for Education-Movement) formally launches its movement to resist implementation of Government’s ill motivated proposed scheme of one stream Education at SSC level (class IX – X). It may be recalled that about a month or so ago the government all on a sudden declared that it is bringing in a major reshuffling in the curriculum and syllabus at SSC level from January 1, 2006. The first SSC examination under this new scheme would be held in 2008. The government further declared they have already made elaborate preparation to introduce the new scheme. Syllabuses have been framed, books are being written and selective publishers have been deputed to publish the books under new syllabus of all subjects so that these are available to the students in Early 2008. The government has made this decision of changing present system of curriculum at SSC level unilaterally to a new curriculum. It has already spent not less than 500 Crores of money (5,000 millions) in Bangladesh currency (~ US $ 80 millions). This money has been advanced by ADB (Asian Development Bank) as a loan. This means we the downtrodden common people have to repay this loan for a futile and harmful cause. With this money foreign expert advice has been, we believe, procured, consultants for implementation of project and learned advice of writing books etc must have been purchased and team of government representatives visited many countries to study the SSC level system in other countries.  

The nation did not know, the students and their parents did not know that such a major change is waiting for theme, _ that too all on a sudden. Without consulting the teachers, teachers’ organizations, organizations related to Education and Culture, guardians nor the students, the government, backed by a set of bureaucrats, is trying to introduce this major change in our curriculum at the SSC level. The scheme, it wants to implement from the next year has been name ‘one stream education’ (in Bangla: Ek Mukhi Shiksa).  

But What this change means ? 

Readers you might know that our lower level education is split into following tiers :

 

1.      Primary (Class I – V) : 5 years duration (ending the course at the age of 11 years)

2.      Secondary:

(I) : Lower Secondary (Class VI – VIII) (ending the course at the age of  14 years)

(II) : Secondary (SSC) (Class IX – X) (ending the course at the age of 14 years)

(III) : Higher Secondary (HSC) (Class XI – XII) (ending the course at the age of  16 years)     

It may be recalled that the Primary education is now compulsory and free (so called). And boys and girls enter at the age of six plus. Up till now, in general education system at Junior Secondary level (Class VI – VIII) same curriculum and syllabus are followed for all students. At the Secondary level i.e. from class IX specialization starts. At this level education is primarily split into three streams introducing broad specialization namely, Science, Humanities and Commerce. (See the appendix for its details). There are other streams as well called vocational and technical. At Higher Secondary level this specialization further strengthened so that learners became adequately prepared for entry into Higher education at the University/Engineering/Medical/Agricultural institutes. The HSC is in fact a linking between SSC and Higher Education as had been conceived by the Calcutta University Commission of 1917 headed by Sadler. 

The new system as intended by the government unifies the three existing stream (Science, Humanities and Commerce) into one single stream eliminating the present division. This means the concept of educating all students as is done at Lower Secondary Level is being extended for further two years i.e. up to Class X all boys and girls reading under general education system will follow same curriculum and syllabus. This the government has termed as Ekmukhee Shiksa (a combined or integrated education). (for details see appendix). The specialty of the scheme is to make religion and commerce education compulsory for all students. The Muslims will read Islam, Christian students - Christianity, Hindu boys and girls – Hinduism (Sanatan Dharma), Buddhists – Buddhism. Not only that, the pupils studying Islam will have to take Arabic language, Buddhist pupil – Pali, and Hindu pupils – Sanskrit. Thank God, the government did not instruct that Christian boys would have to read Latin. Making the religion as compulsory for the students of followers of 4 religions, the system does not say what the students would study who do not follow none of these religions.

The objective of such a move on the part of the government is to produce a true Muslim, a true Hindu, a true Christian, a true Buddhist – whatever those terms mean, but not to produce a free thinker, a rationalist, a scientifically oriented mind capable of tackling problems based on reason, logic, and scientific procedure. If the government succeeds in its far-fetched design to produce a society divided into religion based water tight compartments the realization of an Islamic State from Modern Muslim State is just one step far. Govt.-Reasons behind the proposed change  

The government of Begum Khaleda and Matiur Rahman Nizami put forward following important arguments in its favour:  

1. “One stream education curriculum ensures a common outlook among the learners, whereas multi stream education curriculum creates division among the learners.”

2. “In multi stream education a learner has to choose a specialized stream of discipline (say commerce) at a tender age.”

3. “In many developing as well developed countries only a single channel education system exists.”

4. “A single channel system supplies a wide-based curriculum to the learners which they necessarily should learn and which is useful for working capability.”

5. In a single channel system smaller number of subjects is included as a result it saves wealth and provides effectiveness. It also provides a comfortable easiness for formulating school timetable, publication of books, holding public examination and marking scripts.

 

The Characteristics of the proposed one channel scheme 

The government claims that following characteristics are endowed with propose one channel system 

* giving more importance on self employment with a view to provide facilities for learning professional training for all learners- Agriculture, Home Economics and Computer-education have been included as compulsory elective subjects.

* relatively more importance has been given on History and Geography in Social Science.

* For the development of religious education and to give stress on fundamental conception on associated languages concerned matters have been included.

Why Shiksa Andolan Mancha rejects the Scheme: The so-called one stream education at SSC level ? 

For the following reasons we reject the scheme and call upon the nation to reject it too.

 

1.      Firstly, from no corner- Students-Gurdians-Teachers, Teachers organization or Organizations related to Education, Cultural and Social Organizations, Educationists and individuals connected with Education any such demand have been raised. Nor any movement has been initiated by any organizations (Studens or Teachers) declaring that the existing present curriculum at SSC level dividing the system in three streams (Science, Commerce and Humanities) is bad and needs to be replaced by a single Curriculum combining three separate broad disciplines. The attitude of the government that they understand best what is good for Education must be done away with. Off late the education commission report 2003, led by Dr. Maniruzzaman Mian, a puppet of BNP and BNP led government, has suggested in its favour. Mr. Mian did what the government wanted, and teachers and students have rejected the report totally.

2.      Secondly, the government on all a sudden and very stealthily and cleverly has declared its intention to introduce the new scheme at SSC level without having meaningful discussion and exchange of opinions with teachers, teachers’ organizations, judging students’ sentiments who would be ultimately affected, guardians, and without having a national debate on this vital stage of learning system which has been accustomed by the Society for the last 42 years if we include our colonial period under Pakistanis.

3.      Thirdly, the compulsory Religious Education coupled with learning of Arabic, Sanskrit and Pali and Commerce Education would make our future generation- infected with religious obscurantism, and pleasure and luxury, devoid of free and scientific thinking. If this system is forcibly introduced cultivation of intellectual liberalism and freedom and blooming of freethinking would be definitely impeded.

4.      Fourthly, this retrograde proposal of unifying three stream of Curriculum at SSC level will definitely narrow down and contract the scope of Science-, Mathematics-, Economics-, even Social Sciences-, and Commerce-Education.  as these are compulsory for all students irrespective of their merits, bent of mind and interest. The standard of education of these subjects surely will have to be lowered down because these subjects have been made compulsory for all students irrespective of their merits, bent of mind and interest.

5.      Fifthly, not very far away in time scale, a significant change was brought in the curriculum of from Primary to HSC level in the name of modernizing our curriculum and syllabus and new style books have been written and published incurring huge amount of money whose far reaching result and net effect yet to be perceived or felt- its success and failures yet to be analysed by the experts, students, teachers, educationists and people as a whole. But before doing this, another major attempted jump (a big change) will not lift us anywhere, instead this attempt will let us down in a deep trough. Repeated experimentation with young minds is surely not only desirable but harmful too.      

6.      At present the success of SSC Examination falls between 40-50%. If this scheme is implemented the percentage of success will deteriorate significantly, we are afraid. Should we on the one hand lower down the standard of education, and on the other hand increase the percentage of failures thereby creating a huge unemployment every year. Do you want to turn ourselves a half educated nation ?

9.      If we accept that ‘up to certain level all students have to pass through a common curriculum and syllabus’ as a principle, which is now followed in lower Secondary level in general education system, then system of Madrasah education must be done way with and must assimilated in a single united secular-scientific minded-universal system of Education. This would be the truly single stream education system.

The same story is with English teaching. If the new system is forcibly introduced which needs each school is to provide proper facilities of science teaching, many schools have to be closed down. Let us elaborate those basic points.

 

1.      What do we mean by the term ‘Sarbajanin’ in Bangla ? We are all familiar with English word Universal. We used the word ‘Sarbajanin’ to express this meaning. Our cherished education system would be open to all irrespective of religion, colour, caste or sect, community, gender and socio-economic status- rich & poor. This universality is not confined within our state boundary- any citizen of any country can come to learn through our education system. The equality is not confined to social status only, our universal education system will offer scope of learning all branches of knowledge and disciplines of the world. All these concepts have been embodied in the word ‘Sarbajanin’(universal).

 

2.      What do we mean by the word Ôˆelg¨nxbÕ (non-discriminatory). By this word our intention to eliminate all sorts of discrimination and differences existing in our present education system has found expression. Discrimination and differences exist in different stages – firstly from time immemorial diverse, even oppose or contradictory to each other, system of education are in vogue in parallel to each other having no correlation with each other. As a result our educated class coming out through diverse systems are confined in one’s watertight compartments in their mind and mentality, in the quality of their achieved knowledge, in their thought and analytical capability and in their outlook attitude towards the society and the country. There practically exists no interrelation between one class of people with other as a result of which no interaction and intermingling take place among the people, which are very much essential for making a developed society. For this very reason, between a madrasah educated Moulana (Alem), an output of Madrasah education and a A-level passed smart young man, an output of so called English Medium based education a sky-like infinite distinction being constantly generated in their respective philosophy of life. There exists discrepancy in having facilities between male and female students, between village and urban based education facilities, between rich and poor etc. Our proposed education infrastructure would make no discrepancy between village and town, between upper and lower social positions, between religion and religion and among social cases. The education system that promises to make equal facilities available to a student living in a distance village or living in a city-bustee we may term a non-discriminatory. The system that holds and preserves the concept of universalism is the true non-discriminatory education system, which will make a student a true human being, just not an honest Hindu, an honest Muslim nor an honest Christian Bangladeshi.

3.      The word ‘Secularism’ is a very touchy and at the same time a hateful word to the present government which carries a meaning ‘devoid of religion’ to them. We emphatically say that secularism is such a social system whose fundamental basis is composed of such concepts as- humanism, pluralism and unity in diversity. As we want to see secularism is deeply rooted in our state-life, in a similar way we want secularism with its true spirit reflected in our education system. In other words it means there would be no such education system that is reserved for followers of a particular religion or class. There would be no significant role of religions in our curriculum, syllabuses or in composition of textbooks. Pupils belonging to all religions, all classes, all communities must have education through a single and unitary system of education through which the learners will develop the sense of humanism. This is what we mean ‘secularization of education’. 

4.      What we mean by ‘Science based education’? There is a misconception associated with this term. Many thinks that we speak of giving primarily of education on science subjects. This concept is however not true. In our education system the student when passed through it becomes a scientific minded, will possess mind of freethinking, liberalism and rationalism. Nay, the object of the proposed education system is not to produce only scientists, engineers, doctors or agriculturists. What we mean is that through our education system we want to make our learners analytical based on facts and proofs making them capable of solving a problem(s) as a scientist does, where superstitions or blind faith have no place. Through this process a free and liberal minded society would immerge. This is what we call scientific or science based education.

5.     Now what we mean by ‘Ekmukhi’ (GKgyLx)? The term may translated in English as one stream or unitary, not ‘mono-disciplinary’. The clear meaning of the term is that a learner acquires his education and thereby knowledge in different disciplines from primary to highest level of education a unitary form of education system. If we could establish such a unitary or one stream education in our life, then currently existing different streams of education giving systems such as ‘general education’, ‘madrasah-maktab-tol-chathuspathi’ types of educational infrastructures, and the so called ‘English medium education from kindergarten to O/A level’ will automatically get dissolved. At present in ‘general education system students’ receive education having same curriculum and syllabus from primary to lower secondary level (class I to class VIII). After lower secondary level, at Secondary level (class IX-X), the students are offered specialization in three broad disciplines namely (i) humanities, (ii) science and (iii) commerce. At higher secondary level (class XI – XII) this specialization is followed at an advance level. This stage is primarily a preparatory stage for entering into higher education in a more specified and distinct field.

There we chalked out a long-term programme of action. It was decided that movement must be carried out united, if possible from a common platform.
