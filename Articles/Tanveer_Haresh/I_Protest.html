I Protest

Tanveer Ibn Haresh

 

I have been a reader  and contributor of  mukto-mona.com for many years now. I personally interacted with avijit and jahid vai many times, don’t know if they still remember me. All this time I had this idea that mukto-mona is always progressive.Intellectuals have always been in support for democracy and specially for Bangladesh democrazy(crazy).I think more then a year ago I wrote an article in muktomona forum,stating that, people of Bangladesh are not yet civilized enough to deserve democracy. They love anarchy, they just love to create havoc. Many people protested. Well we have seen 15 years of  democrazy and have seen the last of it. I know most of the moderators here live in abroad. Brother Avijit left Singapore for USA.Jahid vai, you are also in usa for long time now.This person,farid hossain , who thinks Bangladesh army is same as Pakistani army(crack),I heard you also left Bd.I ask why? All of you people love democrazy so much, then why didn’t any of you choose to live in democratic Bangladesh? Why is it that, people feel 1000 times more secured in Bangladesh after it is no more democratic.Ask anyone, who lives in Bangladesh. I just came back from Europe yesterday, and in the airport the custom didn’t even check my luggage, cause two military persons were standing by, they knew they had no chance of harassing me and getting money from my pocket. All of us were so stunned by the behavior of customs. Yes we want this Bangladesh. We want Bangladesh to be always under caretaker govt. or army, until it becomes civilized. I am telling it again and I will tell it 1000 times, that the people of Bangladesh are still not civilized, to have to freedom to make his own decision. Can you remember how these same people, these same customs officers, same police officers treated you or me? Cause it was their fathers own property, oh my great democratic Bangladesh. Anybody could anything, just stand in the street and declare that I am from this xyz party and I want tomorrow to be hartal and any car you bring out in the street tomorrow, I will burn it. Its my democratic right. People who write from abroad about Bangladesh politics.Please stop writing.It is really easy for you people to cry for democrazy.I ask all of you to leave usa , Canada  and come back to bd and then write for democrazy. I think it has become a fashion now, to support democrazy. Man I live in Gulshan and my office is in uttara. During all those democratic hartal and oborodh, when I had to travel by local bus and my parents spent sleepess times, if I would ever return home in one piece. I don’t know how anyone still justify democrazy in Bangladesh. Yes democracy suits all those nations in Europe,where people has some brain matter in their head. Who knows burning a car, burning a shop, everything you do to hurt your countries economy, will hurt you one day.

 

But, I am specially astonished to see the petition here, for the freedom of the Universtiy teachers. I wonder, why most people prefer foreign universities and private universities in Bangladesh now. Because teachers like these, who are actually nothing but dirty political agents of different parties, spoil the lives of our students. Teachers like these are curse for our nation. People like farid hossain, who thinks that Bangladesh army is from mars and they love their country less than the great patriotic leaders of our country, never even consider once about writing something about these teachers. How clinically they are destroying the education backbone of a poor country. These people deserves to be hanged. Why on the earth, students need to be pursued into politics. Look at India and their young generation. Just banking on them, their merit and smartness, India is cruising today. And look at our universities, leave alone a proper standard of education, we can’t even provide them with a safe environment. These teachers, what do they want? Why would they provoke students for  rioting? How much money do they get from the politicians? Is that money more important than the future of our country? I don’t know, how can the citizens of a country become so selfish, that they can only worry about their own pocket  and their own children. Its known that their children doesn’t have to go to DU or CU, they have USA,Aus,Canada everything available. I guess not many soldirs and even officers of Bangladesh army has enough money to send their kids to these international universities.They must be more worried about the existence of pure education environment of these universities. People should grow up, out of their pak hanadar mentality. These are our own fathers, sons and brothers. You have seen for 15 years, where our country has gone. The number 1 thief  in the whole world. What worse can anyone do, what shame can even a general bring now? It would be really difficult to emulate tareq zia or hasina in corruption even if anyone wants to. So please stop crucifying the govt., only because it is supported by army. All of us who live in Bangladesh can feel the change. Like what I felt in zia airport yesterday. This small changes, small small changes can change our life, our attitude. I think many have visited western countries. Dealt with their officials, police.  Didn’t you think that , this is really a civilized country?  All the businessman , shop owners everyone is soooo angry at the govt. If you want to change a country, from the number 1 corrupted nation to something acceptable, we have to accept this phase.If we just let the flow of corruption continue, a day would have come, when there would have been no law and order or administration. Problem is that, we only like to think about the present. A little patience, a little unity, can move us forward as a nation. Not only the people gulshan,banani or dhanmondi. We can keep our head high, only if our whole nation moves forward. Just making money by any means, like we had been doing for the last 35 years, would never help the poor people, and the country would never progress, only the selected few will become zillionaires. We have to sense that and try to help this government. Give them a chance man, what can ssc failed illiterate politicians do better?
