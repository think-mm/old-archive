There is absolutely no contradiction as far as I am concerned. One cannot assign any mechanical meaning to Nirvana. It is the state of mind.  It is the highest, transcendent consciousness, referred to in the Bhagavad-Gita as brahman-nirvana (Brahm-Nirvana), in the Upanishads as turiya (4th State of Consciousness), in yoga as nirbija-samadhi (Samadhi without sprouting of thought or Samadhi with state of Emptiness), and in Vedanta as nirvikalpa-samadhi.

 

In Mahayana, nirvana is an emphasis on the unified nature of the world. Nirvana is conceived as a human experience of oneness with unconditioned consciousness (the absolute) which gives insight into the unity of the world (samsara), body, mind and soul. It is a state of transcending conditioned consciousness. It is also described as dwelling in the experience of the intense bliss in cognizing one's identity with unconditioned consciousness. It is freedom from attachment to the states of unhappiness, satisfaction and happiness

I have gone over Buddha’s life story many a time and I have never read anywhere that any of Buddha’s disciples committed suicide. The above statement apparently comes from some one who is biased and has no clear understanding of Nirvana.

 

In the West nirvana has often been misunderstood as mere annihilation; even in early Buddhism it was not so conceived. Nirvana literally means "The blowing out of a candle". The fire that goes out does not pass away, but merely becomes invisible by passing into a conscious experience of space (akasha); thus the term nirvana does not indicate annihilation but rather entry into another mode of existence and experience.

 

Epithets of nirvana in Buddhism include "the free," "the immortal," and "the unconditioned." Nirvana is attainable in life, and the death of one who has attained it is termed parinirvana. This has often been interpreted as annihilation, but in fact the Buddhist scriptures say that the state of the enlightened man beyond death cannot be described.

 

The term Pari-Nirvana means going beyond Nirvana (which is attained while living). This state itself has to be achieved by going through successive stages of meditation so that the consciousness becomes part of universal consciousness. If some one is calling this as suicide, apparently that person is stupid.
