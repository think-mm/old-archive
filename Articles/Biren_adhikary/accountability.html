RESPONSIBILITY AND ACCOUNTABILITY APPLICABLE FOR ALL

Birendra Nath Adhikary

 

In Bangladesh upon 1/11, there are two extreme heat waves, namely (1) Anti-corruption Drive and (2) Reform Processes, have been blowing on. Since two issues are complementary to each other, as such achieving any desirable result is impossible unless these heat waves continue in parallel. Out of few anti-corruption efforts, everyday newer & newer thrilling and horrifying information and fact-sheets have been coming out in one hand, and on the other side, various races & jumps, placing proposals and processes are ongoing in the event of reform. It is observed that what the commoners could knew and guess previously is a very little fraction of fact-sheets coming out now. The performance of the malpractice-doers in Bangladesh vanquishes the performance of any of the practitioner anywhere on the earth; even that could defeat the detective tales, or cinema & drama. A numerous hit feature films could be made in Hollywood and Bollywood with the life-story of our corrupted people.

 

The latest horrifying information comes out in the mass media is the money laundering by a London based company - the First Security Money Transfer Limited. It is known that the shareholders of this company are the closest friends of influential & top bosses in the immediate past 4-party alliance government of Bangladesh; even they are their business partners as well. The media further informs that the First Solution got a quick permission from Bangladesh Bank for monitory transaction from United Kingdom to Bangladesh with the help of their top-notch friends in Dhaka in one side, and on the other hand they got overnight business success upon utilizing the religious sentiment of Bangladeshi origin Muslims living in London. They were running fine with their business. However, what happened whatsoever the reason all on sudden the company evaporated few days back. While disappeared, they took away around 15 million Pounds (approx 30 million US dollars) that are hard-earned cash of the non-resident Bangladeshis (NRBs). The BBC Television flashed the news first following which different types of media at home and abroad came up with different coverages on the item. Reasonably, being head of the related organization the Governor of Bangladesh Bank became target of the media. At the time, he passed his various comments on the issue everyday. His comments depicted that he took the case lightly to some extend. It seemed his intension was like that the issue was not something bigger, there was nothing to be worried, he would all out try to recover the money of those NRBs who sent money to Bangladesh through First Solution, and that was possible upon verifying document of those banks through which the NRBs sent their money to etc. etc. Through his statement, it was expressed that the First Solution was accused and few banks were associated with the job done, however, he did not utter anything about the responsibility of the central bank of the country and involvement of the related officials there. As such, it would not be irrelevant to politely ask few questions to the Governor, such as

(1)     When did Bangladesh Bank give permission to the First Solution Money Transfer Limited for transaction?

(2)     In which process or under which policy/law, the permission was given to a company whose share-value worth only 100 Pounds?

(3)     Who were the officials involved at Bangladesh Bank on the way giving permission to the First Solution?

(4)     What are the measures that Bangladesh Bank has taken against the officials associate to the officials?

(5)     Why did Bangladesh Bank keep hidden the information of such huge irregularities and corruption of money laundering for quite a long period until it was flashed by BBC?

(6)     Could the Governor avoid the responsibility in this regard as the head of the central bank?

(7)     What does he think to do, if he could not avoid the responsibility?

 

Sooner the Governor would come up with answers to these questions through his activities and flash those in the media, quicker it would bring welfare to all. There is no scope for anyone to be beyond the measures of law under the continued drive of purification according to the commitment of the present government. It is extremely important that the head of the controlling organization of monitory transaction of the country would show the gesture of responsibility and accountability in specific where in terms of level the most hateful crime of committing irregularities in monitory transaction just falls just next to the crime of murdering people. Thus, it is expected that the Governor of Bangladesh Bank as the responsible person upon asking his own conscience would show his accountability to the nation, and accordingly decide and take steps that is the expectation of all from him at this moment.
