Purbo-Megh
Poem 25 through 28

Bangla translation by: Narendra Deb (done in 1930s)
English translation by: A.H. Jaffor Ullah and Joanna Kirkpatrick

Poem 25
              Will you visit Bidisha, my Cloud,
              The fabled capital of Dasharno?
              There you will see the fullness of the lustful life.
              Here Betrabati River courses along impatiently,
              Like a courtesan with one raised eyebrow.
              Drink of this river, slake your thirst.

Poem 26
            Full-blossomed Kodombo flowers perk up
            On seeing you descend on Bidisha.
            Take rest on Nichoi Mountain --
            Full of laughter and sweet-perfumed lust.
            See the pleasure-girls' substantial houses
            Built of stones: Oh, they tell
            Of the town youths' virile powers.

 


Poem 27
            Make Nichoi Mountain your temporary abode.
            Sprinkle cool raindrops on Juthika buds,
            Giving them fresh lustre. Make yourself known
            To the pretty maidens harvesting these flowers.
            Cast your shade on their tired faces, won't you?

Poem 28
            Though your way to Aloka is northward,
            You must visit Ujaayini, city of luxuries and
            Palatial arcades. The eyes of Ujjayinins flash
            Like lightening, enthralling you with their seductive gaze.
            Do respond to their love call --
            No reason to spoil the fun!
