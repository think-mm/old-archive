Purbo-Megh
Poem 9 through 12

Bangla translation by: Narendra Deb (done in 1930s)
English translation by: A.H. Jaffor Ullah and Joanna Kirkpatrick


Poem 9
            While a favorable wind steers your flight,
            As on your left you are flanked by mellifluous skylarks,
            With a column of storks assisting,
            You stare below to observe a Gorbhadhan ceremony --
            Auspicious moment for adolescent girls.

A column of storks assisting... (Poem 9)
(Artist: Purna Chakravarty)

Poem 10
            O my brother Cloud, please step nimbly!
            That wife of mine, your sister in law,
            Waits anxiously, counting the days 'til I return.
            A woman's heart is brittle -- delicate as a flower.
            Bereft of my loving touch, it may wither.
            Only a ray of hope keeps her love alive.
In Aloka, my beloved is counting the days til' I return (Poem 10)
(Artist: Purna Chakravarty)

 


Poem 11
            Your thunder provokes the Shilandra flower to bloom,
            And entices a bounteous harvest.
            Hearing your voice the swans,
            Eager to return to Lake Manosarovar,
            Pluck lotuses to pay their way with you.

Poem 12
            Flying over Ramgiri mountain,
            Embellished with holy Rama's footprint,
            Embrace it, for it touches you every year
            To unburden the heat reflected by tormented lives
            Below. Your cool embrace soothes the mountain.
            It's touch salutes you with love.
