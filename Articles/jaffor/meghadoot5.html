Purbo-Megh
Poem 13 through 16

Bangla translation by: Narendra Deb (done in 1930s)
English translation by: A.H. Jaffor Ullah and Joanna Kirkpatrick


Poem 13
                O Megh, listen to me a while
                As I impart directions to find my beloved:
                Take what you need to find her, slake your thirst
                Through every pore, but only what's necessary.
                If your tired feet must rest on the arduous trip,
                Repose on a mountaintop to regain your power.
                Plentiful crystal waters are there to quench your thirst --
                Then do hurry back to the task at hand.


Poem 14
                Is the mountain peak still all there?
                The enchanted Siddhi women gazed up to the peak in wonder
                As a sharp gale blew over their valley.
                Little did they know that the wind was your helmsman.
                Steer clear of the wild elephant herds as they rampage
                Over the wet Kunja grasslands --
                Lift off and be northbound.

 


A rainbow unfurling his myriad colors (Artist: Purna Chakravarty)
Poem 15
              A rainbow, unfurling his myriad colors against your
              Grey, termite mound-like body,
              Decorates you like Vishnu
              Radiant in peacock plumage,
              Shining in elegance.

Village women look up to you with soft, appreciating eyes
(Artist: Charu Roy)
Poem 16
              You, O Megh, are the patron of fertility in the fields.
              Thus, the hard-working village women look up to you
              With soft, appreciating eyes.
              You climb up above the sweet-smelling, freshly tilled plateau,
              Slow down again to rest -- then once again head north.
