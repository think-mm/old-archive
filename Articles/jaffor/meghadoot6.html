Purbo-Megh
Poem 17 through 20

Bangla translation by: Narendra Deb (done in 1930s)
English translation by: A.H. Jaffor Ullah and Joanna Kirkpatrick


Poem 17
              O Megh! You extinguished the raging forest fire
              With your heavy showers. Now Amrakuta peak
              Will provide you a couch to rest upon.
              A traveler is like Narayana, to whom
              No one however low would refuse to offer refuge.

Poem 18
              Amrakuta mountain looks enticing
              Its mango trees laden with ripe fruit,
              Warmly glowing like the garlands in a
              Beauty's long braids.
              As you climb its peak Amrakuta takes the likeness
              Of a breast, the dark nipple on top, pale below.
              Seems the forest is full of Mithunas,
              Cavorting endlessly -- how can you escape seeing them?

 


Poem 19
              Lightened of your burden of showers on Amrakuta,
              You have time to peek at gamboling forest nymphs
              In the mountain landscape.
              But please step smartly into the further distance,
              Where the scraggy stones of Bindhya Parvat appear,
              Holding at his foot the slender Reba River aimlessly meandering,
              Marking her mountain like creases on elephant's skin.

             

Poem 20
              O Megh, you drenched the land,
              Rendering the runoff acidic,
              But suffused with leafy fermentations
              And sweetened by dripping Jombu groves.
              Now Reba will accept your flood.
              Laden with heavy moisture, you resisted the wind.
              But after all, lightweight things lack substance:
              Fullness is all.
