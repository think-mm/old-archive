Purbo-Megh
Poem 33 through 36

Bangla translation by: Narendra Deb (done in 1930s)
English translation by: A.H. Jaffor Ullah and Joanna Kirkpatrick

 
            Poem 33

                    O Cloud, you must be fatigued tonight,
                    But see the beauties of Bishala, their hair drenched
                    With smoke of frankincense and wearing garlands.
                    The city mansions, arrayed with alpanas under scarlet feet
                    And dancing peacocks --
                    All beckon with festive allure.


            Poem 34
                      O Cloud! Visit the blessed land of Chandishwar,
                      Guru of the Three Worlds. His gardens emanate
                      The intoxicant perfume of blue lotus, as Gandhabati River
                      Exhales the scent of seductive ointments left behind
                      When girls played in her waters. Moving off,
                      They turn coquettish eyes on you, blue-necked like Shiva.

             


            Poem 35
                    O Megh, owner of the waters, stop a while by
                    Mahakal's temple at sunset, time for evening puja.
                    Trident-bearing Mahadev performs the ritual as,
                    With your swelling powers, you beat the mridangam.
                    Lord Shiva blesses you with a thunderous sound.

             

            Poem 36

Carefree dancing girls are gamboling in
Mahakal's temple. Don't they look fine,
Bedecked with jingling girdles and
Waving bejweled fly-whisks in your face?
Sprinkle with raindrops the fingernail scratches
On their bodies: They will flash honeybee eyes at you.
