Purbo-Megh
Poem 37 through 40

Bangla translation by: Narendra Deb (done in 1930s)
English translation by: A.H. Jaffor Ullah and Joanna Kirkpatrick

 

Poem 37
            As Pashupati Mahadev dances at sunset
            To the beat of kettledrums, you, O Megh,
            Assume the sun's red hibiscus hue.
            His arms, like giant forest limbs, are tough
            As the elephant's trunk. Shed your mist on them,
            Bhabani Devi watches you with peaceful gaze.

 


Poem 38
          Well past the gloaming in Bishala,
          Only a needle could thread that darkness
          As pretty girls hurry to their lovers' nests.
          They can barely see the path, O Megh.
          You must emit weak flickers of light but
          Be silent, and keep their delicate bodies dry.

Poem 39
            Your Shakti's eyes, like lightening flashes
            Always illumine your way, so rest them tonight.
            Find a spacious mansion with sleeping pigeons,
            And slumber on its roof until sunrise.
            Then, without delay, resume
            Your promised journey.

Poem 40
            Brother Cloud, do not block the Sun today --
            His warmth is needed by dejected wives
            Cheated and deserted by their beloveds.
            The men return to pacify a nighttime's
            Stormy weeping. Let the Sun return
            To dry their cold tears -- hide him not.
