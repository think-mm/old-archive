Bengali translation by: Narendra Dev (done in the 1930s)
English translation by: A.H. Jaffor Ullah assisted by Joanna Kirkpatrick
Purba-Megh
Poem number 5 through 8
Poem 5
                Jakkha muses: What is a cloud, after all,
                But a chemistry of light, air, and water?
                But what are my messages, after all, to be
                Silenced by the inanity of sending them by cloud?
                I am a passionate lover eager to reach my beloved.
                There's no difference between reality and fantasy
                To a man who yearns to see her.
Poem 6
                O Cloud! Your lineage is noble
                Yet you assume a myriad of forms.
                I, so far away from my beloved, beseech you.
                Anger will not strike me if you refuse,
                For it is better to be rejected by a noble soul
                Than pampered by a mongrel.
Poem 7
                O Cloud! You are the sanctuary of tormented souls.
                Won't you take these messages
                From the one separated from his beloved
                By the cruel order of rich and powerful Kubera?
                Would you visit Aloka, the abode of Jakkha's mighty lord?
                Descend on that garden illuminated by moonlight -- Shiva's crown.
                All those moonlit buildings wear a surreal look
                When drenched by moonlight.


Loving consorts toss back their locks to glimpse you... (Poem 8)


Poem 8
                Loving consorts toss back their locks
                To glimpse you as you pass by,
                Hoping that their sweethearts would soon return home.
                A man not indentured to service who, unlike me,
                Need not work for his living --
                Can he ever neglect his spouse as I now do?
