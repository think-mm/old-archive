Purbo-Megh
Poem 21 through 24

Bangla translation by: Narendra Deb (done in 1930s)
English translation by: A.H. Jaffor Ullah and Joanna Kirkpatrick

Poem 21
Pollen-laden, half-open Kodombo flowers,
Festively tinted light green and ash,
Share the woods with a herd of deer chewing
Champak blossoms already trampled to earth.
O Megh, the soil drenched by you exhales a fragrance
For the snuffling deer, who mark your path.

Poem 22
            Siddhas down below notice your skylark companions
            Sipping the raindrops as they fly and counting them.
            Speak, O Cloud!
            They await your thundering voice --
            An excuse to embrace their fearful consorts.

 


Poem 23
            My friend, a profusion of Kukuva flowers
            Decks the mountainside, their faint
            Perfume may beguile you to delay. Oh no!
            But soon you will hear the piercing cry
            Of sobbing peacocks,
            Moving you on your way.

Poem 24
            O Megh, when you reach Dosharno land,
            The Ketaki hedges will be in full bloom --
            While the gardens languish sere and grey.
            Hungry birds seeking household leftovers
            Will be eagerly building their nests, and
            Jombu fruits will ripen, shiny and black.
            The swans en route to Manasarovar
            Will linger there.
