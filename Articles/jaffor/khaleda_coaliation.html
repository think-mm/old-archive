Of grossly overweight politicians and Khaleda Zia

 A.H. Jaffor Ullah

The Daily Star of Dhaka had published a photo on February 4, 2005 that showed Khaleda Zia conducting a meeting with the leaders of four-party coalition.  One thing I noticed how grossly overweight the leaders are.  If you think I am making this us, then please look at the photograph.


 

Photo and caption from Daily Star (Feb. 4, 2005) - Prime Minister and BNP Chairperson Khaleda Zia hold a meeting with the top leaders of the four-party alliance at her office yesterday. PHOTO: PID

 

Especially, the Islamist coalition partners are corpulent as shown in the above photograph.  They say, “A picture is worth thousand words.” 

When the most common ordinary people of Bangladesh are malnourished and underweight, these corpulent politicians do not think for a moment that their over-sized physique speaks in volume the good life they have. 

In West, most politicians running for public offices try to be fit physically.  The look is very important.  But not so much in Bangladesh.  There, the fatter, the better.    

--------------------------------
Dr. A.H. Jaffor Ullah, a researcher and columnist, writes from New Orleans, USA
