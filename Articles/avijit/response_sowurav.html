Dear Dr. Sowurav,

Its a pleasure to read your encouraging comments about my book. I am an engineer by profession not a writer, never imagined that I would become an "author" (in Jahed's term) one day. I formed mukto-mona with some of my like-minded friends to stress the need to eradicate ignorance, superstition, belief in super- natural phenomena, illiteracy, social evils, and to promote rationalism, humanism, secularism and concrete measures to help those affected by natural and man-made calamities. I started writing Bangla articles just few years ago while Bornosoft came into market and fortunately internet Bengali community accepted my write-ups cordially ; I got some positive feedback. That inspired me a lot to formulate one of my series into book format, though my own publisher initially a bit skeptic (They said, "ei boi cholbe na!"). Upon receiving positive feedback from general readers each and every day, i am pretty convinced that they were wrong!  Apart than my nearest and dearest friends, the monumental work of Aroj Ali Matubbar and Prabir Ghosh inspired me a lot in my childhood. These lovable real "science teachers" literally asked me  not to accept anything without questioning. This is the key issue  that can inspire one to acquire knowledge and lead his life for Spirit of humanity. Yet we have to travel a long way.

The book you are reading is a result of real team work. All of my friends in Mukto-Mona gave valuable suggestions, many of my well-wishers voluntarily worked (and still working) for its successful marketing, and above all my father worked literally in day and night for publishing this book from Bangladesh.  Without their help the book would not come into light.
