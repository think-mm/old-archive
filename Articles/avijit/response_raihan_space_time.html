In classical physics, space is defined as a three-dimensional Euclidean space where any position can be described using three coordinates. However the idea of "space" has dramatically changed after Einstien's revolutionary paper on General Theory of relativity. Einstien's theory of relativity which agrees with a large number of experiments shows that time and space are inextricably interconnected. General relativity basically combines  the "time" dimension with three other dimension of Euclidean space to form what is called "spacetime". So it's useless to talk about space only as current Relativistic physics examines spacetime rather than space; spacetime is modeled as a four-dimensional manifold, and currently, there are theories (for example string theory) that can support even up to eleven-dimensional spaces.

Einstein's basic equation on relativity tells us how the space has been created and how it expands.
