Response : Natural selection is the process by which individual organisms with favorable traits are more likely to survive and reproduce than those with unfavorable traits. In each generation, only some individuals will produce offspring themselves, and of those that reproduce, some will leave more offspring than others. We can think of this as the "natural” process of selection of individuals to reproduce. Individuals with beneficial traits are more likely to be 'selected' than individuals with other traits. When those traits have a heritable component, they tend to become more common in the next generation. The mechanism of selection of individuals in a population does not "know" which traits are heritable; in this sense, the mechanisms of selection are "blind". 

There are many good examples of "blindness" of natural selection. One example is evolution of feather in early bird. Dylan Evans and Howard Selina in their basic book "Introducing Evolution"  (Icon books, UK) wrote :

"Natural selection has no foresight. When feathers first evolved, this was not in order to prepare the way for the evolution of wings. Feathers just happened to evolve for one reason, and it so happened that this provided the material for the evolution of wings."

There are lot other examples. One good example is Sickle cell anemia which is a nearly lethal condition in humans, responsible for about 100, 000 deaths a year. About 80% of individuals with this condition die before reproducing. Yet despite such apparently strong selection against the gene responsible for sickle cell hemoglobin, it is maintained at frequencies of 10% or even more in Africa. This is because "this disease" is proven to be more resistant to malaria than normal person (without Sickle cell anemia) there. In Africa malaria is very dominating, and thus Sickle cell anemia survives in those African  sates, because "blind natural selection" played a vital role.

Richard Dawkins wrote a nice basic book "Blind Watchmaker" on Natural selection and  there this is what he defines Natural selection :

"Natural selection, the unconscious, automatic, blind process that Darwin discovered, has no purpose in mind. If it can be said to play the role of watchmaker in nature, it is the blind watchmaker."
