Cry for Justice: Who is behind it?

Jahed Ahmed

Published on December 29, 2007

 
 
     Bibhuti Bhushan Chowdhury (60+) started his day in a usual fashion. At 10-30 morning on November 8th, 2005, he was going through the pages of a Bangla daily newspaper in the local Bazaar of Delduar Thana in Tangail district. Suddenly someone from the behind attacked him with a chopper. The assailant started hitting Bibhuti at random with the chopper. Fortunately, there were people around him who rushed to the scene, captured the attacker and saved Bibhuti before it was too late. Some of these rescuers were also wounded. Bibhuti now had a chance to look at his assailant. Amid awe and shock, he discovered- the culprit was Motu Mia, a man who had never been a stranger to Bibhiti Bhushan's home; indeed, one who often helped Bibhuti's family with household tasks in return for money, food and clothes. The incident was reported next day (November 9, 2005) in most national Bangla dailies: Ittefaq, Janakantha, Jugantar, Inquilab, etc). What, however, is not yet reported in any newspaper is the following:
 
      Although Police station is only a 10 minutes walk from the place of the incident, police came to the scene three and half hours later. What is even worse, they denied to accept the case initially, and it was after eight hours from the time when the incident occurred that police finally accepted the case. Not so unusual in Bangladesh! I, too,understand. But there is more to it. Police captured Motu Mia but but set him free at the same night of November 8th. Some people have the opinion that Motu Mia is a mentally abnormal living-from-hand-to-mouth person, and didn't have any ill motives behind the attack. This logic falls astray when we consider: why did local police deny to accept the case? Why did they release Motu Mia immediately after they captured him? If Motu Mia were really a mentally ill person, how come he didn't attack any one previously? Is it then possible- some influential group hired Motu or paid him to kill Bibhuti Bhushan Chowdhury and police was aware of it?
 
     We don't yet know any answers to the above questions. Nor does even Biswajit Chowdhury- Bibhuti Bhushan Chowdhury's eldest son living in the New York city- know any. I know Biswajit Chowdhury for quite some time. A former graduate of Dhaka Charukola (fine arts) Institute and now a student of Master's in Fine Arts, Biswajit is thoroughly a patriotic, non-communal and peace loving person from Bangladesh. He lives here with his brother, wife, one son and one daughter. For long, I didn't see him as tense, worried, as I saw after this "unbelievable" (in his words) incident happened to his father who has always been respected as a gentleman by Hindus and Muslims alike in his area. "Does your father have any prior history of animosity with Motu Mia, or any one else in your area?" I asked Biswajit. "No and never, as far as I know," he replied. "The main reason I'm concerned about is A few local people are trying hard to turn the whole incident to 180 degree angle and make it a communal issue." "There are reasons to fear since we have a few hundred Hindu families living over there," said Biswajit with a gloomy face. I could saw him calling his home back in Bangladesh and finding out latest about the situation. "What about administration's role?" I asked Biswajit. "The administration at district and higher level has been responsive but what I need most is the local Thana to act." "All I want is to know- if there was someone else behind the attack and if so, police must take action- disclose the real culprit's identity before the public," Biswajit stated.
 
     I've something to say to the present government and its administrations although Biswajit has requested me not to add any politics or religion to this news. Present government and its administrations always emphasize- we must project our country's image in a positive way before the international community. There is nothing wrong in this belief so long as one acts according to his/her beliefs. As he's been in the past, even now after all this happened, Biswajit Chowdhury is very careful not to do anything which would tarnish the image of the people back home. Is it too much to expect that the present administration  would ensure- Biswajit Chowdhury is not deprived of the justice and the real culprit and the motives of the attack would be unmasked ?
 
New York
11/20/2005
