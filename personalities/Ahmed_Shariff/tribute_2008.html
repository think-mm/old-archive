''আমাদের প্রথম পরিচয়- আমরা মানুষ, আমাদের শেষ পরিচয়- আমরা মানুষ, আমাদের লক্ষ্য হিন্দু-বৌদ্ধ-খ্রীষ্টান-মুসলমান থাকা নয়- মানুষ হওয়া।'
-আহমদ শরীফ [অপ্রকাশিত ডায়েরী, সাপ্তাহিক ২০০০; ঈদ সংখ্যা ২০০৭]

 Our first identity is- we are human beings, our ultimate identity is- we are human beings. Our aim should not be to live as Hindu-Buddhist-Christian-Muslim, but to become humans.

-Ahmed Sharif ['Oprokashito Diary,' Weekly 2000; Eid issue, 2007]   

Editor's Note

"Life is a compromise--Sofa doesn't subscribe to this theory. If we had a few more outspoken, candid persons as Sofa in today's Bangladesh, then the foundation of nobility would have been easier." This comment about the author-critic-poet late Ahmed Sofa was made by renowned Bangalee secular thinker & intellectual late Prof. Ahmed Shariff back in 1972--roughly thirty years before Sofa's death during which time the socio-political scenario of Bangladesh underwent many dramatic changes. Like everything else these changes affected the country's intelligentsia with a majority of them having directly or indirectly supported ongoing politics and in many cases served as the puppets of the ruling class. Hardly did the nation witness voices that spoke for the country and peoples interests with courage and integrity. Ahmed Sofa, much like a shining successor of his enlightened teachers, Prof. Abdur Razzak and Prof. Ahmed Shariff, was one among only a handful of intellectuals and thinkers who always put the nation's interests above everything else. While many of his brilliant associates and colleagues submitted themselves to the privileges and opportunities of the time, Ahmed Sofa felt content by remaining merely an isolated maverick. Yet it must be noted, his patriotism never took the turn of communalism or chauvinism. Indeed, Ahmed Sofa serves as a good example in that a person can have benevolence and a love for the mankind without betraying the allegiance and trust of his nation.

Like rest of us, Ahmed Sofa was also not infallible. Many among us might find some of his views and stances "controversial" or/and "questionable" (for example, his anti-Taslima Nasrin views). Yet one thing would always remain true about Ahmed Sofa: he was honest with his beliefs; rather than any social-political or religious authority, Sofa listened to the call of conscience to live his life. The temptation of accolade or praise could not weaken his beliefs. And this is exactly why Mukto-Mona feels privileged to pay tribute to Ahmed Sofa on his sixth death anniversary. If our small effort awakens even a handful of readers' interest in Ahmed Sofa, we will feel rewarded. In developing this page, many important references have been provided by Mr Nuruzzaman Manik--a freelance journalist, noted member and above all, a well-wisher of Mukto-Mona.

সম্পাদকের বক্তব্য 

 Life is a compromise তত্ত্বে ছফার আস্থা নেই। আজকের বাংলাদেশে এমনি পষ্ট ও অপ্রিয়ভাষী আরো কয়েকজন ছফা যদি আমরা পেতাম, তাহলে শ্রেয়সের পথ স্পষ্ট হয়ে উঠত। প্রয়াত লেখক-সমালোচক-কবি  আহমদ ছফা সম্পর্কে এ মন্তব্য করেছিলেন প্রখ্যাত বাংগালী সেক্যুলার চিন্তাবিদ ও বুদ্ধিজীবি ড: আহমদ শরীফ ঊনিশ' বাহাত্তর সালে, ছফার মৃত্যুর প্রায় তিরিশ বছর আগে। এ সময়ে বাংলাদেশের সামাজিক-রাজনৈতিক জগতে অনেক নাটকীয় পরিবর্তন ঘটেছে। অনেক  কিছুর মত এ সময় পরিবর্তনের হাওয়া দেশের বুদ্ধিজীবিদের গায়ে ও এসে লাগে, অনেকেই প্রত্যক্ষ বা পরোক্ষভাবে চলমান রাজনীতি এবং কোন কোন ক্ষেত্রে ক্ষমতাশীনদের দালালিতে জড়িয়ে পড়েন।

ব্যক্তি স্বার্থের ঊর্দ্ধে থেকে সাহস ও সততার সাথে জাতির কথা বলার মত লোক বাংলাদেশে খুব বেশী দেখা যায়নি। আহমদ ছফা, তাঁর আলোকিত শিক্ষক প্রফেসর আব্দুর রাজ্জাক এবং ড: আহমদ শরীফের সুযোগ্য উত্তরসূরী হিসেবে সেই সব মুষ্টিমেয় চিন্তাবিদদের একজন ছিলেন যিনি সারাজীবন জাতির স্বার্থকে ব্যক্তি স্বার্থের ঊর্দ্ধে স্থান দিয়েছেন। তাঁর মেধাবী সহকর্মী ও সহযোগীদের অনেকেই যখন সামাজিক-রাজনৈতিক বিভিন্ন সুযোগ-সুবিধা এবং বিলাসিতার সুযোগকে কাজে লাগানোর তালে ব্যস্ত থেকেছেন, আহমদ ছফা তখন কেবল একজন নি:সংগ বিদ্রোহী হিসেবেই থেকেছেন সন্তষ্ট। এখানে অবশ্যই মনে রাখতে হবে- ছফার দেশপ্রেমের মধ্যে কখনোই কোনরকম সাম্প্রদায়িকতা বা অন্ধ জাতীয়তাবাদের স্থান ছিল না।বরং এক অর্থে বলা যায়, নিজের দেশ ও মানুষকে ভালবাসার সাথে পুরো মানবজাতিকে ভালবাসার যে কোনরকম সংঘাত নেই- আহমদ ছফা এর একটি উজ্জ্বল উদাহরণ। 

 আমাদের সবার মত আহমদ ছফা ও ভুলত্ত্রুটি বা সমালোচনার ঊর্দ্ধে নন। অনেকেই তাঁর অনেক মতামত এবং বক্তব্যকে "বিতর্কিত" বা "প্রশ্নবোধক" হিসেবে আখ্যায়িত করতে পারেন, যেমন: তসলিমা নাসরিন বিষয়ে আহমদ ছফার কট্রর বিরোধিতা। কিন্তু একটি জায়গায় ছফার চরম সমালোচকরা ও সম্ভবত একমত হবেন: আহমদ ছফা নিজে যা বিশ্সাস করতেন তাই করতেন। কোন প্রকার সামাজিক-রাজনৈতিক বা ধর্মীয় কর্তৃত্বের কাছে নয়, আহমদ ছফা দায়বদ্ধ ছিলেন কেবল নিজের বিবেকের কাছে। কোন পুরষ্কার বা হাততালির লোভ কখনো বিচ্যুত করেনি তাঁর বিশ্সাসকে। আর এজন্যই আহমদ ছফার স্মৃতির প্রতি শ্রদ্ধান্জলি জানাতে পেরে মুক্তমনা বিশেষভাবে গর্বিত। আমাদের এই ক্ষুদ্র প্রচেষ্টার ফলে অন্তত কয়েকজন পাঠক-পাঠিকার মনে ও যদি আহমদ ছফা এবং তাঁর রচনা সম্পর্কে আগ্রহ জাগে, তাহলে আমরা সত্যি খুব আনন্দিত হব। আমাদের এই প্রচেষ্টায় বিভিন্ন রেফারেন্স দিয়ে বিশেষভাবে সহযোগিতা করেছেন মুক্তমনার অন্যতম সদস্য ও শুভার্থী সাংবাদিক নুরুজ্জামান মানিক। তাঁকে বিশেষভাবে ধন্যবাদ।

An Introduction to Ahmed Sharif

Ahmed Sharif (1921-99): Educationist, thinker, and renowned scholar of medieval bangla literature. Sharif was born on 13 February 1921 in the village of Suchakradandi in patiya upazila of Chittagong district. His father was a clerk at the Chittagong Government Collegiate School. Sharif was brought up under the care of his uncle, abdul karim Sahityavisharad, the well-known collector of puthi literature.

Ahmed Sharif was educated at Patiya High School, Chittagong College and Dhaka University from where he obtained his MA degree in Bangla in 1944. He began his professional life as a lecturer of Bangla at Nawab Faizunnesa College in laksham. For some time he worked as a program organizer at the Dhaka station of Radio Pakistan. On 18 December 1950 Sharif joined the Bangla Department of Dhaka University as a research assistant and later became a teacher in the department

He was awarded the PhD degree in 1967 from Dhaka University for his thesis on the Bangla medieval poet syed sultan. In 1972 he became a professor. He was at various times chairman of the Bangla Department, Dean of the Faculty of Arts, member of the Syndicate, and president of the Teachers' Association. After his retirement from Dhaka University after 33 years of service in June 1983, he was appointed Nazrul Professor at Chittagong University for two years (1984-86).

Ahmed Sharif's research work began with the puthis which his uncle, Abdul Karim Sahityavisarad, had donated to Dhaka University. He soon became an expert in Puthi literature. He edited 40 puthis. The first book published by the bangla academy was his edition of laily-majnu (1957), by the 16th century poet daulat uzir bahram khan. The first book published by the Bangla Department of Dhaka University was also a book edited by him, Puthi Parichiti (1958), containing brief descriptions of the 600 puthis donated by his uncle. The puthis edited by Sharif include alaol's Tohfa (1958) and Sikandarnama (1977), Muhammad Khan's Satya-Kali-Bibad-Sangbad (1959), Muslim Kavir Padasahitya (1961), Jayenuddin's rasulbijay (1964), Muzammil's Nitishastrabarta (1965), Madhyayuger Ragtalnama (1967), Banglar Sufisahitya (1969), Afzal Ali's Nasihatnama (1969), Baultattva (1973), and Syed Sultan's Nabibangsha O Rasulcharita (1978).

Sharif became renowned as a scholar of Bangla medieval literature. While he studied the valuable contributions made by the Bengali Muslims, in writing the literary history of this period Sharif reflected on the contributions of all Bengali writers irrespective of religion. Banali O Bangla Sahitya (in two volumes 1978, 1983), perhaps his finest work, is a comprehensive history of medieval Bangla literature and throws light on the society and culture of medieval Bengal and on the literature of different communities: Buddhist literature, vaisnava literature, baul literature, and sufi literature. Sharif was the exponent of the theory of the Dark Age of Bangla literature. He also greatly contributed to the study of love stories and religious books written by Muslims as well as puthis, written in two languages. Some of his other books on the literature of the Middle Ages are Syed Sultan: Tanr Granthabali O Tanr Yuga (1972), Madhyayuger Sahitye Samaj O Sangskrtir Rup (1977) and Madhyayuger Bangla Sahitya (1985).

Sharif was also a distinguished essayist and thinker. He started writing essays in the forties and continued to write until his death. His area of interest in writing was Bangladesh, Bengali society, and Bangla language and literature. He was concerned with the idea of Bangladeshi identity and wrote pertinently on the subject. Toward the end of his life, he used to write columns regularly in daily and weekly newspapers. His essays on Bangladesh society, literature, politics, religion, and philosophy have been published in 40 books, among them Bichita Chinta (1968), Sahitya O Sangskrti Chinta (1969), Svadesh Anvesa (1970), Jibane Samaje Sahitye (1970), Pratyay O Pratyasha (1971), Yug Yantrana (1974) Kaler Darpane Svadesh (1985), Banalir Chinta-Chetanar Bibartan Dhara (1987), Banlar Biplabi Patabhumi (1989), Bangladesher Sampratik Chalchitra (1990), Manabata O Ganamukti (1990), Banla, Banali O Banalitva (1992), Pragatir Badha O Pantha (1994), E Shatake Amader Jibandharar Ruparekha (1994), Svadesh Chinta (1997) Jijnasa O Anvesha (1997), Bish Shatake Banali (1998), Bishvasbad, Vijnanbad, and Yuktibad and Maulabad (2000).

Sharif was a rationalist and humanist who possessed strength of character and determination. He was a dedicated and fearless intellectual, who criticised any government that resorted to unjust and anti-people policies. He wrote and spoke strongly against fundamentalism, military rule, autocracy and the enemies of liberation. His writings were often controversial, inviting the anger of those he criticised. All his life Sharif was associated with associations and institutions committed to humanism, human rights and intellectual freedom. In March 1971 he swore the writers of Bangladesh at the central shaheed minar in dhaka to the fight against Pakistani autocracy. After liberation he was a central figure of many societies, among them the Committee for the Protection of Fundamental Rights and Legal Aid, bangladesh lekhak shibir, Bangladesh Bhasha Samity, Colonel Taher Sansad, Ganatantrik Sanskritik Front and Swadesh Chinta Sangha.

Despite the controversies that Sharif aroused, his scholarship was recognized by all. Among the awards he received were the Bangla Academy Literary Prize, Daud Literary Prize, National Ekushe Award. Rabindra Bharati University, Kolkata, awarded him an honorary DLitt.

Ahmed Sharif led an active and committed life. His dedication to education and research is also evident in his donating his body to the medical college students for research. His wishes were carried out after his death on 24 February 1999.

[by Ahmad Kabir, BANGLAPEDIA]
