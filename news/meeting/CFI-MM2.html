Update: CFI-MM 2nd Monthly Humanists’ Group Discussion

 

 

Last 15th May/Sat day 2nd   Monthly Humanists’ Group Discussion took place at the Center for Inquiry metro New York ( www.cfimetrony.org ), located at Federal Plaza, Manhattan, NYC. The event was sponsored by CFI metro NY and was arranged by Mukto-Mona ( www.mukto-mona.com ).  The topic of discussion was “The Rise of Religious Fundamentalism in South Asia with Special Focus on Bangladesh: Possible Roles the Expatriate Community Could Play”.

 

Although some twenty selected secularists/freethinkers of Bangladesh and Indian origin living in NYC were invited, not all could make it to the meetings for unexpected personal problems. The attendees present were:

 

1.      Mr. Belal  Beg, Former Producer, BD Television, a writer and an esteemed secular humanist.

2.      Mrs. Preethi Sen Gupta, writer and a devoted humanist

3.      Mr. Hasan Ferdaus, a progressive Writer for leading national Daily Prothom-Alo, BD. In NYC he works for UNO.

4.      Ms Farida Majid, Progressive Free-lance Writer

5.      Mr. Hassan Al Abdullah, Poet and editor, NY-based leading bilingual poetry magazine “Shabdaguchha”.

6.      Mr. Biswarup D. Gupta, a Microbiologist and a Progressive thinker

7.      Mr. N. Alom, a devoted secularist

8.      Mrs. Alom

9.      Mrs. Amina Kamal Chowdhury

10. Tahmid Kamal, a fourth grader living in NYC

11. Tahneem Kamal, a 1st grader in NYC

12.  Jahed Ahmed

 

 

The meeting was delayed when decided to wait for some more of the invitees who were expected on a confirmed basis to attend. Three such invitees were Kowshik Ahmed, editor to weekly Banglai, Dr. Mina Farah and Mrs Lutfun Nahar Lata. Unfortunately, they couldn’t show up to the end due to their own problems. Besides, Minaxi Dixit & Jyoti da couldn’t make it due to physical sickness of former although Jyoti da requested a copy of update, probably to publish in their magazine. Syed Muhhamullah, who attended first meeting couldn’t make it this time because of pre-committed involvement with National Drama Festival which started same day. Dr. Jyoti P. Datta couldn’t make it due to some personal problems.

 

Timing:  The meeting was scheduled to start at 12.00 noon. All the attendees had arrived prior to 12.00 noon as per request. While Dr. Austin was receiving the already appeared guests at downstairs warmly and escorted them to their office, poet Hassan Abdullah & I decided to wait few more minutes and remained outside. At 12-15 noon we went back inside and joined the group. Dr. Austin decided to go back downstairs to see if Dr. Mina Saha Fara has arrived. Meantime, we started up an informal conversation before the cup of coffees and free issues of FREE INQUIRY and SKEPTICAL INQUIRER. The topic was Dr. Humayun Azad’s  ”Pak….Bad Sad”.  On being asked, Hasan Ferdaus said, he “didn’t like it at all” which led to a friendly but heated debate with prominent Humyun Azad fan poet Hassan  Al Abdullah. I was favoring the latter. Mr. Belal Beg also joined us. Meantime, Dr. Austin came back, probably a bit disappointed for not having seen Dr. Mina. Hearing our issue of the debate he reminded us not to lose track of today’s specific discussion. So from a book club like discussion, we went back to main topic viz. Rise of Religious Fundamentalism in South Asia with Focus on Bangladesh.

 

Who said what?   

 

After a brief introduction about CFI metro NY (www.cfimetrony.org), its parent organization Council for Secular Humanism (www.secularhumanism.org) and its founder Prof. Paul Kurtz, CSH/CFI publications, missions (in a nutshell: science, reason and freedom of inquiry) by Dr. Austin Dacey; I explained the reason of gathering and  some of Mukto-Mona’s own projects such as celebration of last 1st March as a Rationalist Day, Mukto-Mona Prokashani with the aim of publishing Bangla books in Bangladesh, West Bengal, possibility of a publishing a English secular magazine for new generations of Bangladeshi/Bengali-Americans. I solicited feedbacks from attendees. It started with Miss Farida Majid.

 

Ms Farida Majid (FM): “Religious fundamentalism is a modern phenomenon”, was the starting sentence of FM. I raised immediate objection “what about burning of de Brono, Witch Hunt, and Torture of Galeleo?”.   Belal Beg Bhai came forward interpreting her statement meant for “strategy of fundamentalists”.  Then she delivered an elaborate speech with some references and distributed (Dr. Austin made copies) copies of her recently published article (in multiple BD English newspapers) entitled “Fashioning Lies, Veiling The Truth” whose summary, if I understood it right , is as follows:

 

 Quran should NOT be blamed for wearing of hijab by Muslim women. No where, according to her, Quran clearly says about hijab. It was “illiterate” mullah Moudoudi (I quipped, “was Mohammed himself literate”?), a darn follower of Wahhabism (she narrated the historical background of Wahhabism as well) who misinterpreted Quranic verses and convinced Muslims- wearing hijab is mandatory.

   Interestingly, when I asked, if she was aware about such Koranic verses where it says, to quote a few (at that time I couldn’t mention particular #s but only statements) :

a) “Men are in charge of woman, because Allah has made the one of them to excel the other….” (4:34) b) “A male shall inherit twice as much as a female” (4:11) c) “Women are your fields: go, then, into your fields from whichever side you please”. (2:223); d) "NOT to make friendship with Jews and Christians” (Q. 5:51) e) “Make war on them until idolatry shall cease and God’s religion reign supreme”. (8:39) f) ”It is He who has sent His apostle with guidance and the Faith of Truth, so that He may exalt it above all religions, much as the pagans may dislike it”.  (61:9)  

  FM denied despite her claim of having possessesd all the available versions of Quran and her acquaintance with Arabic language.“Well, Quran was written NOT in today’s modern Arabic language. It was, rather, written in a dialect belonging to Mohammaed’s own tribe Quraish”, I interrupted. I stopped when she reacted while asking “are you an expert?” 

“Common public are not fundamentalists. Politicians and intellectuals often fail to realize that”, a point she made repeatedly during her speech. As for solution to the rise of Islamic fundamentalism in Bangladesh, she stressed on restoration of 1972 constitution, which was based on, amongst other things, secularism, democracy, socialism, and ban on use of religion as a political tool. Some felt skeptics and asked her “how restoration could be done?” FM replied in affirmative and said that it is very much possible through pursuit of legal affairs since Gen. Ziaur Rahman came from military and amended constitution to his advantage illegally. However, a mass-level movement both at home and abroad about it is necessary, as pointed out by FM.

 Mr. Hasan  Ferdaus:   He didn’t want to speak up initially except for listening patiently. Upon request he made some points. Initially the way he understood FM, he said was:“ there is good Islam versus there is bad Islam. Am I right in getting your point?” Farida Majid said, “wrong! I never said so!” For a while the debate was mainly between FM and Hasan Ferdaus until Dr. Austin added up few more words to Hasan Ferdaus’  points. In addition to that,  Hasan Ferdaus made two important points- failure of mainstream political party is one of the major causes of rise of fundamentalism. As for strategy to deal with Islamic reactionary forces: we should never start with agenda to wipe out religion since among poor mass religion is sometimes only hope and initiating any mission to uproot religion would be counter-productive.

 Mr. Belal Beg: Besides supporting some of Hasan Ferdaus’  stance, Beg Bhai  pin pointed to goal-oriented agenda  of Wahaabism from abroad as a source toward  rise of Islamic fundamentalism. He explained- how day by day once a Sufism dominant and peace loving land of Bangladesh is turning toward mini Afghanistan. 

 Mr. Hassan Al Abdullah: He seemed to have only one mission- how to find a sponsor to bring Prof. Dr.  Humayun Azad to USA. I supported him and previously approached  Paul Kurtz about same. Hassan Ferdaus, Ms Farida Majid and Belal Beg Bhai all elaborately described Dr. Azad to Austin. Dr. Austin and Prof. Paul Kurtz have agreed to sponsor the much acclaimed and recently assaulted (now under recovery) writer sometime by the end of this year or beginning of next year. 

     Rest of the attendees were simply patient listeners except at the end when Mr. Biswarup Das Gupta, sitting in a corner a bit far from the main round table, was asked about revealing his opinions on the matter. “I think we have somewhat deviated from our initially declared agenda viz. what the expatriate community could do about it.” Same time, Mr. Gupta, a long time friend of mine, asked to include Hindu fundamentalism on the agenda as well.

      The session was over at about 1-30 pm. While every one saying ‘bye’ to Dr. Austin, I noticed- Belal Beg Bhai was whispering something to Dr. Austin. The word that immediately reached my ear was “fatemolla”. Yes, he was describing to Austin, our dearest fatemolla’s courageous fight against Jamayat and Sharia law. I am glad to tell you- Dr. Austin is already familiar with the name. “Do you have any contacts with Kamran Mirza, Jamal Hasan, fatemolla”, asked Paul Kurtz, himself, other day during fund raising dinner in Long Island. He also enquired about Dr. Mina  Saha Fara.

 

   Date and timing for 3rd meeting was proposed to be decided on later. I also asked, if any body else was interested in being moderator/convener. None said anything except poet Hassan Abdullah “you are doing it. Why don’t you carry it on?”  Prior to meetings, when I requested Dr. Austin to moderate the group, he said, “I cannot act as moderator of any particular group.”   

In between English discussions, there were Bangla chats. Dr. Austin’s face at that time was something worth looking at!

Once again, on behalf of Mukto-Mona, I would like to extend my sincere thanks to Dr. Austin Dacey of CFI metro NY, Prof. Paul Kurtz and all CFI staff and the attendees who made it to the meetings while compromising other priorities during weekend.

Regards,

Jahed Ahmed

Convener & Moderator

CFI-MM Humanists’ Discussion Group for People of South Asian origin
