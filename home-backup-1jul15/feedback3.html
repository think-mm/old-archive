Dear Moderators of the Forum:

It gives me a great pleasure to be a member of such a distinguished forum and thanks for asking our opinion in search of making this forum a better one. "Change" as you know, is the only constant factor in any organization. One has to make efforts to constantly improve on things in order to meet and exceed the expectations of the readers. I am not very sure as to what the organizational goals of MM are but can assess from the name itself as to what the objective should be. Based on my own personal assessment, I would like to suggest the following:

That the articles should be bound in a thread that are pre-determined with a purpose contained therein. Based on the organizational objectives, MM should predefine those threads with an intended outcome in mind. The outcome should be disclosed to the readers in advance. At the end of the session, there will be a summary of the write-ups with all the pros and the cons discussed in the thread and the results of the outcome will have been announced. For motivational purposes, the moderators will pick the most prolific writer on the subject and post his/her writing on the website as an archived item.

The moderators of the forum should not in any way take side of the subject as they are supposed to remain neutral being the judge of the subject matter. It is often seen the moderators taking sides in the write-up and are posting serious comments on the posting. This makes the forum look biased.

There should be some modes and methods of cross checks in evaluating the merit of a particular write-up, as the individuals may wrong some writers based on their personal choices. In doing so, there should be outside members with distinguished credentials to be called on random checks on the ability of the individual moderators.

Currently, there are too many posts and multiple posts from the same writer on the same day. There should be some control over the number of issues being discussed at any given point and time and number of posts from the same individual in a given day. Most professionals would not be able to digest more than 5 postings in a day given their time constraints and reading multiple forum articles at the same time.

Issues under discussion, need to be predefined to contain some of the value less writeups that often crowds the inbox everyday. While MM is one of the better forums in terms of the quality of the writeups, there still are some writeups that do not carry any value to the obejctives of the forum. I would urge MM to rectify such insertions that somehow undermines the very spirit of the forum.

In conclusion, I wish MM the best in their endeavors in making this a better forum for all irrespective of the personal beliefs of the individuals and their orientations.

Kind regards,

Mohammed Rial Ahasn
Currently in Cairo/Egypt
